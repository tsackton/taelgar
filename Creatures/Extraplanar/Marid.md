# Marid

Marids are the name for a diverse group of powerful water genies, among the most famous native inhabitants of the [[Elemental Plane of Water]]. 

Much scholarship depicts marids as solitary creatures, often crafting their own unique watery realms across the vastness of the [[Elemental Plane of Water]]. Depictions and stories of strange watery palaces, constructed from living coral and bioluminescent algae, shimmering ice, rock, or stranger materials point to the vast diversity of marid tastes. Yet, some scholars this picture and hint that organized marid societies must exist somewhere. 

Unlike some of their elemental brethren, marids rarely interact willingly with mortals, and thus tales of their lives on the [[Elemental Plane of Water]] are few and far between. Most stories of marid interactions with mortals depict them as either [[The Ciphered Scroll|unwillingly bound to serve mortals through magic]], or as [[Elemental Plane of Water|binding mortals into their service]].

## Marids In Play

Two marids have appeared or been mentioned by name in play, both in [[Mawar Adventures]]. 

### Nimessa

[[Nimessa]] is the only named marid in the vault. She is a female marid of the [[Elemental Plane of Water]], treated on her page as an elemental with marid as subspecies.

In DR 1747, Azar summoned and controlled Nimessa using old magic of Airion's. The ring forced her to steal a fragment of living water from the Sentient Ocean and bring it to Hamri. She also bound or summoned a creature from her home plane to protect the stolen water.

The ring's exact mechanism is described inconsistently. A secret comment in [[The Ciphered Scroll]] says Airion's treasure included "a ring containing a trapped marid." Later item notes say the ring could bind a genie and command one service, and that Azar used it to compel Nimessa. The core fact is that Airion's ring gave Azar one compelled service from Nimessa; whether she was already trapped in the ring or summoned and bound by it remains worth review.

Nimessa told Azar he needed to go to Hamri because of the distance between the planes or local planar geometry. [[Gazankoa]] seems to have provided Azar's hard-to-see phantom boat after a sacrifice, allowing him to reach Hamri secretly.

After fulfilling Azar's command, Nimessa faded from the world, apparently returning to her native plane. She was furious at being forced into the theft. Azar believed the ring prevented her from harming him for 1001 days after the command. Nimessa's own note calculates her revenge window from July 1747 to DR 1750-04-20.

## Guzo's Shrine Marid

In DR 1749, a second marid appears in the Mawar material. This marid is unnamed.

[[Mawar Adventures Episode 05]] and [[Shrine to Guzo the Mariner]] say a marid seeking a [[Crystallized Song Fragment|crystal fragment]] connected to the Sentient Ocean broke into [[Shrine to Guzo the Mariner|Guzo's Shrine]] in the [[North Cliffs]]. It forced its way through a recently blasted underwater breach, seized the crystal, and triggered the shrine's blue divine protection, described elsewhere as a hallow effect. The protection trapped it in the hidden chamber, blocking teleportation and extraplanar escape.

Its servants were water elemental or watery frog-like creatures. They spoke Primordial, warned intruders away, and had orders not to leave without the crystal. The servants invoked Gazankoa, and the trapped marid offered to return to the Plane of Water and let the party present the crystal to Gazankoa. Its threats made clear that it would keep hunting the shard and anyone carrying it.

The party refused the bargain. In the fight, the marid used a crushing jet of water, turned to mist, passed through a wall, killed one fleeing servant for cowardice, and was eventually destroyed by [[Ryu]]'s marked trident strike after the shrine's blue light and [[Nerissa]]'s magic weakened it. The party then recovered the crystal and gave it to [[Rayna]].

## Contradictions and Open Questions

- The public Plane of Water note presents marids as solitary realm-makers, while broader creature lore says genies build great cities. This may be generic genie lore, older lore, or a true tension.
- The origin question is unresolved: marids may be species, personifications of water, echoes of ancient powers, or descendants/creations of elder elementals.
- The relationship between marids and Gazankoa is inconsistent across source types. Public cosmology has marids whisper Gazankoa's name with caution; worldbuilding chat imagines domain-ruling elementals owing allegiance to elemental powers such as Gazankoa; Mawar Episode 05 shows at least one marid or water-plane faction cooperating with Gazankoa.
- Marids have hunted the Sentient Ocean for generations, but Nimessa belonged to a faction that wanted to leave it in peace. Hostility to the Sentient Ocean should be factional, not universal.
- The exact mechanics of Airion's ring need review: trapped marid in the ring, summoned marid, bound genie, and one commanded service are all present phrasings.
- The status of Nimessa after DR 1750-04-20 is unknown. The revenge hook exists in secret notes but has not been resolved in public campaign material found in this search.
