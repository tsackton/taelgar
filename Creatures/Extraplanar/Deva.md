# Deva

Devas are a kind of [[Celestials|celestial]], rarely encountered but generally understood as angelic messengers and agents of divine or spiritual powers.

A deva's appearance and bearing often reflects the power it serves. While many accounts from [[Mos Numena]] sources describe devas in the familiar language of shining humanoid angels, other accounts suggest stranger forms. 

[[Oskar in Tharn Todor#Episode 2 The Celestial Meteor|Stories occasionally connect devas with falling stars or celestial meteors]], suggesting that at least some devas reach the [[Material Plane]] through violent or imperfect passages between realms, though few understand the metaphysical details of such violent passage.

## DM notes

The basic idea: deva is best treated as a rank, office, or mode of manifestation of the powers that rule spiritual or divine realms. Typically devas are probably the most common manifestation of divine powers; they are a recognizable forms that a celestial manifestation can take, especially when the being has a defined mission rather than a permanent role in the world.

- In D&D terms, devas are messenger-angels below planetars and solars
- In Taelgar terms, a deva is a stable Material Plane body assumed by a [[Soulstuff|soulstuff]] being from a [[Spiritual Realms|Spiritual Realm]] or [[Divine Realms|Divine Realm]].

An open question is how they cross the [[Land of the Dead|Divine Veil]]. The most likely typical explanation is prayer, which opens a portal/gate of some kind. 

The wounded deva in [[Oskar in Tharn Todor]] and [[Ep 2 Crashed Palace]] was associated with a strange meteor in the [[Yuvanti Mountains]], warned [[Ken-to]] of a ritual to summon a servant of an ancient evil, was attacked by fiends, and is identified in DM prep as Mountain's Heart, a servant of the primordial divine of nature that rules [[Arborea]]. This is a possible mechanism for devas to cross the Divine Veil unwillingly or otherwise disconnected from prayer, using the meteor as a metaphor/image/consequence. 

To generalize, a normal deva manifestation might arrive through prayer, summoning, or a particular holy site, but a meteor could be what happens when a spiritual-realm being is forced through the [[Land of the Dead|Divine Veil]] without a proper gate. Its soulstuff condenses, burns, and strikes the world as star-like matter, leaving the deva wounded or unstable rather than simply manifesting cleanly or returning home.
