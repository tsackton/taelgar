## Current Position

As of the last recorded event in [[Great Library Session Notes - Arc 5]], the [[Silver Tempests]] have retreated to [[Castrella]] after fighting through the [[~Drankorian Cleaver-Stone Fort~]], the Yo'nari ruins, and the [[Cleaver-Stone]] chamber. They recovered from the curse of eternal waking with the help of a priest of [[Mos Numena]], took rooms at [[The Laughing Gull]], and are deciding how to enter [[Cairn Dor]] to search for [[Samso]]'s people.

The immediate unresolved goal is to enter [[Cairn Dor]] and find or free the missing lizardfolk and other "dreamers" connected to the [[Shemra Azem]]. The party has already shut down the soul compass and locked the [[Cleaver-Stone]] portal from the Material Plane side, apparently halting the current round of raids for now. They have not resolved what happened to [[Urkabi]], [[Iara]]'s people, or the other vanished lizardfolk villages.

## Established Campaign Events

### Samso and Urkabi

- [[Samso]] was born in [[Urkabi]], a small lizardfolk village on the [[Zurrua]] in [[Apporia]].
- In DR 1737, [[Samso]] returned from days away to find [[Urkabi]] gone. The village was empty, black rot clung to the trees and polluted the streams, and a shimmering green sigil faded on the ground.
- Samso spent years healing the local ecosystem and listening for any trace of his ancestors, but found no answers.
- He eventually left home, traveled through the [[~Yeraad Lizardfolk Swamp~]], and joined the [[Great Library]] expedition because its scholars seemed his best chance to learn what happened.

### Blue and Lyra's Letter

- [[Blue]] found a dead messenger carrying [[Letter from Chardon for Samso on the Umbral Covenant]] and brought it to [[Samso]] in [[Voltara]] in DR 1748-09.
- The letter reports that the green sigil resembles a spell glyph but can be deconstructed into three ancient Northros runes meaning "Sleep Nevermore."
- The letter connects the sigil to research on the [[Umbral Covenant]], the [[War of the Dark Rift]], earlier Apporian wars, the [[Shemra Azem]], and the Wolf-Queen or Queen of Eternal Waking.
- Blue's own secret backstory connects her skull-crystal to fear, anger, danger, and memories or impressions of the [[Umbral Covenant]].

### Castrella Investigation

- The party traveled from [[Urkabi]] down the [[Zurrua]] and then by wind walk to [[Castrella]], a port city at the mouth of the [[Kedron]] in [[Cedrano]].
- In Castrella, they gathered folk legends and local rumors at places including [[The Sober Seal]], [[The Drunken Dolphin]], [[The Whale's Spout]], and [[The Laughing Gull]].

- Official histories disagree, treating the [[Umbral Covenant]] of the War of the Dark Rift as different from the enemies of earlier wars.

### The Fort, the Yo'nari Ruins, and the Cleaver-Stone

- Following clues upriver, the party found a Drankorian milestone pointing toward the Cairn Dor guard post and reached a ravine with the [[~Drankorian Cleaver-Stone Fort~]].
- The fort was guarded by shadowy defenders, including a shadow knight. The party defeated them.
- The Drankorian ruins contained administrative records with instructions for locking the portal and closing or shutting down the soul compass.
- Deeper in the ravine, a sealed door bore an incantation to [[Yeshara]] and the sleepless vigil. Passing through cursed the party so they could not sleep.
- Beyond the door were the remains of an ancient village with wolf-headed plinths in two parallel lines leading to a longhouse.
- Two [[Shemra Azem]] soldiers emerged and spoke of taking [[Mabist]] to [[Cairn Dor]] to join the dreamers.
- After the fight, [[Mabist]] and [[Alton]] could not pass back through the sealed door. [[Adrik]] dragging Mabist through caused psychic harm and erased Mabist's knowledge of the guidance cantrip.
- During a rest, Mabist saw a vision of a young girl speaking as if Mabist were [[Ilanar]], saying Ilanar would not wake and it frightened [[Yeshara]], her mother.
- Murals showed Yeshara leading her people through and under a trilith stone as part of a rite.
- In the Cleaver-Stone chamber, the party saw a trilith stone, several plinths with obsidian and bronze mechanisms, and a greenish sigil matching the symbol from Samso's village.
- The Shemra Azem guarding the chamber threatened to drag party members through the portal to become dreamers and help the [[Yo'nari]].
- Mabist was knocked unconscious and dragged through the portal; Adrik followed and rescued them.
- Samso, in earth elemental form, killed Yeshara's lieutenant or commander.
- After the victory, the party used the Drankorian instructions to lock the portal and shut down the soul compass. They retreated rather than entering Cairn Dor immediately.

## Working Background From Current DM Notes

The following background comes mainly from [[GL - Session 62 - DM Notes]] and appears to be the current intended explanation. Some pieces were confirmed in play, but many details remain DM-facing draft material.

### Yeshara and the Yo'nari

- Thousands of years ago, before the [[Downfall Wars]], the [[Yo'nari]] were a proto-[[Deno'qai]] people living in the interior mountains of [[Apporia]].
- They interacted with the fey and called the Feywild Nazil-Dimu.
- Their queen was [[Yeshara]]. After her brother [[Ilanar]] vanished following a bargain with a fey trickster and was found in a catatonic dream-torpor, Yeshara became convinced that dreams and sleep were dangerous.
- Yeshara's fear hardened into a cult of wakefulness. The DM notes describe outlawed sleep rites, vigil-fasts, punishment for dream-taint, and a final Mid-winter Rite.
- Yeshara built the [[Cleaver-Stone]], a bronze-and-obsidian trilith with wolf imagery, for the rite.
- The rite was meant to sever Yeshara's people from the Plane of Consciousness so they could not sleep or dream.
- Instead, the rite created or opened [[Cairn Dor]], transported Yeshara and her followers there, and turned the Cleaver-Stone into a portal between realms.

%% Review: The personal tragedy in [[GL - Session 62 - DM Notes]] uses the name "Sarzel" once without defining that person. It may be a typo, a spouse, or an abandoned name. Do not rely on Sarzel until clarified. %%

### Cairn Dor and the Sleepless

- [[Cairn Dor]] is now described in the vault as a shadowed realm in the [[Shadowfolds]], connected to the Material Plane through the [[Cleaver-Stone]].
- Cairn Dor is associated with [[Yeshara]], the [[Shemra Azem]], and soul-plinths used to draw dreamers from the Material Plane.
- [[GL - Session 62 - DM Notes]] divides Cairn Dor's inhabitants into two groups:
  - Nurim-Dor, the "People of Eternal Waking," or citizens freed from dreams.
  - The [[Shemra Azem]], "Guardians of the Vigil," who serve as the sleepless army.
- The Shemra Azem are sustained by mortals held in eternal sleep as "dreamers."
- When a Shemra Azem soldier dies in battle, the corresponding dreamer in Cairn Dor may die and the soldier fades.
- This creates the current adventure problem: the stolen lizardfolk and other captives may still be alive as dreamers, but killing Shemra Azem may kill some of the people the party came to rescue.

%% Review: The dreamer-to-soldier metaphysics are powerful for the next adventure but are not fully worked out in the notes. Decide before play whether each Shemra Azem has one linked dreamer, whether groups share dreamers, and whether killing soldiers always kills dreamers. %%

### The Soul Compass

- The standing stones and plinths around the [[Cleaver-Stone]] form a soul compass.
- The soul compass lets Yeshara's forces target raids for dreamers across distance.
- The green sigil at [[Urkabi]], in the stolen villages, and over the Cleaver-Stone chamber appears to mark this magic.
- Taking or disabling the fort and soul compass greatly slows or stops new raids, but does not free those already trapped in Cairn Dor.

%% Review: The mechanism by which a fixed Apporian portal and soul compass steal entire villages across the west coast remains vague. If players press, the current notes support "the soul compass targeted dreamers and opened or guided raids," but not a precise spell-physics answer. %%

### Why Lizardfolk?

- The [[Lizardfolk]] note supports a broad cultural connection to dreams, ancestor omens, prophecy, and cyclical spiritual thinking.
- [[Dreamworld]] notes establish the Dreamworld as a realm of prophecy and a locus of lizardfolk mystic connection to ancestor guidance.
- [[GL - Session 62 - DM Notes]] says lizardfolk are especially strong as dreamers, making them efficient fuel for the Shemra Azem.

## The Historical War Cycle

### Apporian Shadow War

- [[Apporian Shadow War]] is dated DR 657-661.
- It was supposedly fought by [[Drankorian Empire|Drankorian]] armies against Yeshara and the Shemra Azem.
- The [[~Drankorian Cleaver-Stone Fort~]] was constructed afterward to guard the entrance to Cairn Dor and was maintained until the collapse of Drankor.
- [[GL - Session 62 - DM Notes]] says Drankorian armies reached the [[Cleaver-Stone]], built the fortress, and stopped further incursions because the soul compass became inaccessible.

### War of the Severed Dreams

- [[War of the Severed Dreams]] is dated DR 1191-1196.
- It is remembered as a war against Yeshara, the Shemra Azem, and enemies later called the [[Umbral Covenant]].
- [[Blue's Clues]] says scattered legends remember four Castrellan heroes: Atanza, Inor, Thusa, and Arippas. They supposedly fought to the center of a nightmare realm and locked the queen of eternal waking in a dreamless coma.
- [[GL - Session 62 - DM Notes]] says a small party reached the fort, damaged the soul compass, reactivated the Drankorian portal locks, and died. Atanza's soul or plane-touched essence may have crystallized into the crystal later implanted in [[Blue]].

%% Review: There are two versions of what the heroes accomplished: "locked Yeshara in a dreamless coma" in [[Blue's Clues]], and "damaged the soul compass/reactivated portal locks" in [[GL - Session 62 - DM Notes]]. These can be reconciled, but the reconciliation has not been written. %%

### War of the Dark Rift

- [[War of the Dark Rift]] is dated DR 1584-1597.
- The current public history says the [[Umbral Covenant]] arose in the interior of [[Apporia]], devastated the dwarven or stoneborn realm of [[Enderra]], conquered much of northern Apporia, disrupted the [[Old Chardon Canal]], destroyed [[Nevium]], besieged [[Castrella]], and was defeated after its Shadow Armada was destroyed by Chardon.
- [[Letter from Chardon for Samso on the Umbral Covenant]] says scholars think the dwarves of Enderra may have brought the doom on themselves by opening a rift, awakening buried enemies, or attracting a dark power. It also says the Umbral Covenant may originally have been more interested in something hidden in the mountains than in conquering Chardon.
- [[GL - Session 62 - DM Notes]] says the War of the Dark Rift did not begin from the Cleaver-Stone, but from a separate rift that remains undefined. The war weakened Yeshara's forces but also let them recapture the fort and rediscover the Cleaver-Stone.

%% Review: This is one of the weakest pieces of continuity. Official histories say Chardonian campaigns destroyed the Umbral Covenant after the Shadow Armada; DM notes say Yeshara's forces survived, regained the fort, and began a new slow build. This is workable if "destroyed" means "destroyed as a material army," but the point needs careful phrasing. %%

### Current Cycle

- The current cycle began with scattered disappearances and shadow attacks rather than open war.
- [[Urkabi]] vanished in DR 1737.
- [[Iara]]'s village disappeared in DR 1747.
- Other lizardfolk communities were hit over time, including the Lake Valandros information from [[Downtime Notes]] and the broader scattered lizardfolk disappearances noted on [[Cairn Dor]].
- By DR 1748-09, sleeping sickness and village disappearances were active in the [[Kedron]] valley.
- The party has now shut down the current Material Plane access mechanism, leaving the rescue in Cairn Dor itself.

## Open Questions and Weak Points

- How do the dreamers map to the Shemra Azem? The notes imply that killing Shemra Azem may kill dreamers, but the ratio and exceptions are not defined.
- How are dreamers awakened? [[Arc 5 Reboot]] says finding the dreamers is easy but releasing them requires solving or acquiring something. That mechanism is not yet in the notes.
- What exactly did the party's shutdown of the soul compass do? The portal was locked and the compass shut down, but it is unclear whether Cairn Dor can reopen the portal from the other side, use other portals, or keep raiding without the compass.
- How many lizardfolk are still alive? [[Urkabi]] vanished in DR 1737, eleven years before the current arc. If its people are dreamers, Cairn Dor must be preserving them in some unnatural way.
- What happened to non-lizardfolk victims in the Kedron valley? Session 62 established sleeping sickness and village disappearances there, but not whether those victims are also dreamers or collateral damage.
- Who or what is Yeshara now? Sources variously frame her as a queen, folk witch, darklord, wolf-eyed queen, and possibly someone once locked in a dreamless coma. Her current state in Cairn Dor is not specified.
- What is the present state of the Yo'nari? The notes say Shemra Azem soldiers threaten to make captives dreamers to help the [[Yo'nari]], and the DM notes distinguish citizens from soldiers, but the Yo'nari/Nurim-Dor relationship is not fully clear.
- What is the role of Ilanar in Session 63? He is Yeshara's brother in DM notes and the name heard in Mabist's vision, but he may be dead, sleeping, trapped, transformed, or only a memory.
- What is the intended ending? [[Arc 5 Reboot]] suggests a one-shot ending with victory, a return to Taelgar, and a time skip realization. The length and consequences of that time skip are not defined in existing notes.
- What does Blue learn from the crystal? The Atanza connection is useful but not yet revealed. If Session 63 uses it, decide whether Blue receives memories, warnings, recognition, or a mechanical effect.
- How does official history remain wrong? If the same enemy fought the ancient wars and the War of the Dark Rift, explain why Chardonian histories split them apart and why scholars only recently noticed the connection.

## Practical Session 63 Background Summary

The party is in [[Castrella]], recovered but not done. The Material Plane incursion route has been interrupted: the [[Cleaver-Stone]] is locked and the soul compass is shut down. The missing lizardfolk have not been rescued.

[[Cairn Dor]] is the next step. It is a [[Shadowfolds]] domain born from [[Yeshara]]'s failed or evil rite to free her people from dreams. Yeshara's forces use stolen dreamers to sustain the [[Shemra Azem]], their sleepless army. Lizardfolk are likely the preferred dreamers because of their connection to ancestors, prophecy, and the [[Dreamworld]]. The stolen people may still be alive, but may also be linked dangerously to the soldiers the party will fight.

The emotional spine is [[Samso]] finding out what happened to [[Urkabi]] and the other lizardfolk villages. The secondary character threads are [[Blue]]'s crystal, which may carry Atanza's memory from the War of the Severed Dreams, and [[Mabist]]'s strange resonance with Yeshara's visions despite the current plan not to make Mabist's fey/fathomless backstory the main arc.

The key design pressure is to let the party rescue the dreamers without contradicting the established danger that the Shemra Azem are sustained by them. The clearest existing setup is: enter Cairn Dor, find the dreamers, learn how they are bound to the army, discover the release mechanism, wake the dreamers, and escape as the army weakens.
