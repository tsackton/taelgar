# Yeshara, Wolf-Queen of Eternal Waking


_Medium Fey (Dread Lord), neutral evil_

**Armor Class** 19 (natural armor)  
**Initiative** +14 (24)  
**Hit Points** 285 (30d8 + 150)  
**Speed** 40 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|

**Saving Throws** Dex +10, Con +11, Wis +12, Cha +13  
**Skills** Arcana +10, Insight +12, Intimidation +13, Perception +18, Stealth +10  
**Damage Resistances** cold, necrotic, psychic

## Traits

### Dread Lord of Cairn Dor

- Yeshara does not need to sleep, cannot be magically put to sleep, and cannot be surprised while she is conscious.
- Yeshara cannot be truly killed while her domain exists. If she is reduced to 0 hit points, her waking body fades and she falls into a dreamless sleep for 1d4+1 centuries. 

### Dream-Fed Sovereign

If any current lizardfolk dreamer barrows remain sealed, Yeshara regains 20 hit points at the start of each of her turns and has advantage on her first attack roll each turn.

If all current lizardfolk dreamer barrows have been opened, this trait is suppressed.

%% Tuning note: If the party reaches this fight depleted, start with this trait suppressed even if some dreamers remain. If they skipped too many barrows or are very fresh, leave it active. %%

### Lidless Wakefulness

Creatures within 20 feet of Yeshara feel an oppressive exhaustion without the mercy of sleep. The first time an enemy starts its turn within 20 feet of Yeshara, or enters that area on a turn, it must succeed on a DC 20 Constitution saving throw or suffer 10 feet of lethargy until the end of its next turn.

### Lethargy

Some of Yeshara's features inflict lethargy. Each instance of lethargy reduces a creature's speed by 10 feet until the end of the creature's next turn. Lethargy is cumulative, but it cannot reduce a creature's speed below 0.

If lethargy reduces a creature's speed to less than half its normal speed, the creature takes a -2 penalty to AC. If lethargy reduces a creature's speed to 0, the creature falls prone and is restrained while its speed remains 0 from lethargy. If a creature already at speed 0 from lethargy would suffer another instance of lethargy, it takes 11 (2d10) psychic damage instead.

### Shattered Vigil (3/Day)

If Yeshara fails a saving throw, she can choose to succeed instead. When she does, her sleepless command falters until the end of her next turn. She must choose one target who is suffering lethargy, and remove all lethargy from that target. If there is no target suffering lethargy, her Lidless Wakefulness trait does not function until the start of her next turn. 
### Magic Resistance

Yeshara has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.
## Actions

### Multiattack

Yeshara makes two attacks, using Wolf-Shadow Claw or Lidless Nightmare in any combination, and she uses Lidless Stare. She can replace one attack with a use of Spellcasting.

### Wolf-Shadow Claw

_Melee Weapon Attack:_ +13 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target.  
_Hit:_ 26 (5d8 + 4) slashing damage. If the target is a creature, it suffers 10 feet of lethargy.

### Waking Nightmare

_Ranged Spell Attack:_ +13 to hit, range 120 ft., one target.  
_Hit:_ 27 (6d8) psychic damage. If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened of Yeshara until the end of its next turn.

### Lidless Stare

Yeshara fixes her gaze on one creature she can see within 90 feet. The target must make a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, the target takes 18 (4d8) psychic damage and is held in Yeshara's gaze until the end of its next turn.

While held in Yeshara's gaze, the target cannot Move in any way, cannot take the Disengage or Hide action, cannot benefit from being invisible. 

### Sleep Terror (Recharge 5-6)

Yeshara releases a howl of sleepless terror in a 60-foot cone. Each creature of Yeshara's choice in that area must make a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 54 (12d8) psychic damage and falls into a waking nightmare until the end of its next turn. While in this nightmare, the target cannot speak (it must continually scream) and perceives burning fires as if they are trapped in a building on fire (effectively surrounded by 10 feet of heavily obscured). On a success, target takes half damage only. 

### Spellcasting

Yeshara casts one of the following spells, requiring no material components and using Charisma as the spellcasting ability (spell save DC 20, +13 to hit with spell attacks):

At will: _command_, _detect thoughts_, _dispel magic_, _minor illusion_, _sleep_  
3/day each: _confusion_, _fear_, _slow_  
1/day each: _dream_, _eyebite_, _mass suggestion_, _hypnotic pattern_

## Bonus Actions

### Shadow-Wolf Step

Yeshara magically teleports up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space she can see within 10 feet of a creature that is frightened of her, unconscious, or suffering lethargy.

## Reactions

### Shivering Glare

When a creature within 60 feet of Yeshara hits her with an attack roll, they must make DC 20 Constitution saving throw or take 10 (3d6) cold damage. 

## Villain Actions

Yeshara has three villain actions. She can take each action once during an encounter after an enemy's turn. She can use only one villain action per round.

%% Tuning note: Use these if Yeshara is fighting alone or mostly alone. If the party is badly depleted, skip villain actions or use only one. %%

### Action 1: Do Not Close Your Eyes

Each enemy that can see Yeshara must choose: look away or meet her gaze. A creature that looks away is blinded until the end of its next turn. A creature that meets her gaze must succeed on a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw or take 27 (6d8) psychic damage and be unable to benefit from being hidden or invisible until the end of Yeshara's next turn.

### Action 2: Wake, and Feed Me

Yeshara tears every half-formed nightmare back into herself. She ends one spell or condition affecting her. Then each enemy of her choice within 120 feet that is affected by lethargy of Yeshara's features takes 27 (6d8) cold damage and removes their lethargy. 

Yeshara gains 30 temporary hit points, plus 10 THP for each creature damaged this way, then teleports up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space she can see.

### Action 3: Buried Awake

Pale barrow-stones rise around Yeshara's enemies. Each enemy within 120 feet must make a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes  8d8 psychic damage + 8d8 cold damage and is entombed in nightmare-stone. On a successful save, it takes half damage and is not entombed.

An entombed creature is restrained and blinded until the end of its next turn. A creature that is reduced to 0 hit points while buried awake becomes a dreamer.
