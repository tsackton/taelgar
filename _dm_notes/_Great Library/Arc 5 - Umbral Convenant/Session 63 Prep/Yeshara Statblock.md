# Yeshara, Wolf-Queen of Eternal Waking

%% First-pass Session 63 statblock generated at user request. Intended as a CR 19 solo/near-solo final guardian for the escape from [[Cairn Dor]]. Review numbers and action economy before play. %%

%% Design note: The active mechanics are split into three lanes: lethargy/slow effects (Lidless Wakefulness, Wolf-Shadow Claw, slow); nightmares and sleep terror (Lidless Nightmare, Sleep Nevermore, The Queen Opens Her Eyes, The Long Night Falls); and wakefulness/no escape (Dread Lord of Cairn Dor, Waking Command, Shattered Vigil, Shadow-Wolf Step, No Rest for the Fleeing, Drink the Unopened Dreams). %%

_Medium Fey (Dread Lord), neutral evil_

**Armor Class** 19 (natural armor)  
**Initiative** +14 (24)  
**Hit Points** 285 (30d8 + 150)  
**Speed** 40 ft.

**Proficiency Bonus** +6

## Traits

### Dread Lord of Cairn Dor

- Yeshara does not need to sleep, cannot be magically put to sleep, and cannot be surprised while she is conscious.
- Yeshara cannot be truly killed while her domain exists. If she is reduced to 0 hit points, her waking body fades and she falls into a dreamless sleep for 1d4+1 centuries. 

### Dream-Fed Sovereign

If any current lizardfolk dreamer barrows remain sealed, Yeshara regains 20 hit points at the start of each of her turns and has advantage on her first attack roll each turn.

If all current lizardfolk dreamer barrows have been opened, this trait is suppressed.

%% Tuning note: If the party reaches this fight depleted, start with this trait suppressed even if some dreamers remain. If they skipped too many barrows or are very fresh, leave it active. %%

### Lidless Wakefulness

Creatures within 20 feet of Yeshara feel an oppressive exhaustion without the mercy of sleep. The first time an enemy starts its turn within 20 feet of Yeshara, or enters that area on a turn, it must succeed on a DC 20 Constitution saving throw or suffer 10 feet of lethargy until the end of its next turn.

### Lethargy

Some of Yeshara's features inflict lethargy. Each instance of lethargy reduces a creature's speed by 10 feet until the end of the creature's next turn. Lethargy is cumulative, but it cannot reduce a creature's speed below 0.

If lethargy reduces a creature's speed to less than half its normal speed, the creature takes a -2 penalty to AC. If lethargy reduces a creature's speed to 0, the creature falls prone and is restrained while its speed remains 0 from lethargy. If a creature already at speed 0 from lethargy would suffer another instance of lethargy, it takes 11 (2d10) psychic damage instead.

### Shattered Vigil (3/Day)

If Yeshara fails a saving throw, she can choose to succeed instead. When she does, her sleepless command falters until the end of her next turn. She must choose one target who is suffering lethargy, and remove all lethargy from that target. If there is no target suffering lethargy, her Lidless Wakefulness trait does not function until the start of her next turn. 
### Magic Resistance

Yeshara has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.
## Actions

### Multiattack

Yeshara makes two attacks, using Wolf-Shadow Claw or Lidless Nightmare in any combination, and she uses Waking Command. She can replace one attack with a use of Spellcasting.

### Wolf-Shadow Claw

_Melee Weapon Attack:_ +13 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target.  
_Hit:_ 26 (5d8 + 4) slashing damage. If the target is a creature, it suffers 10 feet of lethargy.

### Lidless Nightmare

_Ranged Spell Attack:_ +13 to hit, range 120 ft., one target.  
_Hit:_ 27 (6d8) psychic damage. If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened of Yeshara until the end of its next turn.

### Waking Command

Yeshara snaps one creature she can see within 90 feet into a state of terrible, over-bright wakefulness. The target must make a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, the target takes 18 (4d8) psychic damage, then Yeshara chooses one of the following effects:

- **False Alarm.** The target lurches up to half its speed in a direction of Yeshara's choice, provoking opportunity attacks as normal.
- **Buckling Exhaustion.** The target drops prone.

An unconscious target automatically fails this saving throw and wakes before suffering the effect.

### Sleep Nevermore (Recharge 5-6)

Yeshara releases a howl of sleepless terror in a 60-foot cone. Each creature of Yeshara's choice in that area must make a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 54 (12d8) psychic damage and is frightened of Yeshara until the end of its next turn. On a successful save, a creature takes half as much damage and is not frightened.

If a creature fails the saving throw by 5 or more, it also falls unconscious until the end of its next turn, until it takes damage, or until another creature uses an action to shake it awake.

### Spellcasting

Yeshara casts one of the following spells, requiring no material components and using Charisma as the spellcasting ability (spell save DC 20, +13 to hit with spell attacks):

At will: _command_, _detect thoughts_, _dispel magic_, _minor illusion_, _sleep_  
3/day each: _confusion_, _fear_, _hypnotic pattern_, _slow_  
1/day each: _dream_, _eyebite_, _mass suggestion_

## Bonus Actions

### Shadow-Wolf Step

Yeshara magically teleports up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space she can see within 10 feet of a creature that is frightened of her, unconscious, or suffering lethargy.

## Reactions

### No Rest for the Fleeing

When a creature Yeshara can see within 60 feet moves at least 10 feet away from her, or enters the exit portal, Yeshara teleports to an unoccupied space she can see within 10 feet of that creature and makes one Wolf-Shadow Claw attack against it. On a hit, the attack does not apply lethargy, but the target's speed becomes 0 until the end of the current turn.

## Villain Actions

Yeshara has three villain actions. She can take each action once during an encounter after an enemy's turn. She can use only one villain action per round.

%% Tuning note: Use these if Yeshara is fighting alone or mostly alone. If the party is badly depleted, skip villain actions or use only one. %%

### Action 1: The Queen Opens Her Eyes

Yeshara opens all the eyes in Cairn Dor. Until the end of Yeshara's next turn, the battlefield fills with dim gray shadow, wolf-shaped silhouettes pace at the edge of sight, and creatures of Yeshara's choice within 120 feet cannot benefit from being invisible.

Each enemy within 120 feet that can see or hear Yeshara must succeed on a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw or see a flash of itself buried awake beneath pale stone. On a failed save, the target takes 18 (4d8) psychic damage and is frightened of Yeshara until the end of its next turn.

### Action 2: Drink the Unopened Dreams

Yeshara tears herself awake by drinking from every dream still sealed in Cairn Dor. Yeshara ends one spell or condition affecting her, teleports up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space she can see, and regains 70 hit points if any current lizardfolk dreamer barrows remain sealed, or 35 hit points if all current lizardfolk dreamer barrows have been opened.

### Action 3: The Long Night Falls

Yeshara tries to bury the waking world under the sleep she has denied herself. Each enemy within 90 feet that can hear her must make a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 45 (10d8) psychic damage and falls unconscious until the end of its next turn, until it takes damage, or until another creature uses an action to shake it awake. On a successful save, a creature takes half as much damage and does not fall unconscious.
