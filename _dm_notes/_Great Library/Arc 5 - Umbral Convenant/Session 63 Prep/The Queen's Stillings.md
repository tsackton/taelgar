# The Queen's Stillings

%% Speculative Session 63 prep generated at user request. This note develops the Stillings as adventure structure for [[Cairn Dor]] and should be reviewed after play. %%

## Core Idea

The Queen's Stillings are guarded burial-preserves in [[Cairn Dor]], maintained by the [[Shemra Azem]] in the name of [[Yeshara]]. In local belief, these are dangerous places where sleep is contained. People who fall too deeply into sleep, dream-sickness, or other spiritual peril may be taken to the Stillings so the Shemra Azem can keep the danger sealed away from the waking folk of Cairn Dor.

This is both propaganda and truth. The Stillings are dangerous because they contain enormous concentrations of magically enforced sleep. The sleepers are the "dreamers" whose endless sleep sustains the Shemra Azem. The current Stillings hold the missing [[Lizardfolk]] dreamers, including, likely, the people of [[Urkabi]] and other vanished villages tied to [[Samso]]'s story.

The Stillings should not feel like a dungeon hidden beneath Sarren Kir. They are part of Cairn Dor's ordinary geography: real places in the woods, moors, or old field margins, marked by wolf-boundary stones and avoided by villagers. They are hidden and protected by taboo, local fear, military patrols, and simple warding that discourages curiosity.

## Public Understanding in Cairn Dor

Most ordinary people in [[Eshlem]] and the smaller settlements know some version of the following:

- The Stillings are where dangerous sleep is contained.
- Too much sleep can spread, corrupt, or steal the soul.
- The Shemra Azem guard the Stillings because only the sleepless can safely approach them.
- No one should linger near the mounds, touch the stones, climb the roofs, or try to hear what is underneath.
- People who sleep too much, fail to wake, or show signs of dream-sickness may be taken there.
- The Wolf-Queen keeps the waking safe by keeping sleep still.


Useful local phrasing:

- "Best not ask what sleeps in the Stillings."
- "If sleep gets its teeth in you, the Queen's guards take you where it cannot spread."
- "Never crack a roof. Never let the day touch what the Queen has stilled."
- "The Shemra Azem do not guard the Stillings from us. They guard us from the Stillings."

## The Different Stillings

There should be at least two and possibly three or four Stillings. Only the newest one is actively guarded in force. The older Stillings are optional exploration sites that can provide context, clues, or advantages, but they should not be required to finish the adventure.

### The Current Stilling

The current Stilling contains the living lizardfolk dreamers. It is the freshest, most heavily guarded, and most obviously important site. It has new boundary stones, fresh patrol tracks, recently disturbed earth, green sigils, wetland tokens, lizardfolk craft items, and a stronger soporific weight than the rest of Cairn Dor.

This is the active objective. The party can go straight here if they learn enough in Eshlem, follow patrols, capture a guard, use divination, follow Samso's connection to his people, or otherwise push directly toward the freshest guarded site.

### The Dark Rift Stilling

One older Stilling dates to the [[War of the Dark Rift]]. It contains dead or spent dwarven dreamers, likely tied to [[Enderra]] and the old emergence of the [[Umbral Covenant]].

This site is useful but optional. If the party enters Cairn Dor through the Enderra route, they might arrive here. Otherwise, locals may mention it as an old, cursed Stilling where the sleep went bad or where the dead do not rest properly.

### The Severed Dreams Stilling

One older Stilling dates to the [[War of the Severed Dreams]]. This is the precedent or ritual ancestor for the current practice. It may contain older dead, broken roof-stones, traces of Atanza and the earlier heroes, or clues tied to [[Blue]]'s crystal.

This site should be optional. It can deepen the mystery and make Blue's connection more legible, but the main adventure should not depend on visiting it first.

### Possible Fourth Stilling

If useful, add a smaller Stilling for recent non-lizardfolk victims from the [[Kedron]] valley. This can complicate the party's assumptions and show that Yeshara's current campaign is broader than Samso's people, even if lizardfolk are the most efficient or targeted dreamers.

## The Barrows

Each Stilling contains long barrows: low, pale mounds with stone or chalk roof-slabs beneath turf and roots. They draw on English prehistoric long barrow imagery rather than fortress, crypt, or catacomb imagery.

The lizardfolk dreamers are not woken by opening doors, speaking a password, or defeating a single guardian. They wake when the barrow roof is broken and light enters. The roof is the seal. The release is to "turn out" the barrow and let the sky in.

Do not make ordinary torchlight the solution. The important light is the impossible daylight of Cairn Dor entering through a broken roof. Once the seal is breached, pale dawn-light, gray sky-light, or green-gold waking light enters from above, even if the surrounding domain is foggy or dim.

## Clues at the Current Stilling

The party should be able to solve the barrows even if they go straight to the current, most guarded Stilling. Use several clues at once so this is not a single-roll bottleneck.

### Local Rumors

Before reaching the Stilling, villagers can warn the party:

- "Do not crack the roofs."
- "Do not let the day touch what sleeps."
- "The Queen's stones keep sleep from spreading."
- "People who listen at the mounds hear voices, and then they never sleep right again."

These are local fear-statements, not formal exposition.

### Boundary Stones

Wolf-marked boundary stones ring the Stilling. They can bear simple inscriptions or repeated ritual phrases:

> Let no dawn enter. Let no roof break. Let sleep lie still beneath the Queen's stone.

Other shorter versions:

- Stone above, sleep below.
- No dawn within.
- The roof holds the sleep.
- The Queen stills what must not wake.

### Guard Behavior

The Shemra Azem should reveal the rule through tactics. They care more about the barrow roofs than about entrances, side paths, or symbolic markers.

If a PC climbs a mound, damages a roof-stone, casts stone shape, uses shatter, uses earth tremor, starts digging from above, or otherwise threatens the roof, the guards panic and redirect immediately. A commander might shout:

> Hold the crowns. If light reaches them, the vigil fails.

"Crowns" can be the Shemra Azem term for the roof-stones or capstones.

### The Flawed Barrow

One current barrow should have a cracked roof or flawed capstone. A little gray light leaks in. The dreamers below are stirring, whispering, or twitching. Nearby Shemra Azem are weaker, flickering, distracted, or physically pained.

This is the tutorial barrow. It demonstrates the release method before the party commits to opening all the mounds.

### Dreamer Voices

If Samso or another PC listens at a mound, they can hear faint voices below. This should not require a high roll to get the basic clue.

Simple phrases:

- "Open the sky."
- "Too dark."
- "Morning. Please."
- "Let the light in."
- "We are under stone."

A stronger check, Samso's personal connection, or relevant magic can reveal names, village memories, lizardfolk language, or emotional detail tied to [[Urkabi]].

### Blue or Mabist Resonance

If useful, [[Blue]]'s crystal or [[Mabist]]'s prior contact with Yeshara can provide a sensory flash: lying under stone, hearing soldiers above, feeling sleep pressed into the lungs, and knowing that the roof is the lock.

This should support the clues, not replace them. It is best used if the players are close but stuck.

### Immediate Feedback

When the first roof is breached, give unmistakable feedback:

- Light enters.
- A dreamer wakes.
- A nearby Shemra Azem weakens, flickers, collapses, or loses some power.
- The barrow's sleep-aura breaks or lessens.
- Distant horns, bells, or wolf-howls announce the alarm.

This confirms the rule and starts the pressure phase.

## Running the Action

The challenge should not be guessing the method. The challenge should be executing it under pressure.

Possible structure:

1. The party learns of the Queen's Stillings in Eshlem or through scouting.
2. They locate the current Stilling by rumors, patrols, divination, Samso's connection, or direct investigation.
3. They fight or bypass the first guards.
4. They identify the barrows and learn that the roof-seals must be broken.
5. Opening the first barrow wakes some dreamers and triggers the alarm.
6. Reinforcements arrive while the party tries to open the remaining barrows.
7. Each opened barrow weakens the Shemra Azem but increases the urgency of the response.
8. Once enough dreamers wake, the party must escape Cairn Dor with them.

Useful pressure dials:

- Each barrow roof takes time, strength, magic, or tools to breach.
- Breaching a roof is loud.
- The barrows impose a soporific weight, such as a Wisdom save against magical sleep.
- Dreamers wake disoriented and may need protection or guidance.
- The more barrows open, the more Shemra Azem reinforcements converge.
- The more dreamers wake, the weaker individual Shemra Azem become.

## Design Notes

The Stillings should make Cairn Dor's paranoia coherent. The people fear sleep; the Shemra Azem claim to protect them from it; the Queen's regime hides the truth in plain sight by saying the Stillings are dangerous sleep-prisons. That explanation is false morally, but true physically.

The oldest Stillings let the players explore history if they choose. They can reveal dead dwarves from the [[War of the Dark Rift]], older traces from the [[War of the Severed Dreams]], or Blue's connection to Atanza. But the lizardfolk rescue should remain runnable even if the party goes straight to the newest guarded Stilling.
