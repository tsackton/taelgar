Also set up the scene in Castrella. Chardonian proconsul has sent word back about Yeshara and a contingent of wizards are on their way to repair the fort and check the seals on the gate and the soul compass. 

Then set the plot: 
- Samso's people, and probably many others, are trapped in Cairn Dor, trapped as "dreamers" whose endless sleep powers the [[Shemra Azem]], the never-sleeping army of [[Yeshara]]. 
- Somehow, the party has to return to Cairn Dor, find the sleeping lizardfolk, wake them up, and escape.

## Cairn Dor

Cairn Dor is a reflection and evolution of the [[Yo'nari]] settlers dragged here in a time long past. Yeshara, the Wolf-Queen, has ruled forever, protecting her people from the dangers of the night. 

There is one major village, Eshlem, where most of the population lives, though there are numerous scattered homesteads in two other smaller villages, Belmarn and Sarnel. Organized a bit like [[Raha]], though much more agriculturally focused. No, or at least not many, pigs/cows/horses, but lots of chickens. Boundary stones with the wolf motif mark the edges of the village. 

Eshlem is not unwelcoming, though travelers are rare and often treated with suspicion; there is a lot of local fear about fey who steal your soul while you sleep. The vibe is very strongly that the [[Shemra Azem]] protect them from the dangers of dreams. Without them, people would die every night, just not wake up. The people don't really know about the dreamers or anything like that, they just know that those who are enlightened and transcend the need for sleep are called to protect others who still occasionally fall into danger. 

Outside the villages, this is deep/dense old English woods, often foggy, with crags and outcroppings. 

The Wolf Queen, [[Yeshara]], lives in hill fort, and rarely leaves, preferring to brood over her failures than interact with the population. This is called Sarren Kir, the fort of the sleepless. Most villagers can point it out, though will need a reason why. 

### Sleeping in Cairn Dor

There is an oppressive feeling of wakefulness. It isn't a cheerful, perky energy - it is that feeling of being over tired, exhausted but unable to sleep. While short rests are fine, sleep and long rests have special rules. 

Night is a scary thing in Cairn Dor. People light big fires, many people *try* to stay away, instead of trying to sleep. Because you can never die from sleeplessness, everyone treats mild exhaustion as just the natural state of being. Some folks have wild parties all night to stay awake; there are chants, dances, drinks. Others make themselves uncomfortable, or sit a little too close to raging fires. Improvise here but lots of rituals. Make it clear people aren't scared of the dark, they are scared of sleep. 

(1) After 24 hours without sleep in Cairn Dor, you must make a DC 10 Constitution saving throw. If you fail, you gain one level of Exhaustion. You have to repeat the saving throw after every subsequent 24 hours, with the DC increasing by 2 each time, but if you already have a level of Exhaustion from this source you cannot gain another. 

(2) Falling asleep is challenging. If you try to sleep, you must make a DC 12 Consittution saving throw. If you fail, you cannot sleep, and you must make an Exhaustion saving throw. If you succeed, you fall asleep. 

(3) Sleep is not restful. Even if you do succeed, you must also pass a DC 12 Wisdom saving throw. If you fail, your sleep is haunted by the howls of wolves and the lidless eyes and face of Yeshara yelling at you to wake. You gain no benefit of a Long Rest, and you must make an Exhaustion saving throw. If you succeed, you gain the benefits of a Long Rest and can remove one level of exhaustion. 

Most NPCs in Cairn Dor either do not need to sleep or have a permanent level of Exhaustion.
