Winter comes early. The winter of 1747–1748 is bitterly cold and storm-lashed, marked by repeated gales and then a hard freeze that leaves ice lingering far longer than it should. Many die of exposure. Mawar hunkers down. In Hamri, it is a winter of hunger and hardship, when people speak in low voices of nightmares and strange songs on the wind, though no supernatural danger ever fully emerges. The ordinary dangers are enough. Cold, hunger, and isolation take their toll, and for many families it becomes a winter of loss. Little news comes in or out, and the Leviathan Inn stands quieter than it has in years.

Spring comes late in 1748. April is a month of dreary rain and shuttered harbors, with weeks of gray weather keeping boats close to shore and roads half-drowned in mud. Not until May does the weather finally break.

As spring turns to summer, the port of Hamri fills with news and trade. Wild rumors from the north, of an ancient black dragon, a vicious beast that defeated even the best Chardonian legions, and drove the orc warlord Grumella before him in fear -- killed by the heroes of the Battle of Voltara. From Chardon, the tales in the spring repeat the common refrains. From the merchants and traders, the stories of the wealth and glory of the Empire; from rebels and free-thinkers, stories of the tightening fist of the wizards of the Academy. Rumors of scholarly expeditions, sent to plunder treasure, ancient artifacts of magic and power, from all points of the compass to enrich the Academy. But none of these expeditions ever come to Mawar, and it is hard to tell if these are just stories. 

The summer passes cool and glorious, the fish plentiful, the hunting good. The Leviathan is full of the usual make-believe stories from far away: demons and centaurs fighting in the deserts of Dunmar; inland lizardfolk villages vanishing without a trace; a haunted ghost of a dragon raiding on the shores of Lake Valandros. Who can really say what is true, and what is a tall tale told for coin. 

As summer turns to fall, rumors spread from the south, of a daring theft, the merchants say, powerful magic stolen from a great wizard of Chardon, who was viciously attacked and betrayed by outsiders in the city. Something about a giant's crown full of ancient elemental magic. A particularly daring captain of a Chardonian ship spent an evening trying to harass the guests of the Levianthan with descriptions of these thieves -- a halfling, an elf, a human, and a dwarf -- but was quickly tossed out by Wazir. 

The fall of 1748 lingered, with warm days and calm seas into October. A few folks from Chardon started trickling in, telling a different tale of Chardon. Kaeso, a scholar from Chardon with several mutual friends with Wazir, arrived at the start of October. He told of a greed spreading in the heart of Chardon, a grasping need to gather all magic to the University and control it. He didn't have all the pieces, but said that Kenzo, the human accused of theft, was nothing like the stories spread by Chardonian agents claimed. He was a good kid, scarred by Chardon's dark side as a child but always looking to connect to the souls of things. Kaeso and Wazir could frequently be found in long evening chats as the days grew shorter, wondering what these events meant for Mawar. Could it be long before Chardon looked to claim Mawar's secrets and treasures? After all, Airion the Mistspeaker had long spoken of the Mawarkel Peninsula as an important locus of elemental power, and Airion's scrolls of elemental power had turned up in Chardon, Kaeso had learned. 

By the first storm of winter, more news: there was war against orcs to the east, spreading through Dunmar and tearing the land apart. Kaeso took this hard, and spent many days alone in his room. But emerged to say that Dunmar held, though would not say how he knew. 

The winter of 1748-49 was mild, but still, little traffic comes to Hamri, and news is slow during the cold months. Spring arrives with news of peace, it seems the fighting in Dunmar was over, though tales differed about who was king now there. But few in Hamri really cared about the events of far-off Dunmar. The traders from Chardon whispered that something seemed wrong. Although the amount of chalyte flowing into the city was greater than ever, constant shortages lead to the first outbreak of cholera in years (though, Kaeso would point out, the traders don't seem to count the sickness that spreads on the slums of the north bank, here). It was getting harder to ignore the growing number of people wandering the city, their minds consumed by chalyte-overuse. 

It is now May 21st, 1749. Not long ago, news reached Hamri of a vicious, unprovoked magical attack on the heart of Chardon itself, caused by none other than two of the thieves: Kenzo, a Dunmari spy, and Delwath, an evil elf corrupted by shadows. Many had died in the Kalos Market as a fragment of Pandemonium was ripped into this world. Fausto, one of the leaders of the wizards, was agitating for war, and it seemed the Magistros would comply. Some rumors claimed that Fausto was already marching at the head of a column of war wizards, claiming he alone rightfully could command the ancient wizardly might of Chardon.
