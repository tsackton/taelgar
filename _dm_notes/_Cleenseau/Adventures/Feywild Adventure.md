# Thorn Maze (Duskmire Gate)

### Zone 1 – Outer Briars

- Dense thorn hedges forming shifting corridors; visibility limited.    
- Vines subtly **grow inward toward the center** (visual clue where to go).
- Occasional **thorn lashes** from the walls if characters rush or cut through.
- Fey ritualists know the paths; PCs may find **trampled routes or blood traces**.
- Minor Duskmire corruption: black sap, warped leaves, faint humming from the gate.

---

### Zone 2 – Living Maze

- Maze actively shifts; passages close slowly behind the party.
- **Grasping vines** attempt to restrain or slow PCs moving carelessly.
- Signs of ritual activity: carved stakes, black-thread charms, spilled blood.
- Optional encounter: **thorn predator** stalking the corridors.
- Distant **green light and ritual chanting** from deeper within.
    

---

### Zone 3 – The Knot

- Central clearing where thorns twist into a **massive living knot** (keyston).
- 3 large **thorn roots** anchor the knot to the ground.
- Ritual circle and sacrifice site nearby; Duskmire agents defending it.
- Destroying the roots or disrupting the ritual destabilizes the gate.
- When the knot breaks → **violent backlash wave through the maze**.

---

# Thorn Guardian (appears after backlash)

Ancient Duskmire construct formed from the maze itself.

**Purpose**

- Punish the violation of the gate and destroy Duskmire corruption.
    
- Semi-programmed; not truly intelligent.
    

**Behavior**

- Forms from collapsing thorns after ritual failure.
    
- Prioritizes **Duskmire agents or corrupted vines**.
    
- Mostly ignores PCs unless attacked or they interfere.
    

**End condition**

- When corrupted vines / ritual focus destroyed → guardian collapses into briars.
    
- If attacked → fights briefly but does not pursue beyond the maze.

# ortress of Ianto the Red — Room Notes

### 1. Blood Gate

- Jagged stone entrance with **spiked iron gate** and chains hanging from the cliff.    
- **2 redcaps** lounging near the entrance; **2 goblin slaves** hauling supplie.
- Skulls, broken weapons, and armor fragments nailed to the rock.
- Heavy **iron boot prints** stamped into the dirt everywhere.
- From inside, the party hears **distant laughter and the clang of iron boots**.

---

### 2. Courtyard (Central Hub)

- Open stone yard with **braziers, weapon racks, and blood-stained ground**.    
- Poles displaying **minor trophies**: horns, claws, helmets.
- Goblins move quickly here doing chores while avoiding redcaps.
- Three passages lead to **Goblin Warrens, Butchery/Living Area, and Throne Hall**.
- From certain angles the party can glimpse **the wings turning in the wind beyond the throne rom**.
    

---

### 3. Goblin Warrens

- Low cramped tunnels with **smoke, cooking fires, and ragged bedding**  
- **5–6 goblin slaves**, frightened but eager to bargain.
- Hidden caches of stolen food and small trinkets.
- Goblins know **patrol patterns, secret passages, and the vault location**.
- One goblin quietly whispers: _“Boss keeps the good trophies behind him.”_

---

### 4. Fey Butchery & Living Area

- Stone tables stained with blood and **hooks hanging from chains**.
- Redcaps butcher animals and prepare trophies here.
- **2 redcaps** usually resting, eating, or sharpening weapons.
- Bones, hides, and stripped armor pieces piled around the room.
- A nervous goblin servant here may offer **a distraction or sabotage**.

---

### 5. Throne Hall

- Rough cavernous chamber with **iron braziers casting red light**.
- **Ianto’s crude stone throne** sits at the far end of the hall.
- Ianto and **2 redcap guards** usually present.
- Behind the throne is an **iron-barred opening into the vault**.
- Through the bars the PCs clearly see **the wings mounted among the trophies**.
    

---

### 6. Trophy Vault

- Iron-gated chamber packed with **hooks, chains, and mounted trophies**.
- Major trophies include **the wings**, a troll skull, enchanted armor, and strange fey relics.
- Central iron rack where Ianto mounts his **greatest victories**.
- Room smells of **old blood, metal, and drying leather**.
- A narrow **escape crack in the rock behind the vault** leads down the mountain.

# Ianto the Red

_Redcap Trophy Lord_

Large Fey, Chaotic Evil

**Armor Class** 18 (trophy armor, iron boots)  
**Hit Points** 145 (17d10 + 51)  
**Speed** 25 ft

STR 20 (+5)  
DEX 14 (+2)  
CON 18 (+4)  
INT 11 (+0)  
WIS 12 (+1)  
CHA 15 (+2)

**Saving Throws** Str +9, Con +8  
**Skills** Athletics +9, Intimidation +6  
**Damage Resistances** bludgeoning, piercing, slashing from nonmagical attacks  
**Senses** Darkvision 60 ft  
**Languages** Common, Goblin, Sylvan  
**CR** ~9

---

# Traits

### Iron Boots

Ianto cannot be knocked prone.

If he moves **10 ft straight toward a creature** and hits it with **Stomp**, the target must succeed on a **DC 16 Strength save** or fall prone.

---

### Blood Cap

Whenever Ianto deals damage to a creature, he gains **8 temporary hit points**.

If he goes **two rounds without dealing damage**, he becomes enraged until he deals damage again:

- advantage on attack rolls
- disadvantage on Wisdom saving throws

---

### Trophy Armor

Ianto wears fragments of old trophies strapped to his armor.
He gains **+2 AC against ranged attacks**.

---

# Legendary Resistance — Trophy Sacrifice (2/day)

When Ianto fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.

When he does, **one of the trophies strapped to his armor shatters or falls away**, reducing his AC by **1 for the rest of the fight**.

Examples of trophies breaking:

- a griffon talon charm
- a cracked knight helm
- a bone mask
    

---

# Actions

### Multiattack

Ianto makes **two Cleaver attacks and one Stomp**.

---

### Cleaver

Melee Weapon Attack: +9 to hit, reach 5 ft

Hit: **13 (2d8 + 5) slashing damage**

If the target is **prone**, deal **+5 damage**.

---

### Iron Boot Stomp

Melee Weapon Attack: +9 to hit

Hit: **11 (2d6 + 5) bludgeoning damage**
	
Target must succeed on a **DC 15 Constitution save** or become **dazed** until the end of its next turn  
(disadvantage on next attack and cannot take reactions).

---

# Special Action

### Trophy Taker (Recharge 5–6)

Ianto attempts to **claim a trophy from a living enemy**.

Melee attack: +9 to hit

Hit: **10 (2d4 + 5) slashing damage**

Target must succeed on a **DC 15 Strength or Dexterity save** or suffer one effect:

- weapon knocked **10 ft away**
    
- helmet or armor torn loose (**−1 AC until repaired**)
    
- cloak/backpack ripped away
    
- lock of hair or symbolic item taken
    

Ianto immediately **hangs the trophy on his belt** while laughing.

---

# Bonus Action

### Brutal Kick

Creature within 5 ft must succeed on **DC 15 Strength save** or be pushed **10 ft**.

If pushed into a wall or creature it takes **5 extra damage**.

---

# Reaction

### Trophy Hunter

When a creature within 5 ft takes damage from another source, Ianto may make **one Cleaver attack** against it.

---
Villian Action
- Stomp, if someone within 5 ft, plus 15 ft move

Ianto fights like a **duelist champion**.

Typical turn:

1. **Stomp to knock someone down**
    
2. **Cleaver attacks**
    
3. **Kick enemies into walls or hazards**
    

He constantly taunts the party.

Example lines:

# Krat the Whisperer

Thin, nervous goblin with huge ears and twitchy eyes.

He speaks very quietly and constantly checks the tunnels behind him.

Personality:

- paranoid
- observant
- knows everything happening in the fortress

Useful Information:

- knows **which redcaps are on patrol**
- knows **Ianto sleeps lightly but drinks heavily**
- knows about the **escape crack behind the vault**

Typical dialogue:

> “Quiet quiet quiet. Boots hear everything.”

What he wants:

- safe passage out of the fortress
- food

---

# Droog the Cook

Fat goblin with a stained apron and a huge cleaver that is clearly too big for him.

He works in the **butchery area** preparing food for the redcaps.

Personality:

- loud whisperer
- strangely friendly
- terrified of Ianto

Useful Information:

- knows **when the redcaps eat and gather**
- knows **where keys and tools are kept**
- can cause **a distraction in the butchery**

Typical dialogue:

> “Droog hates boss. Boss eats too much.”

What he wants:

- to leave with the PCs
- a promise he won’t be eaten by monsters outside

---
