The fey gate in the Cleenseau wood was an ancient gate tied to the trees and strength of the wood that faded over time as Duskmire changed. 

It was likely originally tied to Morlaith and the briarwood vibe, and has become unstable and shrunk as his powerbase has disappeared from the region. Umbraeth doesn't know 

My D&D game this afternoon is the showdown between the PCs and Umbraeth's attempt to permanantly reopen the fey gate in the southern Cleenseau wood. Metaphysic idea (which will likely not come up in play but could drive some clues)... The fey gate has been established (on the Duskmire side) as a woody, thorny maze with a dense bramble in the middle that leads into Taelgar (on the Taelgar side, the vibe is similar - a dense bramble that you get lost in where you appear in the Feywild) This struck me as very Morlaith -vibe, so my idea was something like... In the ancient days, Morlaith's realm extended much farther than it does now, and the southern Cleenseau realm was tied to Morlaith, and this fey gate was basically "part of" his essence in some fashion. Over the many years since Morlaith has retreated, this gate has flickered and foundered, working sometimes, not working other times, as Morlaith is no longer consciously maintaining it. In the late 1719/early 1720 the lich provided (via some undefined powerful magic, probably a chaylte artifact of some type) a stablizing force - something very arcane and technical - which kept the gate open (in exchange for Umbraeth's aid in his eventual plan to try to get Tyrwingha and Sembara to fight) But then the Night Queen folks came close to finding out the lich, and he withdrew support from Umbraeth to dedicate his energies much more directly to trying to kill the threat of the night queen paladins and such (which ultimately backfired, but...) This left the gate closed... but Umbraeth had since discovered a ritual he could perform - using Ethlenn's essence in the form of the Queen of Tyrwingha's blood - that would stablize the gate. ...

The ritual requires - for reasons I'm not sure need explaining - that Umbraeth form a connection to the gate in some fashion - perhaps it is effectively ripping it away from the last vestiges of assocation with Morlaith. But Umbraeth is cautious, and establishes himself far away from the gate, as if the ritual goes wrong, the backlash could be dangerous, especially for him (as he is connected to the energies of the gate)... This sets up the confrontation where the PCs save the Queen (hopefully!) and then have the opportunity to disrupt the ritual and destroy (with fire or magic) the thorny maze (as it is no longer connected to any fey power, Umbraeth having succeeded enough to tear it finally away from Morlaith) and thus the gate. Which in turn knocks out Umbraeth for a bit, giving my PCs an opportunity to escape Duskmire and kick off the fey adventure
