# Yeshara
>[!info]+ Biographical Info
> A wolf-eyed queen (she/her)
>> `$=dv.view("_scripts/view/get_Whereabouts")`

Yeshara, the Wolf-Queen or Queen of Eternal Waking, is an ancient ruler associated with [[Cairn Dor]], the [[Cleaver-Stone]], and the sleepless [[Shemra Azem]]. Traditions in Apporia connect her to the vanished [[Yo'nari]] and to old wars of shadow and severed dreams, though official histories disagree on the sequence and meaning of those legends.

%%^Campaign:none%%

## DM notes

- (DR:: 1748-09-18): In [[Castrella]], the [[Silver Tempests]] researched Yeshara as the wolf queen or wolf-eyed queen, an ancient figure associated with a people who once lived nearby, fear of the dark, the sleepless [[Shemra Azem]], and legends such as the War of Dark Rift, War of Severed Dreams, and Apporian Shadow War. The session notes explicitly say official histories disagree. **Source:** [[Great Library Session Notes - Arc 5]].
- [[Letter from Chardon for Samso on the Umbral Covenant]] says the Wolf-Queen, also called the Queen of Eternal Waking, was often dismissed as a folk legend or trickster fey but was probably real and may have come from the first human tribes to settle [[Apporia]]. The letter links her to repeated appearances of the Shemra-Azem and the motto or sigil "Sleep Nevermore."
- (DR:: 1748-09-20): At the [[Cleaver-Stone]] site, the party found murals of Yeshara leading people beneath a trilith and evidence that the portal and soul compass were tied to the theft of mortal dreamers for [[Cairn Dor]]. [[Mabist]] had a vision of lidless Yeshara saying Cairn Dor would consume him. **Sources:** [[Great Library Session Notes - Arc 5]]; [[Cairn Dor]]; [[Lizardfolk Village Disappearances]].
- DM background identifies Yeshara as queen of the [[Yo'nari]], a proto-[[Deno'qai]] people. It says her brother Ilanar vanished after a fey bargain and returned in dream-torpor, leading Yeshara toward fear of sleep, a cult of wakefulness, and the rite at the Cleaver-Stone. This origin is DM-background material and should remain reviewable rather than treated as settled public canon. **Sources:** [[Session 63 Background]]; [[Yo'nari]].
- (DR:: 1748-10-14): The party reached [[Cairn Dor]], met Yeshara in Sarren Kir, learned she could not be harmed while dreamers slept, and defeated her after daylight woke the dreamers and killed the linked Shemra Azem. After the fight, the party fell into sleep or stasis until 1752; when they left, Yeshara and the inhabitants of Cairn Dor still slept. **Sources:** [[Great Library Session Notes - Arc 5]]; [[Shemra Azem]].
- Source limitations: Yeshara's exact nature remains unresolved across the notes. She is framed variously as an ancient queen, folk witch, trickster fey, darklord-like figure, wolf-eyed ruler, and possible first-tribes human. Keep those possibilities separate unless later canon settles them. **Sources:** [[Great Library Session Notes - Arc 5]]; [[Session 63 Background]]; [[Letter from Chardon for Samso on the Umbral Covenant]].
