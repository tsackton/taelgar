# Morlaith
>[!info]+ Information  
> An ancient fey power

Morlaith, also called the Briarheart, is an ancient fey power of brambles, hidden hollows, old paths, and dangerous passages between the [[Feywild]] and the [[Material Plane]]. He is not a courtly lord in the manner of [[Archfey Ethlenn]] or [[Lord Serenveil]], and not an ambitious tormentor in the manner of [[Lord Umbraeth]]. He is older, stranger, and less personal: a remnant of the deep wild, closer to an old god of roots and thresholds than to a lord of a court.

The feeling of Morlaith is old fairy-tale danger. His paths are not evil in a simple sense, but they are not safe. A traveler may find a thorn maze where there was only a copse, a hollow where every direction leads inward, or a door that opens only to song, wind, and the right kind of fear. Those who enter may return changed, confused, or not at all. Those who keep the old courtesies may pass where the careless are taken.

Morlaith's danger is not cruelty for sport. Mortals are beneath his notice until they disturb the rules that keep his country in balance. When those rules are broken, the consequences feel less like punishment than collapse: a gate opens, a boundary fails, a song turns wrong, or something worse is allowed through because the old support was knocked away.

## Mortal Traditions

In [[Addermarch]], especially in the [[Highmoor]], [[Westcliff]], and the older hill villages of the west, Morlaith is feared and placated. Villagers set wards on ridgelines, leave offerings, repeat rhymes, and keep to inherited rules whose original reasons are half-forgotten. This is not worship in the grand temple sense. It is practical respect for something older than law.

These customs distinguish western Addermarch from [[Tyrwingha]]. Tyrwinghan fey traditions are bound up with Ethlenn's protection and the work of keeping a bargain with a powerful patron. Addermarian fey traditions are more fearful. They belong to a world of warning stones, frightening rhymes, hill paths avoided after dusk, and offerings made so that the old powers continue to ignore the village.

Morlaith is therefore malicious only in the way the deep forest is malicious: placable, patterned, and still capable of killing the unwary. A village may appease him for generations and be left alone; a fool may trespass once and bring a season of trouble down on everyone nearby.

## Domain and Presence

Morlaith's influence lingers in the hills and river valleys of western [[Addermarch]], where the Feywild feels less like a court and more like an old wood that remembers every bargain ever made beneath its branches. His realm is associated with brambles, thorn mazes, hidden hollows, singing trees, root-gates, buried stones under roots, and passages that open or close according to old patterns rather than command.

The borders of this influence are uncertain. Some notes suggest Morlaith once controlled a much larger swath of the Feywild, before [[Archfey Ethlenn]], the [[Cloudspinner]], [[Lord Umbraeth]], and other powers drove or crowded him back. His present domain is shrunken, but not weak in the places where it remains rooted.

Where Morlaith has receded, old gates and paths can remain half-awake. In such places the land feels less abandoned than inattentive, as if the power that once held the doors shut has turned elsewhere. The brambles may still remember how to close, the stones may still remember old crossings, and the paths may still test those who step inside.

## Feywild Politics

Morlaith stands outside the main politics of [[Twilight's Grace]], [[Twilight's Edge]], [[Duskmire]], and the [[Sunset Gate]]. He is not one of Ethlenn's elegant powers, not one of Umbraeth's hunters, and not a minor lord looking for protection. He is a remnant of an older pattern in the Feywild, and his presence keeps the Addermarian wilds from becoming merely another front in the conflict between courts.

That makes Morlaith a barrier to everyone. Umbraeth cannot simply swallow the old wilds. Serenveil cannot easily learn what lies beyond them. Ethlenn's influence does not translate cleanly into them. The fringes of Morlaith's country help preserve the Addermarian Feywild as the Wilds: contested, dangerous, old-fashioned, and resistant to being organized by any court.

Morlaith's power over crossings is especially important. He does not fully stop Umbraeth's agents from moving through the Feywild, but he appears to have ancient power over connections, doors, and weak places. When Morlaith maintains a passage, it can hold worse things back. When he withdraws attention, the same passage can become an opening for whatever is waiting nearby.

## DM notes

This version intentionally emphasizes Morlaith's vibe and setting role rather than retelling the Haldrenn incident. The event details are source context, not the main identity of the power.

Core sources treat Morlaith as deliberately unsettled. [[Addermarch Fey Politics]] frames him as an ancient entity, possibly older than [[Archfey Ethlenn]], and explicitly not a traditional archfey. The same note also names Morlaith as Grymholt, the Briarheart, Keeper of the Thornweald, and Lord of the Hidden Hollows.

Discord and DM material add several important design points:
- Addermarch should feel fey-heavy but unlike Tyrwingha: more fear, warning, and Germanic fairy-tale danger than gratitude toward a protector.
- Morlaith has an "Old God" or ancient forest entity feeling, and may once have ruled a much larger region before newer fey realms displaced him.
- Morlaith is agnostic to humans rather than actively mischievous. Humans are beneath him; the danger comes from old laws and broken supports, not personal malice.
- Morlaith's control of portals is ancient and unexplained. Umbraeth has reason to care about that power, but Morlaith is still not an Umbraeth ally.
- Morlaith and the Wilds should keep Umbraeth, Serenveil, and Ethlenn's lieutenants confined to the edges of the Addermarian Feywild.

The Cleenseau notes suggest that thorn-maze gates in the [[Cleenseau Region]] may once have been tied to Morlaith before [[Duskmire]] expanded, but those notes are speculative and should not be treated as settled public canon yet. They are useful for the recurring image of Morlaith's old passages as thorn mazes that test, confuse, and sometimes release travelers rather than simply devouring them.

Specific event context:
- In the Haldrenn arc, [[Duncan of Haldrenn]] disturbed Morlaith's realm while under the influence of the [[Serpentine Battleaxe]].
- The stolen heart of the Haldrenn Singing Tree, the [[Nightsong Roots]], the Hollow Man, [[Vaelithar]], and [[Liraene]] are all useful examples of Morlaith-adjacent hazards, but should not define the whole note.
- [[Caradoc]] traveled into Morlaith's realm as a young man and later watched for signs of trouble connected to him.

Source tensions to review:
- Some early adventure notes say Duncan stole "treasure" from Morlaith, while later session and timeline notes specify that he stole the Singing Tree's heart under the axe's influence.
- Notes vary on how far Morlaith's old influence once extended, especially toward Cleenseau and the Enst. Keep the current public wording broad unless the map is finalized.
- The term "Singing Tree" also appears in unrelated Fellburn/Oracle of Hope material and should not be conflated with the Haldrenn Singing Tree.
