# Brilliant Numbers

*Brilliant Numbers* is a Drankorian mathematical treatise associated with [[Menestheus]], a scholar of the [[Arithrimos Lamperum]]. The book introduces brilliant numbers: six-digit numbers that are each the product of two three-digit prime numbers.

Most of the known text is an index of several thousand brilliant numbers, arranged sequentially. Its introduction argues that, because such numbers are difficult to factor by hand, they can stand openly for particular codes while still deeply obscuring the information they represent.

Menestheus used the same method in his work reading maps of [[planar connections]]. In his explanation, groups of brilliant numbers can encode precise locations on a nested grid, allowing the [[Arithrimos Lamperum]] to hide maps of weak points between planes in plain sight.
