# The Shape of Possibility

*The Shape of Possibility* is a Drankorian metaphysical treatise associated with [[Menestheus]], a scholar of the [[Arithrimos Lamperum]]. The work combines magic, mathematics, and metaphysics in an attempt to describe possibility as a mathematical shape.

The known portions argue that every moment contains a finite number of possibilities, each with a shape that can be represented mathematically. These shapes recur in similar but unique variations, and the book considers whether a sufficiently complex model could describe every possible future outcome of a sequence of events at once.

The closing section turns the argument around: after an event has happened, possibility can be treated as singular, with the whole event existing simultaneously. The book uses a story as a metaphor, where the full story exists on the page at once but is experienced sequentially by a reader.
