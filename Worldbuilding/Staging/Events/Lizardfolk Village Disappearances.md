# Lizardfolk Village Disappearances

The Lizardfolk Village Disappearances were a series of vanishings among lizardfolk villages along the northwest coast and nearby river regions, beginning with [[Samso]]'s home village of [[Urkabi]] in DR 1737 and later striking additional settlements west of [[Voltara]], near [[Lake Valandros]], and in the wider Apporian and Kedron-region context. The disappearances were marked by empty villages, damaged local ecosystems, shadow-tainted creatures, and a recurring green sigil later linked to the [[Shemra Azem]], the [[Cleaver-Stone]], and [[Cairn Dor]].

The active raids appear to have been stopped or severely disrupted when the [[Silver Tempests]] shut down the Cleaver-Stone machinery on DR 1748-09-20. The trapped lizardfolk and other dreamers were not rescued until the party returned from Cairn Dor on DR 1752-06-29.

## First Known Disappearance: Urkabi

Samso was born in Urkabi, a lizardfolk village on the [[Zurrua]] in [[Apporia]]. In DR 1737, he returned home and found the village empty. The trees showed black rot, nearby streams were polluted, and a green sigil was fading at the site. Samso healed what he could of the damaged ecosystem, but he could not hear the ancestors and found no living villagers.

Urkabi established the pattern later seen elsewhere: the people vanished, the local environment was harmed, and the sigil remained as a magical trace. At the time, Samso had no explanation. His later story and the Silver Tempests' investigation eventually tied the event to raids for sleeping or dreaming captives.

## Voltara-Area Disappearance

On DR 1747-06-22, [[Iara]] approached Samso in Voltara and asked for help. A lizardfolk village west of Voltara had disappeared in a way that resembled Urkabi. On DR 1747-06-26, the party reached the empty village and encountered shadowy evil echoes of lizardfolk. They killed the leading creature but did not learn the underlying cause.

This encounter introduced the party to the immediate supernatural manifestation of the disappearances: not only absence, but shadow-tainted remnants or echoes where villagers had been. Later notes connect this to Shadowfold or Shadowfell magic, though the terminology shifts across sources.

## Widening Pattern

Later downtime and DM notes describe a broader pattern. Samso visited lizardfolk near Lake Valandros and learned that many villages had been hit. The clearest DM summary says ten villages had disappeared, with rare survivors, matching or similar sigils, and damaged local ecosystems that could heal over time. The pace of the disappearances seemed to be increasing.

In DR 1747-09, Samso researched the disappearances and the shadow lizards, connecting them to the Shadowfell or Shadowfolds. A shadow creature attacked him and warned him to stop asking questions. This made the disappearances an active threat rather than a cold mystery.

## Blue's Letter and the Umbral Covenant Trail

In DR 1748, [[Blue]] found a dead messenger carrying a letter from [[Lyra]] to Samso. Blue brought the letter to Samso in Voltara on DR 1748-09-17. The letter identified the green sigil as resembling a spell glyph and suggested it could be deconstructed into old Northros runes meaning "Sleep Nevermore."

The letter connected the sign to the [[Umbral Covenant]], the [[War of the Dark Rift|War of Dark Rift]], the [[War of the Severed Dreams|War of Severed Dreams]], [[Shemra Azem]], the Wolf Queen or Queen Eternal Waking, and old Apporian and Castrellan history. This shifted the investigation away from a local village mystery and toward an ancient magical system tied to sleep, dreams, and a Shadowfold domain.

## Return to Urkabi and Castrella

On DR 1748-09-17, the Silver Tempests visited Urkabi and began following the trail down the Zurrua. On DR 1748-09-18, they investigated [[Castrella]]. Local legends described [[Yeshara]] as a wolf queen or wolf-eyed queen who feared the dark and created the Shemra Azem as a sleepless army. Folk tales linked Yeshara, the Shemra Azem, and the old Apporian Shadow War to the War of Severed Dreams and the War of Dark Rift, while official histories did not fully agree.

The party also learned of a sleeping sickness killing people in the Kedron valley and of some villages vanishing. These details suggested that the same system that had taken lizardfolk villages could affect non-lizardfolk victims as well, either through raids, illness, or magical spillover.

## Cleaver-Stone and the Fort

On DR 1748-09-19, the party traveled upriver toward an old Drankorian fort. On DR 1748-09-20, they reached the fort and its Cleaver-Stone complex. A sealed door carried a curse against sleep, and the deeper site included an ancient village with wolf plinths and a chamber marked by the same green sigil seen at Samso's village.

The party encountered Shemra Azem who told them to take [[Mabist]] to Cairn Dor to join the dreamers. They threatened to make the party dreamers to help [[Yo'nari]]. Mabist was dragged through the portal but rescued. Samso killed a Shemra Azem lieutenant, and the party used Drankorian instructions to lock the portal and shut down the soul compass or related machinery.

This action appears to have stopped or sharply disrupted the raids. It did not free the people who had already been taken.

## Cairn Dor and the Dreamers

On DR 1748-10-14, the Silver Tempests reached Cairn Dor by Plane Shift. They found Yeshara, human prisoners, stillings, and barrows where sleepers were held as dreamers. Daylight from a driftglobe woke some dreamers, and when dreamers woke, linked Shemra Azem fell dead. This implied that at least some Shemra Azem were sustained by captive dreamers, though the exact mapping between soldiers and dreamers remains uncertain.

When the last dreamers woke and Yeshara was defeated, the party and the dreamers fell asleep and Cairn Dor entered a long stasis. [[Blue]], [[Adrik]], and Mabist slept from DR 1748-10-15 to DR 1752-06-27. Samso, transformed into an earth elemental, wandered for years before also sleeping. On DR 1752-06-28, the party and the lizardfolk dreamers woke. The native inhabitants of Cairn Dor still slumbered. On DR 1752-06-29, the party returned to Urkabi with the rescued lizardfolk, and Samso parted from his family and kin.

## DM-Facing Mechanism

DM background frames Cairn Dor as a Shadowfold domain connected to the Material Plane through the Cleaver-Stone. Yeshara and Yo'nari belonged to an ancient people whose history became entangled with the War of Severed Dreams, the War of Dark Rift, and Apporian or Castrellan memory. Yeshara feared dreams after Ilanar and built or used the Cleaver-Stone to sever people from the Plane of Consciousness. Instead, the rite created or opened Cairn Dor.

The Shemra Azem were sustained by mortal dreamers. The soul compass let Yeshara's forces locate targets over distance and raid them for dreamers. Lizardfolk were especially valuable or vulnerable because of their strong ties to ancestors and the Dreamworld. The green sigil marked this magic. Disabling the fort and its machinery stopped or slowed the raids but did not free captives already held in Cairn Dor.

This mechanism explains the recurring elements: empty settlements, dream-linked captives, shadow-tainted remains or echoes, ecosystem damage, and the old curse against sleep.

## Conflicts and Unresolved Details

- Older notes and early Samso material use different names or geography for Samso's home, including Yeraad, while current Arc 5 material uses Urkabi, the Zurrua, and Apporia. This note follows the current Arc 5 framing but does not erase the older conflict.
- Some older notes use "Shadowfell"; current cosmology and Cairn Dor material use [[Shadowfolds]]. The underlying phenomenon appears to be the same campaign mystery, but terminology should be regularized by a human.
- The exact number of vanished villages is not fixed in session-facing text. DM background says ten villages and rare survivors, while session recaps emphasize Urkabi, the Voltara-area village, Lake Valandros reports, and Kedron-region disappearances or sleeping sickness.
- Older campaign planning tried to connect the disappearances to the aboleth and the Suwi arc. The session summaries that resolved the Suwi arc do not clearly make that connection, so this note treats the aboleth link as unconfirmed or superseded.
- The War of Severed Dreams, War of Dark Rift, Yeshara, Yo'nari, and the Umbral Covenant have competing folk, official, and DM-note explanations. The current notes do not fully reconcile them.
- The fate of Cairn Dor's native sleeping inhabitants after the party's return is unresolved in this event note.
