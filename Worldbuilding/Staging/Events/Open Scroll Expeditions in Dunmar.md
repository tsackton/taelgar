# Open Scroll Expeditions in Dunmar

The Open Scroll Expeditions in Dunmar were a series of Chardonian scholarly, treasure-hunting, and artifact-recovery missions conducted in [[Dunmar]] before and during the early [[Chardon-Dunmar War]]. They were associated with the [[Society of the Open Scroll]], the [[Great Library|Great Library of Chardon]], private patrons, and, in several important cases, [[Fausto]] and the [[The Cleansed|Cleansed]].

The original staging note described this as a "stub note to move DM information on various open scroll missions from beginning of Dunmar campaign to" a dedicated event page.

## Background

The [[Society of the Open Scroll]] began as a Chardonian scholarly society dedicated to preserving magical knowledge after the Great War. By the eighteenth century DR, it had developed a more active expeditionary wing: practical historians, explorers, adventurers, artifact collectors, and patrons who treated Dunmar and the former lands of Drankor as places where lost knowledge could be recovered.

Chardonian ideology also shaped the expeditions. Some Society members, including [[Vola]], believed Chardon was the rightful successor to the [[Drankorian Empire]] and therefore had a duty or right to recover Drankorian knowledge. This view sat uneasily beside Dunmari claims to their own ruins, local fears of dangerous magic, and the Chardonian tendency to convert "preservation" into extraction.

The political frame changed with the [[Nayahar Articles]]. According to the war chronology, the Society privately sent adventurers and artifact collectors into Dunmar as early as DR 1743. On DR 1747-05-28, the Nayahar Articles gave Chardonian scholarly societies formal permission to extract and return artifacts found on Dunmari land to Chardon. This date is also treated as the conventional beginning of the Chardon-Dunmar War. Later evidence suggests Fausto and the Cleansed were behind the agreements and used the resulting permissions to move people, knowledge, and dangerous artifacts into Chardonian hands.

## Public Scholarship and Private Patronage

The expedition network did not have a single clean command structure. Sources variously describe Society expeditions, Great Library expeditions, University-adjacent scholars, and privately funded treasure hunts. [[Kaeso]] remembered the Society as a learned debating group without a strict hierarchy, while later field evidence shows a much more aggressive expeditionary culture.

Rich patrons funded many missions. Fausto was especially important. He funded or likely funded several Dunmari expeditions, including the Stormcaller mission involving [[Dee Wildcloak]], [[Dain Goldhammer]], and [[Alban]], the work of [[Servius]], and probably expeditions involving [[Kadmos]] and [[Casian]]. His patronage let Cleansed objectives hide inside Society exploration, ordinary scholarly travel, and treasure hunting.

The Great Library and Society remained distinct in the notes, but their personnel and reputations overlapped heavily. The Magistros later blamed both the University of Chardon and the Society for failing to notice Fausto's rise and Cleansed infiltration.

## Vola, Kadmos, and Raven's Hold

One major expedition left Chardon in late DR 1747. Vola traveled with [[Kadmos]], [[Marcella]], [[Hektor]], [[Antonia]], and [[Casian]]. The group left Chardon by sea on DR 1747-11-20, reached [[Nayahar]] on DR 1747-12-26, left Nayahar on DR 1748-01-25, reached [[Askandi]] on DR 1748-03-01, and departed for [[Raven's Hold]] on DR 1748-03-05.

The group reached Raven's Hold on DR 1748-03-31. Vola was captured on DR 1748-04-02 and rescued by the Dunmar Fellowship on DR 1748-04-12. She identified herself as a Society member seeking old artifacts of Drankor and argued that the Society was a better steward than treasure seekers like [[Agata Dustmother]]. The party's conversations with her raised one of the central questions of the early campaign: whether dangerous artifacts should be preserved, removed to Chardon, destroyed, or left in Dunmar.

Vola returned to Chardon on DR 1748-06-03 and reported to [[Agon]]. This report appears to have brought the Dunmar Fellowship to the attention of Agon and Fausto by mid-June. Later DM notes place Vola's cleansing by Fausto or Agon around DR 1748-09-10.

Kadmos's strand of the same expedition became more openly hostile. Kadmos was both a Society figure and a Cleansed cultist. When the Dunmar Fellowship later met him, Marcella, Casian, Hektor, and Antonia on the Tokra-Darba road, Kadmos tried to buy or trade for Drankorian magic and used charm magic. Marcella was uneasy with the party and appeared to want escape from Kadmos's control or influence.

## Stormcaller Tower and the Fausto-Funded Mission

A second major expedition involved [[Arcus]], [[Servius]], [[Dee Wildcloak]], [[Dain Goldhammer]], and [[Alban]]. They left Chardon on DR 1747-12-23 on a Society-linked mission funded by Fausto and reached [[Songara]] on DR 1748-02-02. The group split after disagreements. Arcus and Servius went separate ways from Dee, Dain, and Alban.

Dee, Dain, and Alban found [[Stormcaller Tower]] on DR 1748-03-13. On DR 1748-03-18, they looted the tower, taking [[Hralgar's Eyes]] and the Binding Stones. Their actions partly awakened [[Hralgar]], and Alban was killed. Dee and Dain returned to Chardon on DR 1748-04-22 and delivered the items to Fausto.

The Dunmar Fellowship later learned these details in Chardon. Dee and Dain described Fausto's role as patron, and Vola explained that rich patrons were funding many such treasure-hunting expeditions. Dee and Dain were preparing to go to [[Illoria]] on another Fausto mission to aid Servius.

Arcus's path ended separately. He passed through [[Karawa]] in late February or early March and was later petrified in a Dunmari fort at [[Gomat]]. Arcus is described as a Society member, and DM notes identify him as connected to the Cleansed inner circle. Servius continued as a wandering scholar and historian funded by Fausto; he found the [[Archives Letter]] in the [[Archives|Tokra Archives]], which concerned a Great War-era Kharsan treasury being moved through Darba, Chardon, and Illoria.

The consequences of the Stormcaller mission continued far beyond the initial expedition. The removal of Hralgar's artifacts helped awaken the tower's danger, and the Dunmar Fellowship eventually freed Hralgar and destroyed Stormcaller Tower in DR 1748.

## Other Expeditionary Activity

The notes indicate that by late DR 1747 several major Society expeditions were active in Dunmar, including some secretly run for Cleansed purposes. The full roster is not cleanly mapped.

[[Marcella]] told the party that the Society was open to all and that, in Chardon, they should ask after [[Crispus]], a senior archivist in the Great Library responsible for many artifact-collection expeditions. [[Johar]] mentioned rumors that Karnas had made a deal with Chardon giving access to treasures lost in Nashtkar in exchange for magical aid. This matches the political role of the Nayahar Articles, though from a Dunmari perspective it looked like imperial access bought through wartime desperation.

Later Chardonian activity in [[Xurkhaz]] involved [[Valius]], [[Vargus]], [[Evelina]], and [[Roman]]. These figures had links to the Chardonian Legion and, in some cases, the Society of the Open Scroll. Their missions may belong to the same wider pattern of scholarly cover, military intelligence, and Cleansed infiltration, but the current staging note treats them as adjacent rather than core examples because the early expedition material is more explicit.

## Consequences

The Open Scroll expeditions helped turn antiquarian interest into war politics. They moved artifacts, maps, letters, and rumors from Dunmar toward Chardon. They also created direct contact between the Dunmar Fellowship and the Chardonian networks that later became central to the war: Vola, Kadmos, Marcella, Casian, Fausto, Servius, Dee, Dain, Arcus, and Agon.

The expeditions also exposed the ambiguity of Chardonian "preservation." Some Society members sincerely wanted dangerous knowledge protected from misuse. Others treated Dunmar as a storehouse for Drankorian inheritance. Fausto used that ambiguity to fund artifact recovery, identify dangerous sites, and feed Cleansed plans while appearing to support scholarship.

In hindsight, the expeditions were one of the soft openings of the Chardon-Dunmar War. The Nayahar Articles legalized extraction, the Society normalized it, and private patrons weaponized it.

## Conflicts and Unresolved Details

- The Society of the Open Scroll is not the same as the Great Library, the University of Chardon, or the Cleansed, but the notes often blur their personnel, funding, and public reputation.
- Some expeditions were legitimate Society work, some were private treasure hunts, and some were Cleansed operations under scholarly cover. The boundary is not always identifiable from current notes.
- Fausto's funding is explicit for some missions and inferred or rumored for others. The note preserves "probably" or "likely" where the sources do.
- Vola's expedition, Kadmos's group, and Casian's role overlap but are not always described with the same emphasis. Vola appears sincere in some early sessions; Kadmos is clearly Cleansed; Marcella appears trapped or uncomfortable; Casian's exact degree of complicity is less clear.
- The Nayahar Articles gave legal permission, but the later discovery of Cleansed manipulation makes the agreement politically compromised. The current notes do not resolve how much Karnas understood when he accepted the arrangement.
