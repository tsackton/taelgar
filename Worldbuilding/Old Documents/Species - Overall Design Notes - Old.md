Three rough categories of races:
 
1. The Eldest races, the three original creations: Elves, Goliath, and Lizardfolk. These should feel a little stereotyped as to what classes they are good at. Goliath are warriors; Lizardfolk are nature-focused; elves are magical and graceful.
    1. Goliath traits: should encourage martials, or martial/casters using wisdom like melee clerics. Magic using goliath should tend towards cleric/druid, and as martials they should be optimized for strength-based attacks. Should have an option to make a strength monk more viable with an unarmored defense tweak (use STR instead of DEX for AC). Racial magic subclass would probably be a monk half caster focused on self buffs, or specialized cleric domain, or a variant warlock that is hexblade-y but self-buff focused and casting off WIS. Should feel non-optimized in DEX, CHA, INT based classes.
    2. Lizardfolk traits: should encourage WIS classes, especially druids and to a lesser extent rangers. Racial magic is focused on prophecy, divination, spirits. Magic using lizardfolk should strongly trend towards druid or cleric, and racial magic should have a divination/spirits/dreamworld/prophecy flavor.

Elves are tricky: they should excel as arcane casters in particular; probably reasonable to have the high elves as wizards (+2 INT) and the halfborn as bards/sorcerers/warlocks (+2 CHA), perhaps with some variation between what we called "high Tayledras" and "regular Tayledras". Wood elves are tricky, I think they should support more melee / Dex archetypes. Elves in Taelgar are the first of the firstborn, they are strong and powerful, kind of like Tolkien elves in flavor in some ways. But I don't want them to be too versatile as that is also an advantage reserved for dwarves/halfings/humans, the younger races. I also don't necessarily want to pigeonhole elves by subraces in the traditional sense, e.g. wood elves and high elves don't necessarily live apart etc. Probably the solution is to go more like dwarves, with wood vs high being which firstborn you favor, and not really 'subraces'. Probably also need a third CHA-based subtype that would be the 'force of personality' elves and make for a powerful sorcerer or bard.
