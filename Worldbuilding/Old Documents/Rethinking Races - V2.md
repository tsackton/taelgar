Feel like post-Tasha's and with 2 years testing/experience I have a much better handle on good race design for D&D now, and also a much better sense of Taelgar lore.
 
A few thoughts. First, just scrap all weird ability score nonsense. Every race gets either three +1 or two +1 depending on strength of the racial package, and you can never increase a single ability score more than +2 with these racial ASIs. There is no real benefit / goal here.
 
Second, scrap subraces. They don't add anything to the game IMO. Instead, have each race offer a choice of options. More interesting, more flexible, and no need for weird lore about why there are multiple different kinds of elves which otherwise doesn't fit into the lore.
 
Third, don't worry about balance so much. No race should have a build-defining trait, but also there should be no reason to try to make everything exactly equal, just make them all flavorful and interesting without being flat/boring.
 
## Categories of Races
 
### Elder Races
 
These are elves, lizardfolk, dwarves, halflings, and stoneborn. They are defined by the racial magic from their connection to the firstborn, as follows:
 
Elves: innately magical, should focus on at will rituals and cantrips or cantrip-like effects, no use per day stuff
 
Stoneborn: disciplines that strengthen mind and body, thematically appropriately to have a use per day or use per short rest when it makes sense
 
Lizardfolk: spiritual connection / augury, can be more use per day stuff but should focus on divination, nature spirits, etc
 
Dwarves: runes, working on a charge system
 
Halflings: racial telepathy and racial memory
