    My D&D game this afternoon is the showdown between the PCs and Umbraeth's attempt to permanantly reopen the fey gate in the southern Cleenseau wood.
    
    Metaphysic idea (which will likely not come up in play but could drive some clues)...
    
    The fey gate has been established (on the Duskmire side) as a woody, thorny maze with a dense bramble in the middle that leads into Taelgar (on the Taelgar side, the vibe is similar - a dense bramble that you get lost in where you appear in the Feywild)

    Over the many years since Morlaith has retreated, this gate has flickered and foundered, working sometimes, not working other times, as Morlaith is no longer consciously maintaining it. 
    
    In the late 1719/early 1720 the lich provided (via some undefined powerful magic, probably a chaylte artifact of some type) a stablizing force - something very arcane and technical - which kept the gate open (in exchange for Umbraeth's aid in his eventual plan to try to get Tyrwingha and Sembara to fight)
    
    But then the Night Queen folks came close to finding out the lich, and he withdrew support from Umbraeth to dedicate his energies much more directly to trying to kill the threat of the night queen paladins and such (which ultimately backfired, but...)
    
    This left the gate closed... but Umbraeth had since discovered a ritual he could perform - using Ethlenn's essence in the form of the Queen of Tyrwingha's blood - that would stablize the gate.
