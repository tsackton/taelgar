# The Bek'eni
*(BEHK-en-ee)*
>[!info]+ Information  
> One of the the [[Elderwood Tribes|Elderwood Tribes of the Deno'qai]]

The Bek'eni are one of the major [[Deno'qai]] tribes based in the [[Elderwood]]. Historically, they were largely hunters, foresters, and trappers, extensively trading and intermarrying with the [[Baz'aku]], but under the sway of [[Mezzar]] they became deeply isolationist and mistrusting. Since the unmasking of [[Mezzar]] as the green dragon [[Mezzar|Grimbaskal]], and his death at the hands of the [[Dunmar Fellowship]], the Bek'eni have struggled to recover. 

## Territory and Settlements 

The Bek'eni occupy numerous small villages and family hamlets scattered across the interior of the [[Elderwood]], with the densest settlements north and east of the [[Kayan]]. The core of Bek'eni territory is composed of fifteen or so small villages, most between fifty and one hundred people, stretching east along small, unmarked streams flowing into the [[Kayan]]. 

During the days of [[Mezzar]]'s rule, the Bek'eni paid a high price in tribute, sending the bulk of their wealth and even a fair portion of their food to the dragon's hoard. Since Grimbaskal's death, some villages have recovered, but the Bek'eni remain poor, with few luxuries, problems with hunger, and dwellings in poor repair. 

## Local Culture 

The Bek'eni preserve a proud oral tradition that holds that their tribe long stood closest to the elves of [[Ainumarya]], that in the ancient days they dwelt in friendship and commerce with the great elven cities of the forest. Their myths and tales speak of hidden elven ruins and old scars in the land where the forest does not return. These tales are old, predating [[Mezzar]], and many are recorded by [[Gnaeus]] in [[On the Lost People of the Forests]].

Under [[Mezzar]]'s rule, the Bek'eni grew deeply isolationist and distrustful of outsiders. Bek'eni rumor would cast the [[Baz'aku]] as collaborators who have turned away from tradition, and the deep-forest [[Te'kula]] as a people under a malign influence. 

Though many Bek'eni are still traditionalists with a deep appreciation for ancient culture, the upheavals of [[Mezzar]]'s betrayal have caused much doubt and angst among some in the tribe.  

## Recent History

For decades, the Bek'eni followed the green dragon [[Mezzar|Grimbaskal]], who posed as an elf under the name [[Mezzar]]. In 1748 DR, the elf called [[Mezzar]] was unmasked at a Deno'qai god tree as the green dragon [[Mezzar|Grimbaskal]] by the actions of the [[Dunmar Fellowship]]. Grimbaskal was slain soon after, and the corruption that had spread through the southern [[Elderwood]] began to recede. The Bek'eni now reckon with the losses and divisions left behind by Mezzar's rule, including the disappearance of warriors taken into his service and the collapse of village authority built around him.

## Interactions with Grimbaskal
 
Grimbaskal goes among the Bek'eni as an elf named Mezzar, a survivor of the Blood Years who needs the help of the Bek'eni to go north and drive the evil out of his ancestral home. Takes warriors, trains them, they don't come back - but seen as an honor and Mezzar talks of their great deeds in the north.
 
This group is most deeply in Grimbaskal's sway, almost worshipping Mezzar. This should feel a bit off to the party so play it up.

This tribe has fallen fully under the sway of Grimbaskal, who goes by Mezzar among them, who they believe is an elf, a survivor of the Blood Years, who has found their tribe and seeks to advise them on how to rebuild and grow. He takes tribute - things of great beauty, things that express the spirit of the forest - and asks only to let the people be his eyes and ears, let him see through them at times, to keep watch. Sometimes he will use this as an opportunity to control people and make them kill themselves, or do something foolish/dangerous, or just cause chaos. He takes warriors to train - although none come back, he says they are needed to protect the tribe. In return he animates the spirits of the trees (actually, corrupts treants) and sends them to guard to Bek'eni.

The Bek'eni have become, under Grimbaskal's influence, extremely isolationist. Often attack intruders in their territory on sight, and bring the captives to Grimbaskal to deal with (Mezzar to them).

There are of course some in the tribe who feel things are going in the wrong direction, but it is hard to argue with an elf who seems to have so much power, and with the fact that Bek'eni villages have not been attacked since the alliance with Mezzar started.

There are some that talk about the Baz'aku as collaborators with Chardon, and there is growing talk of an attack.

They believe the Te'kula to be corrupted by evil, hiding in fear of some monster that in fact controls them.
 
## Interactions with the Party
 
Most likely way to end up among the Bek'eni is to encounter a war party and surrender/go with them.
 
The Bek'eni will treat Delwath as an honored guest (although not let him leave until Mezzar arrives to meet him). Others will be kept tied up / contained, although not necessarily extremely tightly / well-guarded as will by default trust their parole.
 
## Plot Points
 
If they end up here, may just stay to meet Mezzar - if they do, he will tell them to stay for a few days and he will take them deeper in the woods when returns from an errand (but again, this should feel weird/off). Give them every opportunity to escape the Bek'eni without being dragged back to Mezzar's / Grimbaskal's lair, where things will get harder to escape.
 
However, should be one NPC, perhaps the Godcaller (or even better, the former Godcaller who has been "deposed" in favor of someone who now effectively worships Mezzar), who will warn them and could help them escape if needed, and even be their guide to the Te'kula.
