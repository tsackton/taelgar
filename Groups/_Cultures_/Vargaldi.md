# Vargaldi
>[!info]+ Information  
> A [[~Northerner~]] culture based in [[Northern Green Sea]]

The Vargald, or Vargaldi, are an ancient [[~Northerner~]] culture, sharing a distant ancestry with the [[Deno'qai]] and the [[Zimka]]. The Vargaldi worship the [[Zariya]], small gods that share many similarities with other [[Northern Folk Religions]] such as the [[Tanshi]] and the [[Kestavo]], and speak a language related to [[Languages#~Old Zimkovan~|Old Zimkovan]], but with many [[Languages#Eastros|Eastros]] loan words. Long ago, the ancestors of the Vargald migrated from the west, moving across the [[Svinjo Mountains]], to settle in the [[~Northern Boreal Forest~|vast interior forests]] of the [[Northern Green Sea]], where the Vargaldi have lived ever since. 

With the threat of dragons to the north, and other dangers such as ice worms ever-present in the forests, some Vargald are willing to accept the conditions required for protection from the frost giants of [[Kaldhalla]] or the wizards of [[Ursk]], and a handful of settlements exist along the northwestern borders of Ursk, in particular. Many Vargald have also settled in [[Ursk]], accepting the limitations of the *krev* rite in exchange for safety. 

Those who still eke out a living in the forests, however, rarely build permanent settlements due to the danger of dragons. However, in the winter, as the ground freezes and the summer muds solidify, dispersed bands of a few dozen people often gather in defensible camps, usually nestled in dense forests where attack from the air is more difficult, to guard against the ever-present danger of dragons. 

In addition to hunting and animal herding, many Vargald make a good living trading monster parts to the markets of [[Ursk]], especially at [[Zvervinka]]; others scrape by guiding hunting parties and other adventurers seeking fame, glory, and riches in the wastes. 

The Vargald are particularly known for their unusual religion, worshipping what they call the [[Zariya]] spirits,  and manifesting for many of them as an ability to shape shift into animal spirits, or to call forth their animal protector as a guide and companion. Their connection to these animal guides is central to their culture and art.

The Vargald are also unusual in being a mixed species group. Although [[Humans|humans]] are the most numerous among the Vargald, many bands also have [[Halflings|halfling]] and [[Stoneborn|stoneborn]] that live together in a united community and, uniquely, share the same religion and magic. 

## Names

Although the Vargaldi speak a language related to [[Languages#~Old Zimkovan~|Old Zimkovan]], it has many loan words from [[Languages#Eastros|Eastros]], and many Vargald names sound rather Eastros in flavor. Common male names include Radovan, Torsetn, Rurik, Sindri, Goran; common female names include Mila, Runa, Danica, Zora, Vesna.

### History

Settled here for a long time. Before the Great War, built more villages, less nomadic, would have relied on giants for protection. 

More recently, some have turned to Ursk for protection, others hide and move quietly through the woods relying on their animal magic. 

## Religion

Worship the Zariya (za-REE-ya), the animal spirits. Mix up Philip Pullman daemons, tanshi/kestavo ideas of small gods, shapeshifting. 

## Vargald Camps and Settlements

1. **Hidden and Mobile:** The Vargald camps are designed to be temporary and easily movable. They blend seamlessly with the surrounding environment, using natural camouflage like snow-covered tents, structures built into the sides of hills, or hidden among thickets of dense, evergreen foliage.

2. **Watchtowers and Lookouts:** Given the threats they face, the Vargald maintain a network of hidden watchtowers and lookout points. These are often manned by their best scouts, who can transform into birds or other creatures with keen senses to detect approaching danger.

3. **Underground Retreats:** In areas where the danger is particularly high, the Vargald might have underground retreats or caves, masked by illusions or natural formations, providing additional safety against larger predators.

4. **Natural Traps and Defenses:** The paths leading to their camps are laced with traps and natural defenses, deterring larger creatures and confusing those unfamiliar with the terrain.

## Vargald Culture and Society

1. **Animal Spirit Worship:** Central to their culture is the worship of animal spirits. This is reflected in their art, storytelling, and rituals. They believe that their ability to transform is a gift from these spirits, and they hold regular ceremonies to honor them.

2. **Shape-shifting as a Way of Life:** Shape-shifting is not just a skill but a way of life. The Vargald believe in living in harmony with their animal forms, often taking on roles in the community that match the characteristics of their animal counterparts.
    
3. **Egalitarian Society:** Their society is egalitarian, with leadership roles often shifting based on the situation and the individual's skills. This fluid structure helps them adapt quickly to changing circumstances.

4. **Skilled Craftsmen and Hunters:** They are excellent craftsmen, creating tools, clothing, and structures that are both functional and harmonious with nature. Their hunting skills are unparalleled, using their shape-shifting abilities to track and hunt without disturbing the natural balance.

5. **Oral Tradition and Storytelling:** Given their secretive lifestyle, the Vargald rely heavily on oral tradition to pass down knowledge and history. Storytelling is a revered art, often accompanied by dances and shape-shifting demonstrations.

6. **Education and Training:** Young Vargald are trained from an early age in the arts of survival, shape-shifting, and reverence for the natural world. This education ensures that every member of the community can contribute and defend the group.

7. **Trade and Relations:** While secretive, they might occasionally trade with trusted outsiders, exchanging furs, crafted goods, or rare herbs for items they cannot produce themselves.

8. **Respect for Nature and Balance:** Above all, the Vargald live by a code that emphasizes balance with nature. They take only what they need and always give back to the land, maintaining a deep respect for the ecosystem they inhabit.

## Names

Male

1. **Bjornar** - Scandinavian origin, meaning "bear".
2. **Radovan** - Slavic origin, meaning "happy, willing".
3. **Leif** - Old Norse, meaning "descendant, heir".
4. **Vilko** - Derived from Slavic "vlk", meaning "wolf".
5. **Torsten** - Scandinavian origin, meaning "Thor's stone".
6. **Rurik** - Old Norse, a historical Varangian name.
7. **Sindri** - Old Norse, possibly meaning "small" but also the name of a mythical dwarf.
8. **Kazimir** - Slavic origin, meaning "destroyer of peace".
9. **Haldor** - Old Norse, meaning "Thor's rock".
10. **Goran** - Slavic origin, meaning "mountain man" or "highlander".

Female

1. **Freja** - Old Norse, associated with love, beauty, and fertility.
2. **Mila** - Slavic origin, meaning "gracious, dear".
3. **Astrid** - Scandinavian origin, meaning "divinely beautiful".
4. **Zora** - Slavic origin, meaning "dawn".
5. **Eir** - Old Norse, the name of a goddess or valkyrie associated with medical skill.
6. **Lada** - Slavic mythology, a goddess associated with beauty and fertility.
7. **Runa** - Old Norse, meaning "secret lore".
8. **Vesna** - Slavic origin, the name of a goddess associated with spring.
9. **Sigrun** - Old Norse, meaning "victory rune".
10. **Danica** - Slavic origin, meaning "morning star".
