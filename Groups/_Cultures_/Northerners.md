# Northerners

The Northerners are a broad family of peoples descended from the [[Historical Framework#Migrations and Emigration|first major migration]] out of [[Hkar]]. The best-preserved Northerner traditions survive among the [[Deno'qai]], the older highland strands of the [[Zimka]], and the [[Vargaldi]]. Northern religious ideas also shaped later cultures, including regional forms of [[Mos Numena]] such as [[Mawaran Religion|Mawaran religion]]. Though Northerner-descended cultures are diverse, they tend to speak languages derived from the original [[Languages#Northros Language Family|Northros]] tongue of the first migrants, and share a set of [[Northern Folk Religions|religious beliefs]] centered on intercession by small gods with the [[Divine Presence]].

## Nomenclature

"Northerner" is a later exonym for these early migrants and their descendants. "Northlander" appears as another exonym in some western and Drankorian-influenced contexts. [[Languages#Northros Language Family|Northros]] is a Drankorian scholarly term for the related language family, not a native ethnic name. No shared endonym is recorded, and it is not known what the original Northerner peoples called themselves. The surviving descendant peoples use their own names, including [[Deno'qai]], [[Zimka]], and [[Vargaldi]].

## Origins, Migration, and Settlement

The Northerners left [[Hkar]] before the rise of the later urban, arcane, and pantheonic culture that produced [[Mos Numena]] and [[Drankorian Empire|Drankor]]. Their traditions preserve an older religious pattern centered on small local gods who intercede with the [[Divine Presence]]. They settled first along the western coast of Taelgar, spread northward along the western side of the [[Sentinel Range|Sentinels]], and later crossed the mountains into the highlands and forests of the north and east. By the time of [[The Downfall]], Northerner-descended peoples lived across parts of the west coast, the far north, and the highlands around the Sentinels. 

After leaving Hkar, the Northerners had little sustained contact with their ancestral homeland. Their cultures developed in the west and north of Taelgar, often in marginal lands and on the fringes of regions dominated by older non-human peoples.

## Religion
_See more: [[Northern Folk Religions]]_

Northerner religion is centered on a set of intercessory traditions and beliefs: small, local gods carry prayers to the [[Divine Presence]], the distant creator and source of divine power. The small gods are not omnipresent, and their attention must be drawn by the correct rite, place, sacrifice, or custom. The major known branches of Northerner religion are:

- [[Tanshi Worship]], practiced among the [[Deno'qai]], in which offerings call the attention of the [[Tanshi|tanshi]].
- [[Kestavo]], practiced among the [[Zimka]] and their neighbors, centered on place-bound shrine spirits who carry prayers to [[Divine Presence|Ako]].
- [[Zariya]], practiced among the [[Vargaldi]], centered on animal spirits and the animal souls associated with Vargaldi magic.

Northern funerary traditions commonly treat the small gods as guides for the soul through the [[Land of the Dead]], though the rites differ greatly between descendant cultures.

## Language

The Northerner languages were grouped by Drankorian scholars as the [[Languages#Northros Language Family|Northros Language Family]]. Known Northros or Northros-derived languages include [[Languages#Deno'qai|Deno'qai]], [[Languages#~Old Zimkovan~|Old Zimkovan]], [[Languages#Vargaldi|Vargaldi]], and [[Languages#Mawaran|Mawaran]]. Several later languages preserve mixed inheritances. Modern [[Languages#Zimkovan|Zimkovan]] descends from an Eastros root but carries older northern influence, while Vargaldi remains Northros in structure but has many [[Languages#Eastros|Eastros]] loan words.

## Northerner Cultures

The [[Deno'qai]] are an ancient Northerner culture of the western forests and foothills of the [[Sentinel Range|Sentinels]], closely associated with [[Tanshi Worship]].

The [[Zimka]] include an older highland strand that preserves Northerner religion and language, and a broader lowland strand shaped by contact with mariners, river traders, [[Drankorian Empire|Drankor]], and [[Sembara]].

The [[Vargaldi]] are a Northerner-descended people of the [[Northern Green Sea]] and the great northern forests. They worship the [[Zariya]], speak a Northros language with Eastros influence, and are closely associated with animal-spirit magic.

### Unresolved Points

Unresolved or uncertain points:

- The exact first landing point on the west coast is deliberately undefined. Older material fixed the landing in Illoria, but current framework language keeps this broader.
- No shared native endonym is currently established. This note keeps "Northerner", "Northlander", and "Northros" as exonyms.
- The earliest shared name for the [[Divine Presence]], if one existed before Anida, Ako, and later regional names, is unknown.
- The degree of influence from elves, lizardfolk, dwarves, stoneborn, fey, and local sacred geography remains underdeveloped.
- The role of ancestor or culture-hero intercession (ala Mawar) in the earliest northern tradition is unclear; it may be original, regional, or a later blended development.
- Mawar should be treated in visible text as influenced by northern intercessory traditions, not as a settled Northerner-descended culture, until further details are decided.

### AI Research Note
*2026-05-20: Evidence-based facts and open issues gathered before revision.*

Scope:
- This note is best understood as a broad cultural-family page for the first major human migration out of [[Hkar]], not as a note for a single polity or one uniform ethnic group.
- "Northerner" is an exonym/convenience term in later scholarship. Northros is likewise a Drankorian scholarly label for the related language family. "Northlander" appears in some Drankorian-era material as another exonym.

Core migration facts:
- The strongest framework sources identify these peoples as the first major migration from [[Hkar]], leaving around CY 2500, peaking around CY 2800, and having little sustained contact with Hkar after departure. Sources: [[Historical Framework]], [[Human Cultures of Taelgar]].
- They left before Hkar's later urban, arcane, Mos Numena-dominated culture became dominant. Their traditions belong to the older small-gods/intercessory religious phase rather than later pantheonic religion. Sources: [[Human Cultures of Taelgar]], [[Religious History of Taelgar]].
- Their route is broadly: first settlement somewhere on the west coast of Taelgar, then spread north along the western side of the [[Sentinel Range|Sentinels]], then eventual crossings of the Sentinels in the far north and expansion eastward. The exact first landing point is intentionally undefined; older material that fixed the landing in Illoria has been removed in favor of a broader "west coast" statement. Sources: [[Historical Framework]], [[West Coast History Framework]], [[Human Cultures - Discussions]].
- By the time of [[The Downfall]], they are scattered along the west coast and far north, mostly isolated from Hkar, following [[Northern Folk Religions]]. They are the ancestors of the [[Deno'qai]], [[Zimka]], [[Vargaldi]], and similar peoples, but the recognizable modern cultures may not yet exist in their later form. They had not penetrated into the [[Green Sea]] outside the [[Western Gulf]]. Source: [[Historical Framework]].
- In [[Greater Sembara]], later Northerner migrants crossed from the west over the Sentinels and met westward-moving [[~Mariners~]]. Their mixing helped produce the Zimka and Tollish strands. Sources: [[Greater Sembara]], [[Humanity in Greater Sembara]], [[History of Greater Sembara]].

Culture and religion:
- The shared religious core is intercession: small, local gods carry prayers to the [[Divine Presence]]. These small gods are limited, local, and not omniscient; their attention must be drawn by ritual, sacrifice, place, or other specific practice. Sources: [[Northern Folk Religions]], [[Divine Presence]], [[Religious History of Taelgar]].
- Surviving branches differ significantly:
  - [[Tanshi Worship]] among the [[Deno'qai]] calls the tanshi by appropriate sacrifice; the Divine Presence is called [[Divine Presence|Anida]], "the First and the Last". Sources: [[Tanshi Worship]], [[Tanshi]], [[Deno'qai]], [[Divine Presence]].
  - The [[Kestavo]] tradition among the [[Zimka]] centers on place-bound shrine spirits who carry prayers to [[Divine Presence|Ako]]. Sources: [[Kestavo]], [[Kestavan Funerary Traditions]], [[Zimka]], [[Divine Presence]].
  - The [[Zariya]] tradition among the [[Vargaldi]] centers on animal spirits, tied both to small gods and to the animal souls/manifestations of Vargaldi people. Source: [[Zariya]], [[Vargaldi]].
- Funerary traditions appear to share the broad northern idea that a small god must find and guide the soul through the [[Land of the Dead]], but specific details vary by tradition. The kestavo tradition strongly emphasizes burial near a sacred place; Deno'qai practice likely relies more on sacrifice to draw the tanshi. Sources: [[Traditions around Death]], [[Kestavan Funerary Traditions]].
- Deno'qai practice has no temples in the sense of gods' permanent houses, but Deno'qai villages often have ritual spaces, altars, or tanshi trees. Source: [[Tanshi Worship]].

Language:
- The related languages are grouped as the [[Languages#Northros Language Family|Northros Language Family]], a Drankorian scholarly category for languages descended from the first migrants from Hkar. Sources: [[Languages]], [[Language - Discussions]].
- Known or likely Northros languages include [[Languages#~Old Zimkovan~|Old Zimkovan]], [[Languages#Deno'qai|Deno'qai]], [[Languages#Vargaldi|Vargaldi]], and [[Languages#Mawaran|Mawaran]]. The Mawaran case is unsettled. Source: [[Languages]].
- Northros languages are modeled with Semitic real-world analogues in development notes, while later Eastros and Drankorian influences explain mixed linguistic profiles such as Zimkovan and Vargaldi. Sources: [[Languages]], [[Language - Discussions]], [[Discord Chat - Languages and Humanity]].

Descendant and influenced cultures:
- [[Deno'qai]] are an ancient human culture of the western forests and foothills of the Sentinels, identified with the old migrations from Hkar and [[Tanshi Worship]]. Source: [[Deno'qai]].
- [[Zimka]] are a shared identity with at least two strands: an older highland/Old Zimka strand preserving older northern ways and Old Zimkovan, and a broader lowland strand shaped by the Sembaran mixing pot, river traders, Drankor, and Sembara. Sources: [[Zimka]], [[Humanity in Greater Sembara]], [[Languages]].
- [[Vargaldi]] are an ancient Northerner culture of the [[Northern Green Sea]], sharing distant ancestry with Deno'qai and Zimka, worshipping the [[Zariya]], and speaking a Northros language with many Eastros loan words. Source: [[Vargaldi]].
- [[Mawar Confederacy|Mawar]] preserves northern influence in language and in the saintly/intercessory structure of its local [[Mos Numena]], but its actual cultural origin is disputed. Public Mawar text traces ancestry to the first peoples from Hkar; background discussion explicitly says Mawar has been a poor fit for a Northerner origin and should remain historically open. Sources: [[Mawar Confederacy]], [[Mawaran Religion]], [[Human Cultures - Discussions]], [[Northwest Coast]].
- Western and northern Drankorian-era realms may have included assimilated Northerner/Northlander populations, with old small-gods traditions suppressed or absorbed into [[Mos Numena]]. Sources: [[Drankorian Empire]], [[West Coast History Framework]], [[Human Cultures of Taelgar]].

Contradictions and weak points:
- The term "Northerner" implies a single people, but the evidence supports a broad migration family whose later branches diverged substantially.
- The timing of first departure from Hkar, around CY 2500, and first arrival in Greater Sembara, roughly CY 3450-3650 in one source, should be reconciled by treating Greater Sembara as a later eastward phase, not the beginning of the migration.
- Zimka should not be described simply as a pure Northerner culture. Old Zimka are the cleaner northern strand; most modern Zimka are mixed culturally and linguistically.
- Mawar should be listed as influenced by northern intercessory traditions unless/until its origin is settled.
- Deno'qai "no temples" language needs nuance because ritual spaces, altars, and tanshi trees exist.
