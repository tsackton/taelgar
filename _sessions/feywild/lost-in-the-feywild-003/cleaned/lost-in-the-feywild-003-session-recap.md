They connect that sending to the bargain, the road bodies, and the neighbor's house.

#### Intermediate
Kaito asks what everyone learned while he was away, and the party pieces together its best theory: the clock's zero chime forces Lord Endless Ending to send mortal guests to the neighbor's house. Txarro watches the woman head north toward a western promontory, while the others review the naming contest, the wizard neighbor, Holda's suspicious deed, and the possibility that the road deaths come from whatever happens beyond that house.

#### Long
Kaito pulls the others into a debrief, and Ayveen lays out the grim pattern: the Lord chooses a mortal when the clock chimes zero, sends that person toward the neighbor's house, and the bodies outside may be people who did not survive whatever followed. Txarro checks through the owl and confirms that the woman is not returning toward the root house but continuing north toward a west-side promontory. The party revisits the Rakshasa's story of Lord Revel being renamed into Lord Endless Ending by a wizard, wonders whether Holda's deed and elf contact fit the same bargain, considers asking the guards about the bad smell, and finally decides that they need to speak to the Lord directly.

They approach Lord Endless Ending with mortal gifts and earn a formal welcome.

The party crosses the ballroom to the elegant fey lord and his anxious badger butler, and Kaito reads the butler's worried surface thoughts. The group answers the demand for gifts with Txarro's loon call, Edric's blessed water, Kaito's musical performance, Tarek's mouse-skull trick, and Ayveen's moonrise sheet music, and Lord Endless Ending receives them with poetry before welcoming the mortals into his home.

#### Long
Lord Endless Ending waits at the ballroom threshold, tall and poised, with a face whose expression only seems visible at the edge of sight. His badger butler presents him and asks what gifts the newly arrived mortals have brought, while Kaito's Detect Thoughts catches the butler's intense anxiety about broken glass, wine stains, household disorder, and being unable to leave his Lord's side. Txarro awkwardly offers the haunting sound of a loon from the mortal world, Edric gives a vial of holy water blessed by the Night Queen, Kaito turns his entrance into a spotlighted performance, Tarek makes a mouse skull vanish as a fragment of death, and Ayveen presents her decorated moonrise composition. Lord Endless Ending answers each in verse or judgment, then welcomes them as rare mortal guests at the end of his party.

### recap-004 | The Twenty-Seventh Room

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: B04
- Date: 1740-10-05
- Time: evening
- Source Range: u0893 -> u1165
- Locations: Lord Endless Ending's manor
- NPCs: Lord Endless Ending, badger butler
- Organizations: none
- Items: grandfather clock
- Enemies: none

#### Short
He explains that a mortal wizard bound him to send guests through the neighbor's house until a glass key from the 27th room is broken.

#### Intermediate
Txarro admits that they are trapped, and Lord Endless Ending says the way home lies through the nearest neighbor's house, the same direction as the woman sent by the clock. When pressed, he describes being tricked by a mortal wizard into an agreement to send guests through that house whenever the clock strikes zero, and says the bargain can be undone if a glass key from the cold-iron 27th room is brought back and broken.

#### Long
Txarro tells Lord Endless Ending the party came through a door that closed behind them and does not know how to leave. The Lord says there is a door home in his nearest neighbor's house to the north, exactly where the woman was sent, and the party's questions force him to acknowledge the clock's role: when it strikes zero, it is time to show a mortal guest home through that house. He describes the agreement as a trick by a human wizard who came with the neighbors, and he bitterly resents being bound by it. Through portents he has learned that the bargain rests on a glass key holding the last light of twilight, locked in a cold-iron 27th room; if the key is returned and broken, the bargain ends. Kaito warns him about the road bodies, but the Lord says his responsibility ends at the gate, and Ayveen draws out that the bargainer was mortal.

### recap-005 | No Fey Bargains

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: B05
- Date: 1740-10-05

- Locations: Lord Endless Ending's manor
- NPCs: Lord Endless Ending, badger butler
- Organizations: none

#### Short
They choose to leave for that house without promising the key or accepting a fey bargain.

#### Intermediate
While Lord Endless Ending steps aside, the party debates whether retrieving the glass key helps Holda, whether the neighbor's house is still their best exit, and whether any agreement with a fey lord is too dangerous. They study the clock, connect it to Dwarven Reckoning dates and the similar clock in Dandelion House, ask the Lord about Holda, and Kaito carefully secures permission to leave toward the neighbor's house without being bound to recover the key.

#### Long
Left alone with the question of agreement, the party splits its priorities into getting home, avoiding Holda's interests, learning what the neighbor's house is, and not accepting a fey bargain. Tarek points out that the deed itself was fishy, Ayveen argues that breaking the pact may still be their route home, and Kaito synthesizes a rule: no bargains with the fey lord, but go check out the house. Tarek studies the clock and notices Dwarven Reckoning numbers around its face, while the group realizes it resembles the magical clock in Dandelion House. Before leaving, Edric pushes Kaito to ask about Holda, and Lord Endless Ending says Holda behaved like a fool, was sent away rather than onward, and was no longer his guest once he left the grounds. Kaito then phrases the departure with careful candor until the Lord says that if the doorway toward the 27th room is truly their destination, his gates are open, and he does not wish to compel them as he was compelled.

### recap-006 | Clockwork And Number Dreams

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: B06
- Date: 1740-10-05
- Time: evening
- Source Range: u1652 -> u1998
- Locations: Lord Endless Ending's manor
- NPCs: Lord Endless Ending, badger butler, road guards, Monetheus
- Organizations: none
- Items: grandfather clock, Monetheus's satchel, The Shape of Possibility, Brilliant Numbers
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Before leaving, they learn the gate depends on their future choice and study the sleeping scholar's books about possibility and brilliant numbers.

#### Intermediate
The exit proves stranger than intention: the gate will be there only if the party has already gone through the neighbor's door in their future. After the guards say bad-smelling travelers have run from that direction, Edric returns to ask about the clock, and Lord Endless Ending points them to a sleeping member of the wizard's entourage in the library; Kaito reads the man's number-dreams while Tarek's stolen books reveal The Shape of Possibility and Brilliant Numbers.

#### Long
As they prepare to leave, the group learns that the gate's existence is not about stated plans but about story and future truth: it opens because, in the future, they have already gone through the other door. Txarro asks the guards where the bad-smelling people come from, and they say some have run from the direction of the neighbor's house. Edric's unease about the clock sends them back to Lord Endless Ending, who says the wizard and her entourage brought it, one of that entourage remains in the library, and the clock is both immovable and indestructible. The party finds the sleeping scholar later known as Monetheus, and Kaito reads dreams made of numbers, beauty, deadlines, unfamiliar language, and anger at his boss. Tarek has already emptied the man's satchel, and with Ayveen's Comprehend Languages the group reads The Shape of Possibility and Brilliant Numbers, finding theories of all possible futures, story-like time, semi-prime encodings, and clock numbers that may be coordinates or possibility markers.

- NPCs: Monetheus, road guards

#### Short
When the clock chimes again, they meet the awakened scholar Monetheus at the gate.

#### Intermediate
Txarro tests the river beside the manor and discovers that swimming around the bend brings him back to the same ending party, leaving him deeply unsettled. The clock then strikes zero, the party waits near the gate to follow the next sent guest, and the newly awakened scholar arrives with his lightened satchel, uses a ring to bridge the language gap, and explains that he came from Drankor and the 27th house with a large group.

#### Long
Still wanting a way home, Txarro dives into the river and swims downriver, only to round the bend and see the same lawns, lamps, and ending party again before the others glance away and find him reappearing near where he began. The clock strikes zero soon after, and the party decides to wait by the gate for whoever Lord Endless Ending sends next, while Tarek keeps the scholar's books and the group wonders whether waking him might help. The formerly sleeping man comes across the lawn on his own, notices the missing weight in his satchel, and hails them in a language only Ayveen can understand through magic. When she answers in Old Tollish, he recognizes Northros, touches a translation ring, and begins speaking with them directly. He says he is from Drankor, came from the 27th house with two or three dozen people, drank at the party, fell asleep, and woke to find everyone gone.

### recap-008 | Monetheus And The Door

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: B08
- Date: 1740-10-05
- Time: evening
- Source Range: u2240 -> u2598
- Locations: Lord Endless Ending's manor, single door on the western promontory
- NPCs: Monetheus, Lord Endless Ending
- Organizations: Erythromos Limperum
- Items: Brilliant Numbers, Monetheus's book, single door, eastern screech owl familiar
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Monetheus explains Telestria's order and leads them to the lone door while Lord Endless Ending asks for the key.

#### Intermediate
Monetheus realizes that Drankor has been gone for centuries and that everyone he knew is dead, then explains that Telestria led an order dedicated to maintaining the boundaries between worlds. He describes brilliant numbers as a way to hide precise maps of weak places in plain sight, accepts his book back without noticing the theft, and leads the party down the north road to a single door on a cliff-ringed promontory as Lord Endless Ending's distant poem asks them to bring back the key.

#### Long
The party's questions force Monetheus to confront that he is centuries out of time: Drankor has fallen, its language is no longer spoken, and Telestria's warnings about the empire's end may have been right. He explains that his society maintained the porous walls between the world, the Feywild, dream, and other realms, closing or repairing holes that should not stay open. Telestria, his master and the head of the order, redirected them toward sealing known breaches as the emperor's great project consumed more of the empire's resources. Monetheus names the order Erythromos Limperum and describes brilliant numbers as encoded location keys: with the right factors memorized, they can hide maps of weak points between spaces in plain sight. Tarek secretly returns the lifted book to his satchel, and Monetheus admits that he is a guide and decoder, not the person who performs the containment itself. After learning the current year and the empire's fall, he presses on toward the 27th house, and the party follows him north to a westward path ending at a lone door above dark cliffs. As they stand before it, Lord Endless Ending speaks from the manor, asking them to bring back the key and set him free, and Txarro's owl waits on the doorframe.
