**Time Window**: unknown

**Short Summary**: The party travels through a trackless old forest and reaches the overgrown grounds of Dandelion House at dusk.

## Holda Explains The Contract
*B05*

**Date**: 1750-10-04
**Time Window**: night
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: Lord Holda explains the ancient deed and hires the party to end an unknown treaty beyond a basement door.
**Long Summary**: Over dinner, Holda recounts decades as an adventurer, a costly victory over a young dragon, and the strange elf who delivered a centuries-old deed naming him heir to Dandelion House, its lands, and its treasure. The deed is also a contract: the house and hoard become his only if he steps through a door in the floor and convinces the man on the other side to end a long-held treaty. Holda admits he failed badly, offers the party half the treasure to succeed for him, and explains that the door opens onto another place with a road and another manor; Tarek reads the Sembaran contract and senses that something about its pieces does not fit, but the party agrees to rest and attempt the job in the morning.
**Location**:
- Dandelion House: kitchen dinner with Lord Holda
  Note: The party remains at the kitchen table while Holda explains the contract and proposed job.
**NPCs**:
- Lord Holda (encountered): employer. He wants the party to complete the treaty condition that he failed to fulfill.
- Alden (encountered): servant. He brings food and later is assigned to show the party to quarters.
- unnamed elf archivist (mentioned): deliverer of the deed. Holda says this elf's organization held the deed in trust until the right time.
- unnamed lord beyond the door (mentioned): target of the treaty negotiation. Holda met him through the basement door but failed to learn much or persuade him.
**Items**:
- ancient deed and contract (encountered): The brittle Sembaran document names Holda and lays out the condition for inheriting Dandelion House.
- treaty (mentioned): The party must persuade the unknown man beyond the door to bring a long-held treaty to an end.
- treasure hoard (mentioned): Holda promises the party half the value of the gold, coin, jewels, and other treasure if they complete the job.
- door in the floor (mentioned): The contract says the doom of the house lies locked beyond this basement door.
**Organizations**:
- unnamed elf organization (mentioned): The organization allegedly held the deed in trust for centuries.

## Into The Root Tunnel
*B07*

**Date**: 1750-10-05
**Time Window**: dawn
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: Before dawn, the party descends below Dandelion House, enters a root tunnel, and is sealed in as Tarek realizes the contract's language is wrong.
**Long Summary**: A magical clock wakes the party before dawn, and Alden leads them from their rooms down through the ground floor, basement, and a rougher lower stair opened with a glowing gold key. In a root-covered sub-cellar, Lord Holda waits by a hidden door and tells them to crawl through the dark passage, promising to hold this end open. The party enters in order through what proves to be the hollow center of an enormous dry root, but the door closes behind them; in the same moment, Tarek realizes that the unquestionably 400-year-old contract was written in perfectly contemporary Sembaran, and the session ends with the way back shut.
**Location**:
- guest quarters (Dandelion House) -> root tunnel beneath Dandelion House: pre-dawn descent through the basement and root cellar into the hidden passage
  Note: The beat ends with the party crawling through the root tunnel after the door behind them closes.
**NPCs**:
- Alden (encountered): guide to the basement. He wakes and collects the party before leading them down.
- Lord Holda (encountered): employer at the hidden door. He stands by the hidden door and says he will keep this end open.
**Items**:
- gold key (encountered): Alden uses a smaller glowing gold key to unlock the lower door.
- hidden door (encountered): The door opens into a narrow root passage and closes behind the party.
- ancient deed and contract (mentioned): Tarek realizes the old contract is written in contemporary Sembaran, creating a new contradiction.
**Organizations**:
