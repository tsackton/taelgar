[u0031] ## Narrative
[u0032] ### Wednesday, May 19th, 2 Robert I
[u0033] Returning to town, the party stops to speak with [[Helen of Haldrenn]], the temple administrator at the Temple of the Mother in the village.
[u0034] She tells them the basic story: Duncan lived alone in a small stone cottage on the edge of the village; he was a shepherd and would spend the summers up in the high moors with his flock, but in the winters he spent most of his time hanging out at [[Alewyn]]'s, whose hearth served as an informal pub in the winters.
[u0035] He was boastful, and most people thought he just talked big, even when he kept talking about a secret treasure.
[u0036] So the village was surprised when, last March, he said he had had enough sitting around and left, never to return.
[u0037] Most people think he died in the big late storm that came through in April.
[u0038] She also tells them that Duncan's cottage (now empty) sits on the ridge above the coppice wood, and the charcoal burners might have seen him when he left.
[u0039] The party then gathers information.
[u0040] First they talk to [[Alewyn]], who is excited to see them having heard the tale of their defense of the village from worgs, and feeds them lunch of stew and sauteed apples.
[u0041] She tells them the same basic story as Helen, but says that this past winter, something changed, and Duncan sounded more serious about actually having information about a secret treasure, and about planning his journey.
[u0042] [[Yvan Greenrabbit|Yvan]] gives her 1 gold piece for her trouble, for which she is very grateful.
[u0043] Next, the party speaks with [[Bran]] and [[Karl]], on their way to Duncan's cottage.
[u0044] [[Bran]] says that a few weeks ago, he saw a fire up on the ridge, and heard shouting in the middle of the night, but the next morning [[Karl]] checked it out (briefly and cautiously) and didn't see anything.
[u0045] Duncan's cottage is a simple one room stone and thatch affair, with a hearth, a table, and a straw bed.
[u0046] There is blood on the doorframe, though, that [[Yvan Greenrabbit|Yvan]] notices, while [[Drou]] finds smeared ash from the hearth suggesting a struggle and a heavy body being dragged, and [[Fazoth de Brune|Fazoth]] notices that the shepherd's crook is broken and hastily mended.
[u0047] Concluding that there was a fight in the cottage, but the body was dragged out, the party goes to search the root cellar.
[u0048] The latch on the door is broken, and inside are the signs of a fire: burned apples, turnips, and straw.
[u0049] Beneath the burned straw is a dead body, from which a vengeful spirit rises to attack.
[u0050] Though it immolates Yvan at the beginning of combat, it cannot leave the root cellar and between Fazoth and Yvan's _Witch Bolt_ spells and Drou's _Shatter_ spell, it is defeated, though [[Yvan Greenrabbit|Yvan]] is nearly killed.
[u0051] After the haunt is killed, the party searches the body.
[u0052] [[Fazoth de Brune|Fazoth]] deduces it was likely killed by an axe, though there is also evidence of blunt force trauma.
[u0053] The party also finds scattered ancient coins and treasure (120 ancient gold coins, 300 ancient silver coins, a pendant that looks like a amulet of [[The Warlord]]), and burned scraps of notes, which they take.
[u0054] [[Yvan Greenrabbit|Yvan]], with his painter skills, makes a painting of the dead body.
[u0055] That evening, the party warns the town and gets a good night's rest.
[u0056] ### Thursday, May 20th, 2 Robert I
[u0057] In the morning, the party departs for [[Roscombe]].
[u0058] On the road, and at camp that evening, they piece together bits of the fragments of paper.
[u0059] They seem to mostly be the rantings of a madman: "It needs me to find a new heart for his master" or "Must go home, get the heart from the old stories" or "The axe will help me with my quest".
[u0060] But there are occasionally moments of lucidity too: "It haunts my dreams, and I cannot make it stop".
[u0061] ### Friday, May 21st, 2 Robert I
[u0062] The party arrives at [[Caradoc]]'s cottage in the early afternoon.
[u0063] They speak with him for a while.
[u0064] He reveals that indeed he went by Aurenxis when he traveled in the [[Feywild]] long ago, to keep his true identity safe from mischief.
[u0065] He reminds them about [[Morlaith]] and the dangers of the [[Feywild]], and tells them that if they are venturing into the wilds after Duncan, they should stop by his cottage first.
[u0066] He has some old adventuring gear to give them.
[u0067] He also suggests they speak to [[Owen Tavish]] in town about the old coins.
[u0068] %% Caradoc pulls Fazoth aside, tells him he knows of his family, and they should say [[Caradoc]] is Aurenxis%%
[u0069] The party returns to [[Roscombe]], and meets with Owen Tavish before dinner.
[u0070] He tells them that 300 years ago, a war was fought in these parts against the armies of the [[Dominion of Avatus]].
[u0071] He claims the old coins found on the corpse in Duncan's cottage are dated from the reign of [[Derik I]], who ruled during the war against [[Avatus]]; indeed, these coins are from the same era as those found with the [[Serpentine Dagger]], he says.
[u0072] Owen suggests that the coins could plunder and to investigate old forts and battlefields from the ancient wars to find more.
[u0073] We end as the party returns to [[The Traveler's Rest]] to sleep.
