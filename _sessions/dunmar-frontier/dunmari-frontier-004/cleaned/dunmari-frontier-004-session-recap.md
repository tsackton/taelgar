## Recap

### recap-001 | Fort Aftermath

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b01
- Date: 1748-03-26
- Time: morning
- Source Range: u0001 -> u0420
- Locations: Dunmari Fort
- NPCs: none
- Organizations: Five Siblings
- Items: black iron staff, obsidian orb, holy symbol of Aagir
- Enemies: none

#### Short
At the fort, the party gathers the relics left in the ruined temple and quits the place with hard proof that the corruption there was real.

#### Intermediate
At first light the party sorts through the remains of the old temple, debating the meaning of the black iron staff, the obsidian orb, and the sacred objects tied to the Five Siblings. Once they have taken what they can and recovered their horse, they leave Dunmari Fort behind and start back toward Kerala.

#### Long
At first light the party wakes in the ruined fort and takes stock of what survived the night's struggle. In the temple they recover the black iron staff, the obsidian orb, and other relics linked to the Five Siblings, then argue over whether the dead hobgoblin and its artifacts belong to the Great War or something older and worse. After salvaging what they can and freeing their trapped horse, they abandon the fort and ride west with proof of what they destroyed.

### recap-002 | Return to Kerala

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b02
- Date: 1748-03-26 to 1748-03-29
- Time: unknown
- Source Range: u0421 -> u0699
- Locations: Dunmari Fort, Kerala
- NPCs: Kesa
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Over the next several days, the party rides through a quieter borderland and returns to Kerala to report that the fort has been dealt with.

#### Intermediate
The road back feels less oppressive than it did on the way east, and by the time the party reaches Kerala the town is crowded with travelers gathering for the Festival of Rebirth. Kesa hears their account with relief but warns them that attacks on the roads have not stopped.

#### Long
Over the next several days, the party rides west from Dunmari Fort through country that already seems less burdened by the evil they found there. That sense of relief fades when Kerala comes into view, alive with festival traffic and uneasy talk from the roads. Kesa receives them gladly, thanks them for ending the trouble at the fort, and then delivers harder news: travelers are still being attacked, and the town's quiet may only be temporary.

### recap-003 | Stories at Krom's

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b03
- Date: 1748-03-29
- Time: afternoon
- Source Range: u0700 -> u0979
- Locations: Kerala
- NPCs: Krom
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Back at Krom's inn, the party is welcomed as returning heroes and starts pulling on the thread Arcas left behind.

#### Intermediate
Fed and celebrated in Krom's crowded common room, the party shows him Seeker's sketch and learns that he recognizes the petrified traveler as Arcas. Krom recalls the man's odd silver coinage, his interest in ancient relics, and his last stop with the town blacksmith.

#### Long
Back in Kerala, Kesa sends the party on to Krom's inn with food and drink at the town's expense, and they find the place packed with festival visitors. Krom greets them warmly, studies Seeker's sketch, and identifies the figure as Arcas, a traveler who passed through town chasing old relics and paying with unfamiliar silver. His memories of Arcas, the growing crowds, and the recent troubles on the road convince the party that the stranger's path through Kerala is worth retracing.

### recap-004 | Maps and Suspicions

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b04
- Date: 1748-03-29
- Time: afternoon
- Source Range: u0980 -> u1355
- Locations: Kerala
- NPCs: Krom
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Pressing Krom for more, the party ties Arcas's search to old battlefields and decides to follow the trail to Josue.

#### Intermediate
The inn conversation broadens into talk of border roads, ruined fields, and the old war between powers that still mark the region. By comparing what each of them knows, the party begins to suspect that Arcas was hunting relics tied to those deeper histories rather than scavenging at random.

#### Long
Pressing Krom further, the party turns a simple identification into a longer exchange about Chardon, Sambara, Gomat, border roads, and the fields where older wars left their wreckage behind. Krom's recollections sharpen their suspicion that Arcas had come to Kerala in pursuit of specific relics rather than ordinary salvage, and that whatever he found may connect directly to the evil they faced at the fort. By the end of the discussion, they have a clearer lead and decide to seek out Josue the blacksmith.

### recap-005 | Josue's Spearheads

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b05
- Date: 1748-03-29
- Time: evening
- Source Range: u1356 -> u1549
- Locations: Kerala
- NPCs: Josue
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Josue confirms that Arcas left silver spearheads with him, but no fire or hammer in Kerala could make them yield.

#### Intermediate
The party catches Josue as his workday ends and follows him into a longer conversation about the traveler Krom mentioned. Josue explains that Arcas brought in several strange spearheads to be mounted, yet the metal would not take heat or shaping.

#### Long
That evening the party catches Josue just as he is closing his forge and persuades him to talk at greater length. He confirms that Arcas passed through Kerala looking for old Dunmari relics and left behind several silver spearheads to be hafted before vanishing from town. What stays with Josue is not merely the job but the metal itself, which resisted every ordinary attempt to heat, cut, or shape it, making the party all the more certain that Arcas uncovered something unnatural.

### recap-006 | Halfling Camp Warning

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b06
- Date: 1748-03-29
- Time: evening
- Source Range: u1550 -> u1794
- Locations: Kerala
- NPCs: Bree Charmheart, unnamed halfling caravaner
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
At Bree Charmheart's camp, the party discovers that the voice behind the fort's evil may already be reaching travelers on the road.

#### Intermediate
Moving through the outer camps at dusk, the party questions Bree Charmheart and learns that one wagon has been kept apart because its occupant is sick and unstable. When the afflicted halfling stumbles out babbling about a master speaking in his head, the resemblance to the staff's influence is impossible to ignore.

#### Long
From Josue's forge the party heads into the caravans gathered beyond Kerala, where Bree Charmheart receives them with guarded politeness. Wellby notices that one wagon is being kept apart and presses until Bree admits that her companion has been hearing voices and behaving erratically. When the hidden halfling lurches into view, raving that a master is speaking to him and will soon consume him, the party realizes the corruption they fought at the fort may already be spreading along the roads.

#### Short
Later that night, the party keeps watch at the oasis until Candrosa interrupts the vigil and calls them to the temple.

#### Intermediate
Disturbed by the halfling's madness, the party turns cautious and keeps a late watch around Kerala's oasis. Delwath pursues the local vigil for visions, Seeker joins him, Kenzo drinks in town, and Candrosa finally appears with an urgent summons.

#### Long
Later that night, with the halfling's ravings still fresh in their minds, the party splits into a loose vigil around the oasis. Delwath commits himself to the old custom of waiting there in hope of a true vision, Seeker keeps him company, Wellby remains alert, and Kenzo lingers at the inn in a drink-heavy haze. Before the night can settle into anything meaningful, Candrosa arrives in clear agitation and asks them all to come with him to the temple at once.

### recap-008 | Kendrasar's Truth

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b08
- Date: 1748-03-29
- Time: night
- Source Range: u2061 -> u2485
- Locations: Kerala
- NPCs: Candrosa
- Organizations: Shakun Mystai
- Items: black iron staff, healing mud vials
- Enemies: none

#### Short
In the temple, Candrosa puts the party under a zone of truth and finally admits that the border's ancient protections are failing.

#### Intermediate
Once the party reaches the temple, Candrosa tests their honesty before speaking plainly about what he and the mystai have been hiding. He links the staff, the mad halfling, the strange weather, and Havdar's reports into one frightening picture of a border coming undone.

#### Long
In the temple, Candrosa first subjects the party to a zone of truth, then abandons his careful reserve and tells them what he fears. Shakun's mystai, he says, have guarded Dunmar's border since the Great War, but the staff from the fort, the halfling's madness, the endless storm to the northeast, the hyena-like raiders to the south, and fresh reports from Havdar all point to those protections breaking down. Ashamed of his secrecy but desperate for help, he asks the party to remain in Kerala through the Festival of Rebirth and help watch over a border that no longer feels secure.
