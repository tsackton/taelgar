## After the Roper
*beat-001*

**Date**: 1748-04-12
**Time Window**: afternoon
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: After killing the roper in the ruined marketplace, the party searches the corpse and tavern, recovering a magical pearl and finding old hobgoblin graffiti.
**Long Summary**: The session resumes in the ruined dwarven marketplace beneath Raven's Hold just after the roper's defeat. While the party gets its bearings, Kenzo and the others examine the corpse closely enough to discover a magical pearl that survived the creature's acid. The group also inspects the still-standing tavern, studies old graffiti there, and realizes the outpost has seen both deep-underground predators and later hobgoblin occupation since the dwarves abandoned it.
**Location**:
- Dwarven Outpost (Raven's Hold): the ruined central marketplace and the partly intact tavern beneath the outpost
  Note: The whole beat stays in the first cavern where Inakara's group had forced the party to fight the roper.
**NPCs**:
- Inakara (encountered): derro leader watching from the passage. Though not named yet, she and her followers remain nearby and monitor the party after the roper dies.
**Items**:
- Pearl of Power (encountered): The pearl is found inside the roper's body and identified as a magical item that can restore spellcasting energy.
**Organizations**:
- Blackened Claw (mentioned): Wellby recognizes the burned three-fingered claw graffiti in the tavern as a hobgoblin banner from older frontier conflicts.

## Rest in the Tavern
*beat-002*

**Date**: 1748-04-12
**Time Window**: afternoon
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: The party regroups in the tavern, discusses the graffiti and the roper's lair, and completes a short rest while Seeker settles into the pearl's stored magic.

**Organizations**:

## The Mirror Doors
*beat-004*

**Date**: 1748-04-12
**Time Window**: afternoon
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: At the vault threshold, the party solves the mirror-door puzzle by noticing reflected necklaces on the statues and using the invisible keys to open the sealed chamber.
**Long Summary**: The approach to the vault leads into an antechamber of faceless statues and three polished metal doors that behave more like mirrors than ordinary entrances. Careful observation reveals that the statues bear necklaces only in reflection, and the party works out how to remove those unseen keys without looking away from the mirrored surfaces. Turning them together sends light through the stonework and opens the true central vault, proving that the dwarves left behind a lock meant to defeat tomb robbers and impatient scavengers alike.
**Location**:
- Dwarven Outpost (Raven's Hold): the antechamber before the sealed dwarven vault, with mirrored doors and flanking statues
  Note: Inakara follows invisibly at a distance but does not solve the puzzle herself.
**NPCs**:
- Inakara (encountered): invisible observer outside the puzzle chamber. She trails the party to the threshold but leaves the actual door puzzle to them.
**Items**: none
**Organizations**: none

## The Dead Vault
*beat-005*

**Date**: 1748-04-12
**Time Window**: afternoon
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: Inside the sealed vault, the party finds dead dwarven defenders and a slain mind flayer, then recovers the Brawnanvil shield, Shatterstorm, potions, driftglobes, and stamped trade bars.
**Long Summary**: The opened vault reveals a chamber undisturbed for centuries: five dead dwarves laid carefully to rest, a shattered mind flayer corpse, emptied shelving, and a few surviving caches. Riswynn reclaims the Shield of the Brawnanvil Clan, Seeker studies the rune-heavy warhammer Shatterstorm, and the party catalogs six marked potion bottles, two dwarven driftglobes, and a supply of gold and silver trade bars. The scene makes clear that the outpost did not merely decay; it fell in violence tied to the ancient mind flayer threat that once drove the dwarves out.
**Location**:
- Dwarven Outpost (Raven's Hold): the sealed Brawnanvil vault beneath the marketplace
  Note: The room has remained closed long enough for the dust and bodies to stay almost perfectly undisturbed.
**NPCs**:
- mind flayer (encountered): long-dead invader in the vault. Its skull has been smashed open, suggesting the dwarven defenders killed it before dying themselves.
- five dead dwarven defenders (encountered): fallen guardians of the sealed vault. They were laid out with weapons and shields, preserving the room as a place of memory as well as treasure.
**Items**:
- Shield of the Brawnanvil Clan (encountered): Riswynn finds her family's heirloom shield in the vault and claims it for return to the clan.
- Shatterstorm (encountered): A rune-inscribed magical warhammer found beside one of the dead dwarven defenders.
- six sealed potion bottles (encountered): The labels indicate three restorative draughts, two strength potions, and one health-related potion.
- Dwarven driftglobes (encountered): Two crystal globes survive in the vault's stores.
- dwarven trade bars (encountered): The surviving silver and gold bars are stamped with Brawnanvil marks and become the party's main bargaining currency.
**Organizations**:
- Brawnanvil Clan (encountered): The shield, trade bars, and crafted magic items all mark the vault as a Brawnanvil family holding.

## The Eastward Grate
*beat-007*

**Date**: 1748-04-12
**Time Window**: afternoon
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: Back outside the vault, the party pays Inakara in silver for the eastern route, reaches a locked grate beneath Raven's Hold, and ends still debating her warnings about the mind flayers' return.
**Long Summary**: Once the vault is shut again, Inakara immediately presses for more treasure and lets silver bars buy answers, guidance, and just enough cooperation to keep moving. She reveals her name, refuses to pass through the eastern tunnel herself, and leads the party through other dark sections of the outpost while a driftglobe's light drives lurking shapes back into the walls. At the end of that route the party reaches a rusty iron grate before a tunnel sloping east toward Raven's Hold, and the scene closes with unresolved arguments over whether Inakara's feared brain-eaters are real, how far below they may be, and what the roper had been keeping in check.
**Location**:
- Dwarven Outpost (Raven's Hold): the passages around the vault and the locked eastward grate beneath the outpost
  Note: The last in-world stopping point is the chained grate overlooking the route toward Raven's Hold; the transcript then trails into end-of-session chatter.
**NPCs**:
- Inakara (encountered): derro guide to the eastern tunnel. She takes silver for guidance, finally names herself, and keeps speaking in fearful fragments about the brain-eaters and the blind ones.
- blind ones (mentioned): lurking creatures in the shadowed side passages. Inakara warns the party away from them but never clearly explains what they are.
**Items**:
- silver trade bars (encountered): The party uses several of the bars recovered from the vault to pay Inakara for directions and information.
- Dwarven driftglobe (encountered): One of the recovered globes is activated to light the passages while Inakara guides the party east.
**Organizations**: none
