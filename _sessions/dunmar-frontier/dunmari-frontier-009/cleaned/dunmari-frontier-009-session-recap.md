#### Long
In the aftermath of the roper's death, the party uncovers a Pearl of Power, old hobgoblin traces, and enough leverage to make Inakara lead them deeper into the abandoned outpost. A mirrored vault puzzle yields the Brawnanvil shield, Shatterstorm, potions, driftglobes, and trade bars from a sealed chamber where dead dwarves and a smashed mind flayer still lie, and the day closes at a chained grate toward Raven's Hold with Inakara's warnings about returning brain-eaters still unresolved.

### recap-001 | After the Roper

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-001
- Date: 1748-04-12
- Time: afternoon

- Locations: Dwarven Outpost
- NPCs: Inakara

#### Short
Fresh from killing the roper, the party searches the ruined marketplace, pulls a magical pearl from the corpse, and finds old hobgoblin graffiti in the tavern.

#### Intermediate
Fresh from killing the roper, the party takes stock of the abandoned marketplace beneath Raven's Hold and searches both the corpse and the only structure still standing. A magical pearl survives the creature's acid, the tavern walls preserve old hobgoblin markings, and the scene confirms that the outpost has stood empty long enough to attract predators from the deep.

#### Long
Fresh from killing the roper, the party finally has room to look around the ruined dwarven marketplace where it was forced to fight. Kenzo and the others search the corpse closely enough to discover a magical pearl that somehow survived the creature's acid, then turn to the half-collapsed tavern and the older traces left there. What they find is not just rubble but evidence of layered abandonment: the place has been empty long enough for deep predators to settle in, yet the walls also carry later hobgoblin marks that prove other hands occupied the outpost after the dwarves fled.

- Kind: beat

- Locations: Dwarven Outpost
- NPCs: none

#### Short
Using the tavern as shelter, the party debates what the graffiti and the roper mean, then takes a short rest while Seeker settles into the pearl's power.

#### Intermediate
Using the tavern as shelter, the party pauses long enough to sort fear from evidence. The discussion turns on how long the outpost has lain empty, what sort of creatures now live in it, and whether the pearl taken from the roper is worth the risk of going deeper, until a short rest gives Seeker time to confirm that the pearl stores restorative magic.

#### Long
Using the tavern as shelter, the party lets the first burst of danger fade and starts thinking like explorers again instead of prey. The graffiti, the roper, and the strange dwarf-like inhabitants all become pieces of the same question: what exactly took root here after the dwarves abandoned the outpost, and how much of it still matters now? By the end of the short rest Seeker has settled into the pearl's magic and learned that it stores and gathers restorative power, which turns the roper's last swallowed prize into something worth carrying deeper into the mountain.

#### Short
Rested and steadier, they return to Inakara and force a bargain that gets them guided to the vault Riswynn came to find.

#### Intermediate
Rested and better prepared, the party goes back to the derro leader instead of blundering forward alone. Inakara tries to turn Riswynn's search for her family's treasure into leverage, but silver, pressure, and the party's obvious strength are enough to make her lead them toward the vault on terms she thinks will still profit her.

#### Long
Rested and steadier, the party decides that the next move must go through Inakara whether it likes her or not. She makes it plain that she wants treasure more than trust, and every answer she gives arrives tangled up with fresh demands or evasions, but the party now has enough confidence to keep the bargain from slipping entirely into her hands. Under that uneasy arrangement she leads them away from the marketplace and toward the vault entrance that Riswynn came to Raven's Hold to find.

- Beat IDs: beat-004

- Time: afternoon
- Source Range: u0977 -> u1349
- Locations: Dwarven Outpost
- NPCs: Inakara
- Organizations: none
- Items: none

#### Short
At the vault threshold, careful work with mirrored doors and reflected necklaces reveals invisible keys and opens the sealed chamber.

#### Intermediate
At the vault threshold, brute force gives way to patience. The party studies the statues, notices that the missing necklaces exist only in reflection, and learns how to lift the invisible keys without looking away from the mirrored surfaces, until the stonework itself answers with light and opens the true central door.

#### Long
At the vault threshold, the outpost finally shows the party the kind of craftsmanship the dwarves left behind when they fled. The statues are faceless, the polished doors behave like mirrors, and nothing yields until the group understands that the important details exist only in reflection. Once the unseen necklaces are lifted and the keys are turned together, light runs through hidden channels in the stone and the real vault opens, proving that the dwarves expected thieves, scavengers, and the impatient long before Inakara ever started circling the place.

### recap-005 | The Dead Vault

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-005
- Date: 1748-04-12
- Time: afternoon
- Source Range: u1350 -> u1659
- Locations: Dwarven Outpost
- NPCs: mind flayer, five dead dwarven defenders
- Organizations: Brawnanvil Clan
- Items: Shield of the Brawnanvil Clan, Shatterstorm, six sealed potion bottles, Dwarven driftglobes, dwarven trade bars
- Enemies: none

#### Short
The chamber beyond holds dead dwarves, a shattered mind flayer, and the Brawnanvil relics Riswynn came to reclaim.

#### Intermediate
The opened vault preserves a scene of death that has not been disturbed in centuries. Riswynn finds her clan's shield where she hoped it would be, Seeker begins to understand the rune-heavy warhammer beside the dead, and the surviving stores of potions, driftglobes, and trade bars make the room feel as much like a sealed family treasury as a tomb.

#### Long
The chamber beyond holds exactly the sort of history Riswynn feared had already been looted away. Five dwarven defenders still lie where they were laid to rest, a dead mind flayer with a smashed skull proves that the old enemy reached even this vault, and the shelves around them preserve just enough of the outpost's wealth to show what was lost. Riswynn reclaims the Shield of the Brawnanvil Clan, Seeker studies the warhammer later known as Shatterstorm, and the party inventories the surviving potions, driftglobes, and stamped trade bars while the room's long-sealed silence makes the ancient fall of the outpost feel suddenly close.

### recap-006 | Tunnel Beneath the Vault

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-006
- Date: 1748-04-12
- Time: afternoon
- Source Range: u1660 -> u2094
- Locations: Dwarven Outpost
- NPCs: none
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
After sorting the treasure and the old battle's meaning, the party probes a hidden tunnel to an abandoned mineshaft, then seals the vault behind it again.

#### Intermediate
After the first rush of discovery, the party turns from treasure to the more troubling question of what else the vault might connect to. Delwath scouts the descending passage beyond the chamber, finds only an old worked shaft rather than fresh danger, and that thin reassurance is enough to bring the group back to the antechamber, replace the keys, and leave the vault sealed once more.

#### Long
After sorting the treasure and arguing over what the dead mind flayer might imply, the party decides it cannot ignore the hidden passage beyond the chamber. Delwath follows it far enough to prove that it ends at a worked vertical shaft from the outpost's older mining life, not at an obvious nest or current stronghold, and the lack of fresh tracks is almost as important as the stone itself. With no immediate threat revealed, the group retreats to the antechamber, restores the keys to their reflected resting place, and closes the vault again before Inakara can seize more than rumor and silver from what was found inside.

### recap-007 | The Eastward Grate

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-007
- Date: 1748-04-12
- Time: afternoon
- Source Range: u2095 -> u2561
- Locations: Dwarven Outpost
- NPCs: Inakara
- Organizations: none
- Items: silver trade bars, Dwarven driftglobe
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Back outside, Inakara sells the eastern route for silver and leads the party to a locked grate, where the day ends with grim debate about what may be rising below.

#### Intermediate
Back outside the sealed chamber, every answer has its price again. Inakara names herself, takes silver for guidance and scraps of truth, leads the party through the dark side passages of the outpost, and stops only at a chained grate before the eastern tunnel, leaving the group to weigh whether her talk of brain-eaters and blind ones is madness, prophecy, or both.

#### Long
Back outside the sealed chamber, the party has to buy movement the same way it bought access: with silver and patience. Inakara proves practical enough to trade directions for bars cut from the vault, fearful enough to refuse the eastern way herself, and coherent enough to guide the group through other shadowed sections of the outpost while a driftglobe's light drives unseen watchers back from the walls. At the chained grate before the tunnel sloping toward Raven's Hold, the day's discoveries settle into a harder question: whether the dead mind flayer in the vault belongs to a finished story, or whether Inakara is right that something below is still preparing to come up.
