#### Short
Before dawn, Candrosa brings Kenzo to a hidden mesa and sends him through the Dreamweaver Staff into the Dreamworld.

#### Intermediate
Before dawn Candrosa shares the jade-hand dream that has haunted him and leads Kenzo to a hidden mesa above Karawa. With the Dreamweaver Staff he opens a path into the Dreamworld, where Kenzo repairs a cracked jade hand and meets a robed figure who names the Order of the Awakened Soul.

#### Long
Before dawn Candrosa wakes Kenzo, leads him out to a hidden mesa above Karawa, and finally explains the dreams that have been troubling him: each night a cracked jade hand reaches toward him, and in the latest vision Kenzo stood inside it. Using the Dreamweaver Staff, Candrosa sends Kenzo into the Dreamworld, where Kenzo fits his jade fragment into the hand, is drawn before a robed figure with a jade arm, and hears a warning to seek the Order of the Awakened Soul. He returns to his body with the vision still burning in him and with a new, unsettling sense that the world around him can be read in colors and auras.

By the end they have not solved the mystery, but they have decided on their next practical steps: stay for the council, learn what Karawa can tell them, and keep Raven's Hold in mind as a likely lead.

- Source Range: u0980 -> u1324
- Locations: Karawa
- NPCs: Jasu
- Organizations: none
- Items: silver spearpoints
- Enemies: none

#### Short
After lunch the party visits Jasu and finds that Arcus left behind spearpoints no ordinary forge can master.

#### Intermediate
Jasu shows them the silver spearpoints Arcus wanted remounted and explains that neither fire nor hammer would make the metal yield. The party's own inspection suggests Great War craft and anti-undead magic, deepening the question of what Arcus thought he was walking toward in the east.

#### Long
After lunch the party goes to Jasu's forge and studies the strange silver spearpoints Arcus left in his care. Jasu explains that Arcus expected to be back within a week or two, but the more striking fact is that the spearpoints themselves refuse ordinary work: they stay keen, resist heat, and look bound up with older magic than a frontier smith can easily name. Once the party recognizes the relics as Great War weapons likely meant for fighting undead, Arcus's vanished expedition starts to look much less like scavenging and much more like a deliberate search for something dangerous.

### recap-008 | Council Alarms

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b08
- Date: 1748-04-04
- Time: morning
- Source Range: u2068 -> u2484
- Locations: Karawa
- NPCs: Kisa, Candrosa, Havdar, Alesh, Suma
- Organizations: Karawa council
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
In council, Havdar and Alesh confirm that Karawa faces raids, refugees, fixed storms, and a threat too large to understand at a glance.

#### Intermediate
Havdar reports hyena-like raiders ravaging the southern farms and driving survivors north, while Alesh describes stranger signs beyond them: a storm that does not move and black water reeking of blood. The room quickly grasps that Karawa is not confronting one local outrage but a spreading disorder whose edges are already wider than its knowledge.

#### Long
In council, Havdar and Alesh strip away whatever comfort the festival still offered. Havdar reports hyena-like raiders tearing through the southern farms and warns that refugees will soon arrive in force, while Alesh adds the broader signs she found on her ride: a fixed storm, a black river stinking of blood, and similar creatures working the land beyond Karawa's ordinary horizon. With Kisa, Candrosa, and Suma listening, the room is forced to admit that it is not facing a single raid or even a single border breach, but a disorder whose shape is still unclear and whose scale may already exceed anything Karawa can answer alone.

### recap-009 | Arcus and Karawa's Choices

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b09
- Date: 1748-04-04
- Time: morning
- Source Range: u2485 -> u2705
- Locations: Karawa
- NPCs: Alesh, Candrosa
- Organizations: none
- Items: scout's horn

#### Short
Arcus, Raven's Hold, and the old forts return to the center of the discussion as the council tests what Karawa can actually defend.

#### Intermediate
Alesh confirms that Arcus had been asking about Great War forts, especially Raven's Hold, and the party shares what it found with him at Gomat. From there the talk turns practical: how safe Tokra really is, how hard the temple can be made, and how long Karawa can hold if Shakun's old protection no longer answers.

#### Long
Once the first shock has passed, the council circles back to the clue the party has been carrying all session: Arcus's trail. Alesh confirms that he had questioned her closely about old Great War forts, especially Raven's Hold, and the party adds its own evidence from Gomat, the scout's horn, and the relics found around his body. That shift from history to logistics is immediate, because the council then has to ask what Karawa can actually defend, how safe Tokra might be, how strong the temple remains, and what any of those answers are worth if Shakun's power is no longer reliably there to support them.

### recap-010 | Evacuation and New Missions

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b10
- Date: 1748-04-04
- Time: morning
- Source Range: u2706 -> u3141
- Locations: Karawa
- NPCs: Kisa, Candrosa, Havdar, Alesh, Suma
- Organizations: Karawa council
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Kenzo's proposal carries the room: save the people first, fortify the temple, send for the king, and let the party ride north toward Raven's Hold.

#### Intermediate
Kisa and Suma back an evacuation west under guard, Candrosa accepts a smaller holdout at the temple, and Havdar commits his riders to protecting the retreat. By the time the council rises, Karawa has chosen survival over pride, while the party takes on the clearest lead left to pursue.

#### Long
Kenzo's proposal gives the room a way to turn fear into action: move Karawa's people west under Havdar's protection, fortify the temple as a fallback, send for the king, and avoid wasting strength on a blind strike into the south. Kisa and Suma support the evacuation, Candrosa accepts that he will hold the temple with fewer people than he would have wished, and Alesh offers to help divide the wider response between political appeal and fresh reconnaissance. By the time the meeting ends, Karawa has chosen to survive as a people rather than die for its buildings, and the party has chosen its next road north toward Raven's Hold.
