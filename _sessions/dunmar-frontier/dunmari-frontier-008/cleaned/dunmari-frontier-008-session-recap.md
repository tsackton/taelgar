#### Short
At the foot of the mountains, the party chooses Riswynn's mapped back route over the exposed approaches to Raven's Hold.

#### Intermediate
The party stands at the pass arguing over poisoned water, the open road, and the possibility of a hidden dwarven entrance. Riswynn's map and their memory of the outpost slowly push them toward a mountain approach rather than a direct assault.

#### Long
At the mountain pass south of Raven's Hold, the party talks through what the centaurs reported about poisoned water, spreading corruption, and the uncertain source of the danger ahead. After comparing the front road, the water route, and the hidden passages marked on Riswynn's map, they decide the best chance lies in finding the old back entrance through the mountains.

### recap-002 | Rune Stones and Gorge

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b02
- Date: 1748-04-12
- Time: midday
- Source Range: u0450 -> u0584
- Locations: Gorge entrance west of Raven's Hold
- NPCs: Taster
- Organizations: none
- Items: Dwarven marker stones
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Seeker and Wellby follow numbered dwarven stones to a hidden gorge, and the party commits to entering by the stream.

#### Intermediate
Searching the rough ground east of the road, the party identifies displaced stones carved with descending dwarven numbers. The markers lead them to a gorge entrance, where Taster's reluctant service convinces them the water is safe enough to risk.

#### Long
Leaving the road behind, the party works through the boulder-strewn foothills until Seeker and Wellby spot strange volcanic stones that have clearly been placed by hand. The carved numbers count down toward a stream-cut gorge marked as an entrance, and after forcing Taster to drink from the water and survive, the party accepts that this hidden route offers their best way into the outpost.

### recap-003 | Flooded Tunnel Entrance

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b03
- Date: 1748-04-12
- Time: midday
- Source Range: u0585 -> u1076
- Locations: Raven's Hold
- NPCs: none
- Organizations: none
- Items: Entrance chain mechanism
- Enemies: none

#### Short
The gorge narrows into a flooded tunnel, but Riswynn frees the hidden chain and drains the entrance passage.

#### Intermediate
The party presses into the watercourse until the horses and most of their gear can go no farther. In the fully flooded tunnel Delwath scouts ahead, Riswynn wrestles the submerged chain loose, and the old dwarven water gate finally gives way.

#### Long
The hidden route quickly turns into a difficult submerged approach as the gorge tightens, the water rises, and the party is forced to abandon the horses and most of their supplies. Delwath reaches the metal obstruction ahead, Riswynn manages to pull the hidden chain, and the mechanism drops the plate and redirects the water through the floor, turning an impossible entrance back into a walkable tunnel.

### recap-004 | Escape Hatch and False Rubble

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b04
- Date: 1748-04-12
- Time: midday
- Source Range: u1077 -> u1365
- Locations: Raven's Hold
- NPCs: none
- Organizations: none
- Items: Concealed hatch and ladder, Illusionary rubble
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Beyond the drained gate, the party works through a suspicious escape chamber and an illusion of rubble to uncover another hidden door.

#### Intermediate
The old guard passage offers a hatch, a ladder, and a lever that looks more like a trap than an invitation, so the party proceeds carefully instead of taking the bait. Farther in, a wall of rubble proves to be nothing but illusion, and a concealed stone knob finally reveals the next secret door.

#### Long
With the entrance cleared, the party explores a cramped dwarven security passage built around escape hatches, hidden access, and defensive deceit. They ignore the tempting lever, open the concealed ladder route, pass through an ancient illusion of collapsed stone, and eventually find the disguised mechanism that opens the wall ahead.

### recap-005 | Inakara's Bargain

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b05
- Date: 1748-04-12
- Time: afternoon
- Source Range: u1366 -> u1741
- Locations: Outer cavern of the Dwarven Outpost
- NPCs: Inakara
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
The hidden door opens into Inakara's cavern, where the party wins passage only by agreeing to kill the creature troubling her halls.

#### Intermediate
The secret door gives onto a foul inhabited grotto full of pale cavern dwellers, refuse, and murky pools, all under the wary gaze of Inakara. Shocked that the party has come from the hidden side, she questions them in broken Dwarven and turns the encounter into a bargain: kill her monster, and she will let them pass.

#### Long
The wall swings open onto a large grotto that was once part of the dwarven complex and is now occupied by a strange community of gray-skinned cavern dwellers. Their leader, Inakara, confronts the party in alarm, probes their purpose, and then redirects the meeting toward a blunt exchange of service for service, promising onward passage if they destroy the dangerous creature deeper in the outpost.

### recap-006 | The Roper Strikes to Wearing the Roper Down

- Kind: combat
- Beat IDs: b06, b07, b08
- Date: 1748-04-12
- Time: afternoon
- Source Range: u1742 -> u2907
- Locations: Raven's Hold
- NPCs: Inakara
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: Roper

#### Short
In the trading hall, the party learns the roper's reach, stays beyond it, and slowly shoots the monster down.

#### Intermediate
Inakara shoves the party toward the old trading hall, where a roper hidden among the stone forms lashes out and nearly turns the fight into a disaster at once. After enduring grapples, missed attacks, and some painful trial and error, the group realizes the creature can barely move and methodically destroys it from outside the reach of its tentacles.

#### Long
Led into the former trading hall, the party is ambushed when the roper's tentacle snaps out of the darkness and seizes Wellby, forcing everyone to test hurried tactics against a creature with a stony hide and long reach. The fight turns once they understand that the beast is slow, lacks useful mobility, and can be managed by spacing, line of sight, and ranged attacks; from there they keep away from the tentacles, cut themselves free when needed, and wear the monster down until it cracks apart while Inakara's people laugh and cheer from the edges of the cavern.
