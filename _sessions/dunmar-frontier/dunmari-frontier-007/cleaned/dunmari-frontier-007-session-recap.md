#### Short
Fresh from Karawa's defense, the party rides north, reads the split tracks on the plains, and decides Raven's Hold is the likeliest source of the attacks.

#### Intermediate
Fresh from Karawa's defense, the party leaves the village with urgency but not yet a fixed route. Seeker's prepared maps and calculations shape the discussion, and once the fresh tracks on the plains are sorted out, the group concludes that the larger movement came from the northeast and that Raven's Hold must be investigated first.

#### Long
Fresh from Karawa's defense, the party leaves the village before the fear of the night attack has had any time to settle into memory. Seeker tries to turn the route question into something orderly with maps and measured travel options, but the real answer lies in the fresh sign waiting north of town. Once the split tracks are studied in the open grassland, the party sees enough to believe the larger threat came down from the northeast, which turns Raven's Hold from a suspicion into the clearest destination ahead.

### recap-002 | Choosing the Route

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-002

- Time: unknown
- Source Range: u0243 -> u0542
- Locations: Northern Dunmar Plains
- NPCs: none
- Organizations: none
- Items: Eberk Brawnanvil's maps
- Enemies: none

#### Short
From there, they choose a direct hard ride across the plains, trusting Riswynn's inherited maps because no faster warning can reach the settlements beyond them.

#### Intermediate
From there, the argument shifts from where the attackers came from to whether anyone else can still be warned in time. Once the party accepts that neither birds nor riders can outrun the danger well enough to matter, it abandons the road, trusts Riswynn's maps, and begins cutting northeast across the prairie toward Raven's Hold.

#### Long
From there, the party spends its energy on the harder question of what can still be saved by speed alone. The western raiders seem too far gone to catch with confidence, and the lack of any quick warning network makes distant settlements feel painfully unreachable. With Riswynn's inherited maps in hand and Wellby's sense for the land to steady them, the group accepts a direct line over the plains, makes its first uneventful camp, and rides on the next day with Raven's Hold fixed more firmly in mind.

#### Short
On the third day, that ride brings them to Shani and a band of goat-herders fleeing south from gnolls, fire, and the endless storm to the north.

#### Intermediate
On the third day, the road north finally produces witnesses instead of tracks. Shani and her fellow goat-herders arrive in dust and fear, carrying south what goats they managed to save, and their account of lightning, fire, and ravening creatures confirms that the danger around Raven's Hold is wider and stranger than a single raid.

#### Long
On the third day, the long ride north finally turns from deduction to testimony when the party reaches a road crossing and intercepts a refugee band in flight. Shani, speaking for the goat-herders with her, describes fire driven by lightning, a storm that has sat unnaturally over the north for days, and gnolls that fell upon them while they ran. The party offers healing, points them toward safety in Karawa, and rides away with a harsher picture of the country ahead: the uplands are already beginning to empty.

### recap-004 | Ambush at Camp to Breaking the Gnolls

- Kind: combat
- Beat IDs: beat-004, beat-005, beat-006
- Date: 1748-04-09 to 1748-04-10
- Time: unknown
- Source Range: u1006 -> u1895
- Locations: Northern Dunmar Plains
- NPCs: gnolls, maws

- Enemies: gnolls and maws, gnolls

#### Short
That warning becomes immediate when night falls and gnolls with maw-creatures crash into the camp, forcing the party to fight for hours in darkness and broken cover.

#### Intermediate
That warning becomes immediate when the party's camp is attacked under cover of darkness. The opening rush of maws and the more disciplined pressure from gnoll archers pin the party among the rocks, and the fight only stays manageable because magic, cover, and Seeker's rabbit-bait trick keep the attackers from pressing home all at once.

#### Long
That warning becomes immediate when the party settles for the night and the darkness around camp suddenly fills with teeth, arrows, and motion. Maw-creatures hurl themselves into the first rush while the gnolls behind them prove far more organized than the village attack suggested, using cover, poison, and missile fire to grind the party down. The fight stretches across the whole night as the party clings to rocks and spellwork, and only by drawing out the attackers with confusion and improvised bait does it stay alive long enough to turn the battle.

#### Short
At dawn's edge, the last gnolls fall, and the party takes stock of crude weapons, a grisly bone necklace, and the vulture's thin warning about what lies ahead.

#### Intermediate
At dawn's edge, the fight finally breaks in the party's favor, and the last fleeing gnoll is cut down before it can carry warning elsewhere. The search that follows yields almost nothing of value beyond a necklace of bloody goat bones, but it leaves the group with a more important lesson about how disciplined these creatures are becoming, and Seeker's exchange with a circling vulture adds only a spare confirmation that death and movement lie ahead near the fortress.

At dawn's edge, the last of the gnolls are finally hunted down and killed before any of them can vanish into the plains. What the dead carry offers little reward, only crude local weapons and a grisly necklace made from goat knuckle bones, yet even that sparse evidence matters. The ambush has shown the party that the threat ahead is not just savage but adaptive, and Seeker's brief exchange with a scavenging vulture only sharpens that impression by hinting at fresh death and motion around Raven's Hold and the stormlands.

### recap-008 | Parley with Tharandros

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-010
- Date: 1748-04-12
- Time: morning
- Source Range: u2519 -> u2978
- Locations: Approach to Raven's Hold
- NPCs: Tharandros, centaurs
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Their leader proves to be Tharandros, who explains that poisoned water and a tether to another realm are spreading corruption from Raven's Hold, and who agrees to carry warning south to Karawa.

#### Intermediate
Their leader proves to be Tharandros, and the tense first contact settles into a long exchange of broken translation, old suspicion, and shared alarm. He tells the party that his people are fleeing poisoned streams and watering holes that seem to spread from Raven's Hold, and by the end of the parley he is persuaded that the Dunmari in Karawa need warning as badly as the centaurs need safer ground.

#### Long
Their leader proves to be Tharandros, and what begins as a wary dawn encounter turns into the party's clearest account yet of the corruption ahead. Through halting translation and metaphor, he explains that the waters near Raven's Hold are turning poisonous because some tether to another realm still remains in place, feeding a corruption that spreads faster each day. The talk ranges from demon-haunted other planes to the old human beliefs that gave such horrors shape, and it ends with an uneasy alliance of necessity: the party will continue north toward the hold, while Tharandros turns south to carry warning and what knowledge he can spare to Karawa.
