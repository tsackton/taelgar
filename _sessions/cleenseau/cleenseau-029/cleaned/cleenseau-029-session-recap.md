Robin accepts the lordship of Asineau and begins remaking the village into a stronger, richer home.

#### Intermediate
Robin takes possession of Asineau after the Baroness's fall, and the party throws itself into rebuilding the village around its new lord. New defenses, workshops, trade plans, and local improvements begin to turn their demesne into a real base rather than a neglected backwater.

#### Long
Robin's acceptance of the lordship of Asineau turns the party's recent victory into a new civic responsibility. He takes charge of defenses and administration while Celyn pushes for a healthier local economy and Izgil adds research and craft infrastructure, with the village gaining new smithing, workshop, garden, and glassmaking projects. As halfling merchants, craftsmen, and nearby lizardfolk become more involved in village life, Asineau starts to look less like a burden inherited from Lorin Valbert and more like a place the party genuinely means to build.

#### Short
When Vahaiya warns of a dragon on the eastern border, the party rides to Marwick and offers to take up the hunt.

#### Intermediate
Vahaiya arrives in high summer with news of a dragon ravaging the border between Tyrwingha and Addermarch, and the party decides to intervene before the damage spreads further. At Marwick they find a castle already half-emptied by fear and learn just how badly the upland settlements have been mauled.

#### Long
That settled stretch ends when Vahaiya brings word of a dragon attacking the eastern borderlands and fears it may be tied to an older, more terrible draconic threat. With the Sembaran Army and the Rangers already stretched thin by the southern ash crisis, the party steps forward because few others can. Their ride to Marwick brings them to a stronghold stripped down for survival, where the local Earl can offer little more than hard-won intelligence about destroyed summer villages, vanished herds, and a predator that has already scattered the countryside.

#### Short
At Marwick, Robin plans a dragon-killing ballista while the others scout the ruined uplands and learn the beast's habits.

#### Intermediate
Robin turns his recent study of siegecraft toward building a ballista suited for bringing down a fast-moving dragon. Meanwhile Celyn, Viepuck, and one of the Earl's surviving knights search the ruined villages, find signs of kobolds, and confirm when the dragon leaves the heights to hunt.

#### Long
At Marwick, Robin applies everything he has been learning about fortification and siege warfare to the problem of dragging a dragon out of the sky. While he and Izgil work on the specialized ballista, Celyn and Viepuck head into the ruined uplands with one of the Earl's surviving knights to see what the wreckage can teach them. Their scouting reveals traces of kobolds around the dragon's territory, and a day spent watching from the ridge tells them when the creature leaves to plunder, giving the party the timing they need to turn theory into an ambush.

### recap-004 | Kobolds Take the Cow

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: b04
- Date: unknown
- Time: evening
- Source Range: u0028 -> u0048
- Locations: ruined mountain village near the dragon's lair
- NPCs: the winged kobold leader, the winged kobold's sister
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Their trap catches kobolds instead, and the party turns the meeting into a surrender, an interrogation, and an alliance of convenience.

#### Intermediate
When the baited village draws in a kobold foraging party rather than the dragon, Viepuck and Celyn help force a surrender before bloodshed can really begin. The questioning that follows reveals rival kobold factions, the southern disaster that drove them north, and enough fear of the dragon that Robin can recruit the least loyal among them.

#### Long
The carefully prepared trap does not bring the dragon first; instead it snags a small kobold party hoping to profit from the cow set out as bait. Viepuck's sleep magic, Celyn's sudden appearance, and Robin's sheer presence bring the standoff under control, and what might have been a skirmish turns into a long, unexpectedly fruitful interrogation. The kobolds explain that they were driven north by the ash-cloud horror in the south, that their dragon's followers are split between zeal, caution, and quiet panic, and that even loyal servants do not think the beast hunts the way the party expected. By the end of the exchange, Robin has bent that confusion into a practical arrangement: the least committed kobolds will stay close and help spring the trap when the dragon returns.

- NPCs: none
- Organizations: none

#### Short
With the ambush reset and the friendlier kobolds in place, the party waits in the dark until the dragon begins its descent.

#### Intermediate
The party repositions for the night, sends Es*tiaslos near the lair as a watcher, and settles into a long silence broken mostly by talk of what might happen to the surviving kobolds afterward. Just after midnight, the warning comes that the dragon is moving with five kobolds in tow.

#### Long
With the plan chosen, the party settles into the last tense stretch before battle. Es*tiaslos takes up the forward watch near the lair, the allied kobolds remain close enough to be useful, and Robin is already thinking past the fight to whether the survivors might be brought back to Asineau. Celyn, amused by how badly that would play with the local miller, has time to enjoy the thought only briefly. After midnight the quiet breaks: the dragon is coming down with five kobolds, and the trap is about to become a battle.
