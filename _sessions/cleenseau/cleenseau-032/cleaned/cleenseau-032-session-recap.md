### recap-001 | Toward the Portal

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-001
- Date: unknown
- Time: afternoon
- Source Range: u0001 -> u0015
- Locations: Cleenseau, thorn maze in the Feywild
- NPCs: none

#### Short
Asked to make the Cleenseau portal stay shut for good, the party arms itself, slips into the Feywild under concealment, and picks a cautious long route through the living thorn maze.

#### Intermediate
The queen's request leaves the party with no pleasant option, so it gathers specialized supplies, masks its approach, and enters the portal determined to end the problem properly. On the far side, a cold thorn maze, a distant peak feeding ritual power into it, and Izgil's careful mapping all point toward a slower but safer approach inward.

#### Long
Asked to make the Cleenseau portal stay shut for good, the party begins by gathering exactly the sort of supplies one brings when planning to wreck a hostile magical structure. Celyn prays for concealment, Izgil tries without success to draw Ethlenn's attention, and the group slips through the portal into a wet, bitter corner of the Feywild where thorn walls shift around them and magical tendrils run from the maze's heart toward a distant peak. With Es*tiaslos scouting from above and Izgil keeping a live map, they choose the longer route inward on the theory that the obvious path in a place like this is probably the worst one.

### recap-002 | Vines on the Long Route to The Gazebo Falls

- Kind: combat
- Beat IDs: beat-002, beat-003
- Date: unknown
- Time: afternoon
- Source Range: u0016 -> u0030
- Locations: thorn maze in the Feywild
- NPCs: Greymalkin
- Organizations: none
- Items: Robin's lantern, barrel of tar
- Enemies: grabby vines, thorn knot, thorn monster

#### Short
The maze answers with hostile vines, a collapsing core chamber, and a thorn guardian, but radiant magic, eldritch improvisation, and brute persistence destroy the knot and kill the portal.

#### Intermediate
The long route proves why it was needed when grabby vines lash out from the covered passages and force the party to learn by trial that radiant power works where other attacks do not. That hard-won lesson carries into the core chamber, where Viepuck's escape from a collapse, Izgil's moonlight, and a final stand against the knot's guardian finally break the ritual focus and still the maze.

#### Long
The long route through the maze does not stay quiet for long. Hanging vines seize at the party, necrotic magic only angers them, and each kill bursts into foul ichor, so Celyn and Izgil settle the question with radiant cantrips while Robin's lantern exposes false turns in the dark. At the heart of the maze the fight changes shape but not pressure: the roof drops on Viepuck, the chamber opens around a massive thorn knot drinking ritual power from afar, and the whole room turns into a race between destruction and entrapment. Viepuck tears a Far Realms wound into the problem, Celyn forces insight through with divine aid, Izgil floods the chamber with moonlight, and when a thorn monster lurches out to break the assault the party beats it back just long enough for the last glowing remnant of the knot to be blasted apart. The guardian topples, the hedges go still, and the dead maze is left burning behind them as they escape.

### recap-003 | Flight to the Pine Forest

- Kind: beat

- Organizations: none

#### Short
With the maze dead behind them, the party flees east at uncanny speed and reaches a pine-forest cottage that looks too welcoming to trust.

#### Intermediate
There is no appetite to linger once the portal is shut, so the party hides its tracks again and runs for the edge of the Gloomshaper's lands. The retreat becomes unnervingly easy, almost as though the road itself wants them gone, and by evening that strange luck delivers them to a pine wood and a cottage whose hospitality is suspicious from the first step.

#### Long
With the portal finally dead, the party does not waste its margin. Celyn veils the trail again, the group pushes east across the Gloomshaper's territory, and the journey takes on an eerie effortlessness that nobody trusts enough to question for long. By the time the air feels less oppressive and pine trees rise around them, all anyone wants is shelter, which is exactly why the neat path and the pleasant cottage ahead feel like another test. The old lady who owns it confirms that suspicion the moment she starts deciding who may enter, who must stay outside, and what counts as civilized conduct under her roof.

### recap-004 | Tea Under House Rules

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-005
- Date: unknown
- Time: evening
- Source Range: u0036 -> u0044
- Locations: pine forest cottage in the Feywild
- NPCs: old lady of the pine cottage, Greymalkin, goatboy servant
- Organizations: none
- Items: Robin's lantern
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Inside, an exacting little old lady, a furious Greymalkin, and a goat-headed servant turn supper into an etiquette trial that slowly yields the name Izar the Red.

#### Intermediate
The cottage proves survivable only so long as the party can read the room faster than it offends its host. Greymalkin's outrage, Robin's reluctance, dreadful tea, and a glimpse of the goatboy in back all threaten to spoil the visit, but politeness, gifts, and careful conversation gradually win enough trust to bring Izar the Red into the open.

#### Long
Inside the cottage, the party walks into a different kind of danger. The little old lady is not openly hostile, but she expects obedience to her rules of hospitality, and Greymalkin's instant conviction that the place is evil nearly ruins everything before anyone has even finished sitting down. Tea arrives, Viepuck breaks a cup, Izgil repairs it, Celyn works overtime to say the right flattering things about a truly dreadful brew, and Robin eventually gives up trying to resist the logic of etiquette. That slow recovery of good will matters, because meanwhile the goat-headed servant in back and Viepuck's mindreading make it clear that he once belonged to Izar the Red, a frightening collector of trophies taken from defeated fey.

### recap-005 | Wings in the Trophy Room

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-006
- Date: unknown

- Locations: pine forest cottage in the Feywild
- NPCs: old lady of the pine cottage, goatboy servant, Greymalkin
- Organizations: none
- Items: none

#### Short
Further questioning reveals that Izar keeps wings that may be Greymalkin's, and the old lady makes plain over dinner that she would welcome his downfall.

#### Intermediate
Once the old lady points them back toward the goatboy, the party finally gets the answer Greymalkin most needs to hear. The servant confirms that Izar's trophy room holds wings that may be his, asks help for a brother still trapped in the keep, and returns the whole evening to the dinner table, where their host quietly blesses any effort that removes Izar from her neighborhood.

#### Long
With the niceties established, the party is finally allowed to ask the right questions. Celyn speaks to the goatboy gently, Viepuck pushes the memories the rest of the way open, and together they learn that Izar keeps a pair of wings in his trophy room that may well be Greymalkin's. The goatboy also wants help for his brother, still trapped in that service, which turns the next journey from vague revenge into something more concrete. When everyone goes back in for dinner, the old lady's opinion of Izar becomes unmistakable: he is ruining the character of the neighborhood, and if someone were to happen to him she would not object in the slightest.

Morning strips away the cottage's ambiguity and leaves only terms. Viepuck buys the goatboy with weirdberries, the old lady provides directions but no cure, and the party heads toward Izar's keep with a guide who may be more useful there than he is safe anywhere else.

With that, the party leaves the cottage behind and starts marching toward the keep, stopping only halfway there at lunch to think through what help they can realistically offer before the next confrontation begins.
