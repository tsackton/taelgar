## Vines on the Long Route
*beat-002*

**Date**: unknown
**Time Window**: afternoon
**Combat**: yes (full)
**Main Enemies**:
- grabby vines: Animated thorn vines guarding the route into the maze's covered center.
**Combat Notes**: The party discovers the vines' damage resistances and settles on radiant magic as the reliable answer.
**Short Summary**: Hostile grabby vines force the party to learn the maze's rules the hard way and fight their way onward with radiant magic and lantern light.

  Note: This beat covers the first active defensive layer inside the maze.
**NPCs**: none
**Items**:
- Robin's lantern (encountered): The lantern proves unusually helpful for choosing turns in the dark maze.

## The Gazebo Falls
*beat-003*

**Date**: unknown
**Time Window**: afternoon
**Combat**: yes (full)
**Main Enemies**:
- thorn knot: The immobile ritual focus sustaining the portal.
- thorn monster: A guardian that emerges from the hedge to break Izgil's concentration and protect the knot.
**Combat Notes**: The source frames this as a literal Combat With The Gazebo, with the knot as the objective and the thorn monster as its active defender.
**Short Summary**: At the maze's heart, the party survives a collapsing chamber, destroys the thorn knot powering the portal, kills its guardian, and escapes through the dead maze.
**Long Summary**: The center chamber reveals a massive thorn knot fed by ritual energy from a distant peak, and the room immediately turns hostile when the roof drops on Viepuck. Once he escapes, the party studies the knot under pressure, with Viepuck opening a Far Realms rift against it and Celyn forcing his insight through with divine aid while Izgil floods the chamber with moonlight. As the immobile knot burns down, a thorn monster emerges to protect it, but the party stalls and beats it long enough for Viepuck to destroy the last glowing remnant, collapsing the ritual, stilling the maze, and letting the group escape after setting more of the thorns ablaze.
**Location**:
- thorn maze in the Feywild: the roofed core chamber and the passages leading out through the now-stilled maze
  Note: The chamber holds both the ritual knot that sustains the portal and the guardian that tries to stop the party.
**NPCs**:
- Greymalkin (companion): Robin's wingless gryphon. Greymalkin stays close during the collapse and the final chamber fight.
**Items**:
- barrel of tar (encountered): The tar barrel is lost under the collapse, then later ignited to help spread more fire through the maze.
**Organizations**: none

## Tea Under House Rules
*beat-005*

**Date**: unknown
**Time Window**: evening
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: Inside the cottage, the party survives a strict etiquette test, dreadful tea, and Greymalkin's outrage while slowly learning that a goat-headed servant fled Izar the Red.
**Long Summary**: The cottage proves to belong to an archetypal little old lady who insists on civilized behavior, even as the party discovers a goat-headed servant in the back and Greymalkin loudly objects to the whole arrangement. By sitting, speaking politely, repairing broken crockery, and flattering her truly terrible tea, the group gradually restores her approval. In the course of that careful social dance, Viepuck learns that the goatboy once served Izar the Red, a frightening figure who keeps trophies taken from defeated fey.
**Location**:
- pine forest cottage in the Feywild: the front room, tea table, and immediate grounds around the cottage
  Note: Most of the beat turns on the old lady's hospitality rules and the party's efforts not to offend her further.
**NPCs**:
- old lady of the pine cottage (encountered): strict host. She is benign enough, but only if the party follows her standards of etiquette.
- Greymalkin (companion): excluded from the tea table. Greymalkin's agitation over the cottage keeps Robin from sitting at first and nearly derails the visit.
- goatboy servant (encountered): former servant of Izar the Red. The party finds him in the back of the cottage, partially transformed and eating hay.
- Izar the Red (mentioned): frightening master named by the goatboy's memories. Viepuck learns that Izar collects trophies from defeated fey, including a unicorn horn.
- Cloudspinner (mentioned): absent ruler of Amberglow. The old lady asks whether the party has come from Amberglow after noticing Robin's lantern.
**Items**:
- Robin's lantern (encountered): The lantern's origin in Amberglow is what prompts the old lady's question about the party's travels.
**Organizations**: none

## Wings in the Trophy Room
*beat-006*

**Date**: unknown
**Time Window**: night
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: Questioning the goatboy reveals that Izar keeps wings that may be Greymalkin's, and over dinner the old lady all but invites the party to move against him.
**Long Summary**: With the old lady's permission, the party speaks to the goatboy directly and learns that his brother is still trapped in Izar's service and that Izar keeps a pair of wings in his trophy room that may once have belonged to Greymalkin.
