#### Short
The party spreads out the ballista crew, the decoy, and the allied kobolds, then waits for the dragon to enter the trap.

#### Intermediate
Plans that have clearly been in the making since the prior cliffhanger are finally put in place. Viepuck prepares to serve as the decoy, Celyn and the ballista hold a separate position, and the allied kobolds stay back while the group readies the magic and timing meant to bring the dragon down.

#### Long
The source begins by reestablishing who is present and what the party has spent its time preparing since the last break in play. Viepuck is set to impersonate a kobold, Celyn and the ballista are placed apart from the main cluster, and the allied kobolds are deliberately kept well away from the center of the coming fight. Everything is arranged around one blunt objective: survive the first pass, ground the dragon, and turn the battlefield into confusion before it can simply terrorize the party into collapse.

### recap-002 | Crashing the Dragon

- Kind: combat
- Beat IDs: beat-002
- Date: unknown
- Time: unknown
- Source Range: u0012 -> u0018
- Locations: dragon ambush site
- NPCs: Greymalkin, kobold leader
- Organizations: none
- Items: fly potion
- Enemies: dragon, hostile kobolds

#### Short
When the dragon dives in, the plan frays into chaos, but the party brings it down and forces the surviving hostile kobolds to yield.

#### Intermediate
The dragon's opening sweeps burn through key defenses and shrug off some of Izgil's magic, turning the encounter into a messy scramble across the prepared field. Even so, Celyn keeps finding openings, Viepuck continues the assault, Robin holds the line, and Greymalkin joins the aerial chase until the wounded dragon can no longer escape.

#### Long
The first exchanges go badly enough to prove that the plan is only a plan once the dragon actually starts moving. Fire tears through the field, the ballista fails to produce the hoped-for breakthrough, and the dragon repeatedly resists the party's attempts to control the fight. Robin stays between danger and Izgil, Celyn turns invisibility and mobility into brutal close strikes, Viepuck keeps applying pressure, and Greymalkin takes a fly potion so he can carry Robin into the air for the final pursuit. Once the dragon crashes, the remaining hostile kobolds lose whatever nerve they had left, and the field ends in surrender rather than a second clean battle.

### recap-003 | Tribute and Tunnel Entry

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-003

- Locations: dragon ambush site, kobold lair
- NPCs: Greymalkin, captive kobold
- Organizations: none

#### Short
With the field won, the allied kobolds heap tribute on Greymalkin, and the party uses the pause to slip through a back tunnel into the lair.

#### Intermediate
Victory gives the party only a short breathing space. The allied kobolds decide Greymalkin must be the real leader, reward him with a belt buckle and more tribute, and then send the party on with what little extra information they can provide about the lair and its hidden way in.

#### Long
The aftermath is comic enough to keep the victory from settling into ordinary triumph. The allied kobolds, impressed by Greymalkin's flight, elevate him into a figure of command, shower him with gifts, and use him to cow a captive enemy. That burst of celebration ends quickly once the party hears what it needs to hear and moves on to the back entrance. Celyn scouts through the tight tunnel, Izgil solves the worst bottleneck with dimension door, a few kobolds are quietly neutralized before they can raise the alarm properly, and the whole group manages to gather inside the lair with surprise still mostly intact.

### recap-004 | Clearing the Kobold Lair

- Kind: combat
- Beat IDs: beat-004
- Date: unknown
- Time: unknown
- Source Range: u0022 -> u0026
- Locations: kobold lair
- NPCs: kobold leader
- Organizations: none
- Items: dragon loot
- Enemies: kobold leader, remaining kobold defenders

#### Short
Inside the lair, the last kobold resistance collapses quickly, and the party spends the rest of the push clearing traps, claiming the dragon loot, and deciding what to do with the carcass.

#### Intermediate
The final lair fight barely has time to become dangerous before it is over, with Celyn and Viepuck dropping the kobold leader before she can show what she was meant to do. From there the action shifts straight into cleanup as the party loots the barracks, works through the trapped tunnel, and reaches the treasure at the far end.

#### Long
Once the guards stirred by the tunnel disturbance come back, the last meaningful resistance of the session proves brief and one-sided. Celyn lands the decisive blow almost immediately, Viepuck finishes the leader, and the remaining defenders stop mattering as individual threats. The rest of the beat turns into a methodical advance through the lair's hazards: tripwires, spikes, a grease trap, and the front-door mechanism near the treasure all have to be understood or bypassed before the party can settle over the dragon loot. By the end, triumph gives way to logistics, because killing a dragon is easier than deciding what to do with one afterward.
