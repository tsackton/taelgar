Back in Asineau, the party sorts dragon spoils, absorbs its new kobold followers into village life, and begins a more serious map of the surrounding fey realms.

#### Intermediate
Back in Asineau, victory over the dragon gives way to practical cleanup as Izgil sorts useful remains, Vahaiya carries off the best trophies, and Robin's new village keeps adjusting to life with kobold dependents and lizardfolk trade. At the same time, Celyn and Izgil turn their attention to a growing map of the Feywild, comparing what they know of Ethlenn, Lord Umbraeth, Greymalkin's lost realm, and rumors from farther west.

#### Long
Back in Asineau, the party is still living in the long shadow of the dragon kill. Izgil separates out the best pieces of the carcass for future magical work, while Vahaiya takes the marketable remains back toward elven lands to place them with a crafter who will eventually answer in kind. The kobolds who survived the expedition have now attached themselves to Greymalkin, which unexpectedly helps Robin's project of making Asineau a more open and prosperous village, especially as the local lizardfolk youth and the human teenagers settle into a mix of rivalry and friendship around the new glassworks. Meanwhile, Celyn and Izgil begin the more strategic labor of assembling a real map of nearby fey realms and their mortal correspondences, tying together what they know of Ethlenn's lands, Lord Umbraeth's reach, Greymalkin's conquered home, and word of another portal being destroyed in western Addermarch.

#### Intermediate
Early in October, the queen's advance people arrive to determine whether Asineau can manage even a single night of royal hospitality. Robin, Celyn, and the others muddle through food, lodging, wine, and decorum, then settle on the more important work of riding out ahead and escorting Elaine II through the swamp road themselves.

#### Long
Early in October, Asineau receives the sort of visit that makes everyone's station suddenly feel very visible. A small corps of royal retainers arrives to determine whether the village can host Queen Elaine II for one night on her circuit through Aveil, and the answer is effectively yes, provided nobody expects luxury from the poorest village in the region. Robin fields awkward questions about whether he has ever hosted an entourage before, Celyn quietly enjoys being able to produce Tyrwinghan wine from home, and the whole exercise turns into a crash course in how to stage a royal stop without pretending Asineau is anything it is not. By the end of the planning, the real issue is not manners but danger, so the party commits to heading out a day early, camping beyond the swamps, and escorting the queen's train through the dangerous stretch themselves.

### recap-003 | Ambush in the Swamp Road to Running Down the Duskhound

- Kind: combat
- Beat IDs: beat-003, beat-004, beat-005
- Date: unknown
- Time: unknown
- Source Range: u0020 -> u0040
- Locations: swamp road between Asineau and Cleenseau
- NPCs: Elaine II, The Hunter, Greymalkin, Duskhound, Es*tiasilos
- Organizations: none
- Items: Hunter's spear
- Enemies: The Hunter, ensorcelled elk, Duskhound

#### Short
On the swamp road the Hunter springs his ambush with red-eyed elk and a Duskhound, but the party rescues Elaine II, kills the Hunter, and runs down the fleeing hound before it can warn Lord Umbraeth.

#### Intermediate
The escort through the swamps turns ugly the moment the kobold scouts report red-eyed elk charging the baggage train, a sure sign that the Hunter is near. The fight escalates from a hard roadside melee into outright crisis when a Duskhound grabs Queen Elaine II, but Viepuck and Izgil improvise a wildly effective rescue, the Hunter is beaten into paste, and the last fleeing hound is hunted down before it can escape.

#### Long
The escort through the swamps goes bad exactly the way the party feared it would. The kobold scouts return with word of giant deer with the same red-eyed corruption the Hunter has used before, and within moments Robin is shielding the entourage, Viepuck is keeping a telepathic link open, Izgil is tracking the Hunter by his spear, and Celyn is slipping into cover to strike from the side. The first exchanges are dangerous but manageable until a Duskhound teleports into the royal ring and actually seizes Queen Elaine II, turning the ambush into a desperate rescue. Viepuck solves the immediate problem by sending Es*tiasilos into the melee with a Fly spell, and Izgil follows with a Dimension Door straight upward that hauls the queen clear before the abductors can extract her. With his prize lost, the Hunter rages, orders the Duskhound to warn Lord Umbraeth, and is promptly beaten to death. The hound nearly makes good on the order, but Robin and Greymalkin run it down, sweep Celyn into the chase, and stay on it long enough for the last warning to die on the road as well.

### recap-004 | Ordered to Close the Portal

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-006
- Date: unknown
- Time: unknown
- Source Range: u0041 -> u0047
- Locations: swamp road between Asineau and Cleenseau
- NPCs: Elaine II
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
In the aftermath, the party tells the queen that only closing the portal will meaningfully blunt the threat, and she orders them to ride on, sleep, and strike it in the morning.

#### Intermediate
With the ambush broken, the party explains that killing the attackers does not solve the larger problem while Lord Umbraeth still has a working portal into the region. Elaine II accepts that answer at once and turns the next day into an explicit royal order: get her safely to Cleenseau, gather what they can, and close the portal before the enemy can reset.

#### Long
Once the road is briefly secure, the party has to explain the more uncomfortable truth: this victory has bought time, not safety. Celyn lays out that so long as Lord Umbraeth's portal remains active, the threat to Aveil and to the queen herself will keep renewing in one form or another. Robin makes no secret of how exhausted he is by the entire business, but that does not change the answer, and Elaine II asks plainly whether the only real solution is to close the portal. When Celyn says yes, she immediately instructs the party to do it. That turns the end of the session into a hard pivot toward the next mission: get the queen safely to Cleenseau, stop briefly in Asineau, see whether the research has yielded anything more, sleep, and strike the portal at first light before Lord Umbraeth can reinforce it.
