## Dragon Spoils and Fey Maps
*beat-001*

**Date**: unknown
**Time Window**: unknown
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: After killing the dragon, the party ships off valuable remains with Vahaiya, folds a band of kobolds into Asineau's life, and expands a growing map of nearby fey realms.
**Long Summary**: The session opens in the aftermath of the dragon kill as Izgil sorts out useful dragon parts, Vahaiya carries the marketable remains back to elven lands for eventual repayment in crafted magic, and the party settles into its new role as lords of Asineau. Robin's effort to make the village more open and prosperous now includes kobold retainers and stronger ties with the local lizardfolk, while Celyn and Izgil turn their attention to a large annotated map of the Feywild and its mortal correspondences. Their work draws together what they know about Ethlenn's lands, Lord Umbraeth's reach, Greymalkin's lost realm, and new rumors from western Addermarch about another destroyed portal.
**Location**:
- Asineau: the village after the dragon fight, including the party's household and Celyn's growing fey-realm map project
  Note: The whole beat stays in post-dragon village life and planning rather than moving to a new adventure site.
**NPCs**:
- Vahaiya (mentioned): elven ally carrying the dragon remains home. They take the marketable dragon parts to elven crafters who are expected to return equivalent magical gifts.
- Greymalkin (companion): wingless gryphon now venerated by the kobolds. His past and grudges also inform the party's growing map of fey realms.
- Ethlenn (mentioned): fey queen whose territory anchors the party's map. Celyn and Izgil are especially clear on the bounds of her realm and its tie to Tyrwingha.
- Lord Umbraeth (mentioned): the Gloomshaper whose claimed territory shapes the party's planning. His expansion in the Feywild is one of the main problems Celyn and Izgil are trying to understand.
**Items**:
- dragon remains (encountered): Izgil sorts the carcass into useful magical components and marketable trophies.
- annotated fey-realm map (encountered): Celyn and Izgil begin compiling a heavily marked map of fey realms and their mortal counterparts.
**Organizations**: none

## Preparing for the Queen
*beat-002*

**Date**: unknown
**Time Window**: unknown
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: Royal retainers inspect Asineau for Queen Elaine II's visit, and the party turns the hosting problem into a plan to escort her through the swamps themselves.
**Long Summary**: Early in October, a royal advance party arrives in Asineau to see whether the village can host the queen for a single night during her circuit through Aveil. Robin, Celyn, and the others work through sleeping arrangements, kitchens, and the awkward realities of entertaining an entourage from a poor but newly important village, while Celyn gets a small private satisfaction out of being able to produce Tyrwinghan wine from home. ## Ambush in the Swamp Road
*beat-003*

**Date**: unknown
**Time Window**: unknown
**Combat**: yes (start)
**Main Enemies**:
- The Hunter: The main attacker lying in wait for the queen's escort.
- ensorcelled elk: The red-eyed beasts are used to open the ambush and scatter the train.
**Combat Notes**: The first phase of the fight is about recognizing the trap, protecting the entourage, and surviving the Hunter's first committed strike.
**Short Summary**: While escorting the queen's train through the swamps, the party recognizes the Hunter's signature work in red-eyed elk and braces for an ambush on the road.
**Long Summary**: The escort begins with the party's kobolds scouting ahead of the baggage train until they spot giant deer charging in with the same red-eyed corruption the Hunter used before.   Note: The beat stays on the road where the ambush first breaks open.

- The Hunter (enemy): ambusher stalking the royal escort. The red-eyed elk and the tracked spear confirm that he is lying in wait for the queen's passage.
- Greymalkin (companion): Robin's gryphon shouting a challenge into the woods. He openly taunts the Hunter as the ambush takes shape.
**Items**:

## Saving the Queen
*beat-004*

**Date**: unknown
**Time Window**: unknown
**Combat**: yes (middle)
**Main Enemies**:
- The Hunter: He remains the central battlefield threat until the party finally kills him.
- Duskhound: The hound's successful grab of the queen forces the fight into an immediate rescue operation.
**Combat Notes**: This phase is defined by the queen's near-abduction, the desperate spell rescue, and the Hunter's death.
**Short Summary**: A Duskhound reaches the entourage and seizes Queen Elaine II, but Viepuck and Izgil improvise a vertical rescue while the party beats the Hunter to death.
**Long Summary**: The ambush escalates when a Duskhound teleports into the royal ring and grabs the queen after the Hunter has already broken into the guard. Robin keeps pressure on the Hunter despite a spear wound, the guards try to keep the lesser threat contained, and the whole fight suddenly turns on whether the queen can be pulled clear before the Duskhound escapes with her. Viepuck solves that problem with startling audacity by having Es*tiasilos deliver Fly, then Izgil follows up with Dimension Door straight upward so the queen is hauled out of the melee and away from the extraction attempt. Deprived of his prize and roaring orders to warn Lord Umbraeth, the Hunter is then cut down on the road.
**Location**:
- swamp road between Asineau and Cleenseau: the royal guard's defensive ring around the queen as the ambush becomes a rescue scene
  Note: The fight remains centered on the baggage train and the queen's immediate surroundings.
**NPCs**:
- Elaine II (encountered): queen seized and then rescued from the Duskhound. The entire beat turns on keeping her from being abducted into the Feywild.
- The Hunter (enemy): fey ambusher fighting inside the royal guard's formation. He drives the attack until the party kills him at the roadside.
- Duskhound (enemy): teleporting fey hound attempting the abduction. It grabs the queen and nearly escapes before Izgil blocks the extraction.
- Es*tiasilos (companion): Viepuck's familiar carrying the rescue spell into the melee. It delivers the Fly spell that lets the queen be lifted clear.
**Items**:
- Hunter's spear (encountered): The Hunter uses the spear to teleport into the guard ring and later leaves it buried painfully in Robin's side.
**Organizations**: none

## Running Down the Duskhound
*beat-005*

**Date**: unknown
**Time Window**: unknown
**Combat**: yes (end)
**Main Enemies**:
- Duskhound: The final hostile survivor of the ambush.
**Combat Notes**: The last combat phase is a fast moving chase whose entire purpose is to prevent a warning from reaching Lord Umbraeth.
**Short Summary**: The surviving Duskhound tries to flee and warn Lord Umbraeth, but Robin, Celyn, Greymalkin, and Viepuck run it down before it can escape.
**Long Summary**: With the Hunter dead, the last open question is whether the Duskhound will get away and carry a report back through the Gloomshaper's network. Robin and Greymalkin launch into pursuit at magical speed, sweep Celyn into the chase, and keep pressure on the hound while Izgil falls more slowly back toward the field and Viepuck works around the still-flying queen problem. The chase stays just close enough for repeated blows until Celyn finally kills the fleeing Duskhound, after which the party regroups and rapidly assesses how much danger remains if Lord Umbraeth notices the silence.
**Location**:
- swamp road between Asineau and Cleenseau: the running pursuit away from the baggage train and the regroup immediately afterward
  Note: Although the action spreads out, the chase remains tied to the same escort road and ends back in discussion at the ambush site.
**NPCs**:
- Elaine II (encountered): queen kept aloft and protected while the chase finishes. The party is still managing her safety while it hunts down the last enemy.
- Duskhound (enemy): last surviving attacker fleeing with orders to warn Lord Umbraeth. The party kills it before it can escape the road or deliver the message.
- Greymalkin (companion): mount and pursuit engine for Robin and Celyn. His magically accelerated speed is what makes the kill possible.
- Lord Umbraeth (mentioned): fey lord the Duskhound is trying to warn. The regrouping conversation centers on how quickly he may notice the hound's death.

**Organizations**: none

## Ordered to Close the Portal
*beat-006*

**Date**: unknown
**Time Window**: unknown
**Combat**: no
**Short Summary**: After the ambush, the party tells Queen Elaine II that the only real answer is to close the Feywild portal, and she orders them to do it at once.
**Long Summary**: Once the party has a moment to breathe, it explains to the queen that the roadside victory has not really ended the problem so long as the Feywild portal remains open and useful to Lord Umbraeth's servants. Celyn states plainly that permanently closing the portal is the only practical way to thwart the threat for any meaningful length of time, while Robin makes no effort to hide how tired he is of fey disasters. Elaine II accepts the answer immediately and asks them to undertake the job, so the new plan becomes getting her safely into Cleenseau, stopping briefly in Asineau, gathering what little more they can from their research, sleeping, and striking the portal early the next morning.
**Location**:
- swamp road between Asineau and Cleenseau: the roadside regroup and strategy discussion immediately after the ambush
  Note: The beat stays in the aftermath conversation before the party rides on toward Cleenseau.
**NPCs**:
- Elaine II (encountered): queen taking counsel and then issuing the order to close the portal. She accepts the risk assessment and puts the next mission directly on the party.
- Lord Umbraeth (mentioned): archfey whose network still threatens Aveil while the portal remains active. The discussion assumes that only shutting the portal will seriously blunt his reach into the region.
**Items**: none
**Organizations**: none
