#### Long
The first afternoon fight leaves the forge-side jailers dead and the party free to recover stolen gear, food, and a clearer view of Perdoli Manor around them. After a short rest and a cautious survey of the nearby barracks, they attempt a disguised prisoner transfer, lose control of the bluff, win a second fast skirmish under cover of fog, and then drag the captured Folcan back to the smithy, where he explains that Grimstone takes one prisoner a week and that tunnels from the barracks, kitchen, and perhaps the chapel may reach the manor's wine cellar.

#### Short
Four strangers wake in a crude cell inside an abandoned smithy, break through a rotten plank wall, and arm themselves after overhearing that goblins are keeping them for Grimstone.

#### Intermediate
Acescale, Gaudin, Loria, and Dilion regain consciousness in a makeshift prison on the grounds of Perdoli Manor in Western Cymea. They introduce themselves, break out through a rotten wall, scavenge usable gear from the smithy and side room, and learn from the guards' drunken chatter that Grimstone, Folcan, and Ulgna are tied to a steady trade in captured prisoners.

#### Long
Acescale, Gaudin, Loria, and Dilion awaken stripped of their equipment in a crude wooden cell built inside an abandoned smithy. After taking stock of one another, they discover that one prison wall is badly rotted, tear their way through it, and crawl into the workshop beyond. There they gather smith's tools, tinker's tools, improvised weapons, and better clothing, while Loria listens at the next door and hears drunk goblins complaining about Ulgna, cursing Folcan, and talking openly about Grimstone taking prisoners deeper into the manor. A side room full of neatly arranged clothes and armor, including one magically preserved formal outfit, confirms that their captors have been collecting spoils from many victims before the party settles on an ambush.

### recap-002 | The Drunk Goblins Fall

- Kind: combat
- Beat IDs: beat-002
- Date: 1748-10-17
- Time: afternoon
- Transcript Range: u0424 -> u0805
- Locations: Perdoli Manor
- NPCs: Morky, Norrica, Skelver
- Organizations: none
- Items: Loria's short swords
- Enemies: Morky, Norrica, and Skelver

#### Short
The planned two-door ambush goes crooked, but the party still cuts down the three drunken goblin jailers in the guard room.

#### Intermediate
One barred door forces a quick rewrite, and the attack on the goblin guard room becomes a messy little brawl instead of a clean surprise. Loria hacks at knees, spells and missiles keep pressure on the room, and the three drunken jailers go down before the rest of the estate can react.

#### Long
The party's plan to hit the drunk goblins from two sides fails the moment one of the doors proves barred from the far side, forcing them into a noisier distraction and frontal rush. Even so, the guards are slow, drunk, and badly positioned, and the fight tilts steadily toward the prisoners despite darkness, confusion, and a few ugly moments. Loria is especially vicious in close quarters, while the others pile on with spells, steel, and missiles until Morky, Norrica, and Skelver are all dead in the forge-side room. By the time the magical darkness drops, the prison guards have been wiped out and the party has survived its first real test together.

### recap-003 | Breather on the Manor Grounds

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-003
- Date: 1748-10-17
- Time: afternoon
- Transcript Range: u0806 -> u1207
- Locations: Perdoli Manor
- NPCs: none
- Organizations: none
- Items: Acescale's playing cards, silver ring with weathered pearl, stolen rations and brandy
- Enemies: none

#### Short
With the first guards dead, the party reclaims stolen gear, takes a short rest in the smithy, and finally gets its first clear look at the overgrown manor grounds and nearby barracks.

#### Intermediate
The dead guards leave behind weapons, food, cards, and pieces of the party's missing gear, giving everyone a chance to rearm and breathe. After stepping outside to understand the ruined estate around them, the group settles back into the smithy for a short rest, levels up, and talks through whether to flee, hide, or press their luck.

#### Long
Once the immediate danger has passed, the party searches the goblin room thoroughly and starts piecing itself back together. Loria recovers her short swords, Acescale gets the playing cards back, Gaudin reclaims a silver ring set with a weathered pearl, and the room's stolen rations and cheap brandy make it clear that many prisoners have passed through this place before them. Stepping outside, they realize the smithy sits on the edge of sprawling Perdoli Manor, with overgrown formal gardens, hedges, a nearby barracks, and the larger villa farther off across the grounds. That view, plus the few hours of breathing room the goblins seem to have given themselves, is enough to justify a short rest, a meal, and a longer tactical debate about how to handle the rest of the estate.

### recap-004 | A Goblin Ruse Unravels

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-004
- Date: 1748-10-17
- Time: afternoon
- Transcript Range: u1208 -> u1531
- Locations: Perdoli Manor
- NPCs: Folcan
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Rather than rush the barracks, the party sends Gaudin out disguised as a goblin with Loria posing as a captive, but Folcan proves too alert for the bluff to hold.

#### Intermediate
The next move is a careful deception instead of another direct assault. Gaudin takes a goblin shape, Loria lets herself be staged as a prisoner, the others hide behind the hedge with the steel defender ready, and the whole performance nearly works until Folcan starts reading the scene too well.

#### Long
Instead of storming a building full of goblins, the party tries to trade nerve for leverage. Gaudin disguises himself as a goblin, Loria acts the part of a bound captive, and the others wait behind the hedgerow to support the act if it goes wrong. The approach is good enough to pull goblins out of the barracks and close enough to the path for Loria to feed Gaudin telepathic translations, but Folcan is not as drunk or as gullible as the first jailers were. He begins testing the story, issuing orders, and punishing the supposed offenders in a way that makes it obvious the bluff is about to collapse under pressure.

### recap-005 | Fog Fight by the Barracks

- Kind: combat
- Beat IDs: beat-005
- Date: 1748-10-17
- Time: afternoon
- Transcript Range: u1532 -> u1806
- Locations: Perdoli Manor
- NPCs: Folcan
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: Folcan and barracks goblins

#### Short
Loria's fog cloud turns the failing ruse into a fast skirmish, and the party kills the barracks guards on the path while dragging Folcan away alive.

#### Intermediate
The moment Folcan presses too hard, Loria drops a fog cloud across the path and throws the whole scene into confusion. Fighting from the mist and the hedgerow, the party cuts down the nearby goblins quickly enough to avoid a full alarm and keeps Folcan breathing just long enough to make him useful.

#### Long
When the deception finally breaks, Loria answers with a fog cloud that smothers the path, the hedge line, and any hope the goblins had of keeping the advantage. The party uses the obscuring mist exactly as intended, cutting down the exposed barracks guards before reinforcements can pour out in force and before the wider estate fully understands what is happening. Folcan is battered, overmatched, and ultimately captured alive, while the rest of the goblins on the path are killed quickly enough that the party can retreat back toward the smithy before the barracks organizes a proper response.

### recap-006 | Folcan Names the Tunnels

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-006
- Date: 1748-10-17
- Time: afternoon
- Transcript Range: u1807 -> u2113
- Locations: Perdoli Manor
- NPCs: Folcan
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Back at the smithy, Folcan cracks under threats and pain, revealing Grimstone's prisoner arrangement, the goblin numbers, and the tunnel routes that may reach the manor's wine cellar.

#### Intermediate
With Folcan tied up in the smithy, the party turns the rest of the session into a hard interrogation. He explains Grimstone's weekly prisoner-taking scheme, names Ulgna and the goblin numbers in the barracks, and admits that tunnels from the barracks, kitchen, and perhaps the chapel may lead toward the manor's wine cellar.

#### Long
Back at the smithy, the party revives Folcan only long enough to terrify him into talking. He describes Grimstone as a figure in black robes and a white marble mask who takes one prisoner each week into the manor and lets the goblins keep silver, copper, and lesser plunder in return. Under pressure, Folcan also reveals that around ten goblins plus Ulgna remain in the barracks, that hobgoblins hold the main approach, and that a tunnel network may connect the barracks, the kitchen, and perhaps the ruined chapel to the manor's wine cellar. By the end of the conversation, the group has not solved the manor, but it finally has a workable path inward and enough breathing room to turn survival into a plan.
