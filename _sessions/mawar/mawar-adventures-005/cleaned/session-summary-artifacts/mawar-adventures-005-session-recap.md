#### Short
Raina comes to the Leviathan with news that the ocean's song has returned and asks for help recovering a lost fragment from a hidden shrine near Hamri.

#### Intermediate
At the Leviathan Inn, Raina tells Wazir and the others that the sea has begun speaking to her again after years of silence. The visions point to a trapped fragment of song in a shrine of Guzu somewhere near Hamri, and she asks the party to help restore it.

#### Long
At the Leviathan Inn, after a broad recap of Hamri's recent troubles, Wazir gathers Ryu, Trok, Nerissa, and Ander to hear out a weathered sailor named Raina. She explains that she has heard the ocean in visions since childhood, that the songs fell silent years ago after a nightmare of tearing and loss, and that only recently they returned with a plea about something trapped under rock yet still resting on the waves. The voice showed her a shrine of Guzu the Mariner and begged for a fragment of song to be restored, so she has come to Hamri asking for help from people she has been told can be trusted.

Still at the Leviathan, the party works from Trok's sketch of the shrine and brings in local memory to test Raina's story. Yaz recognizes the image as an old Guzu shrine in the North Cliff sea caves, and Ovina confirms that the place collapsed long ago near the last tower.

#### Long
Still at the Leviathan, the group presses Raina for more detail and works from Trok's sketch of the place shown in her vision. Yaz recognizes the picture as matching an old shrine of Guzu the Mariner once built in a sea cave on the North Cliff, while Ovina, an old dwarf who has been in Hamri for more than a century, remembers the cave and confirms it collapsed long ago near the last tower in the line. With their clues aligned around a buried coastal shrine and a likely low-tide approach, the party settles on taking Ryu's boat out in the morning to search the site directly.

### recap-004 | Into the Sea Cave

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-004
- Date: 1749-05-27
- Time: morning
- Transcript Range: u0778 -> u1037
- Locations: buried sea cave shrine of Guzu
- NPCs: none
- Organizations: none
- Items: shattered reinforced door
- Enemies: none

#### Short
They swim through the breach into a blue-lit cave, find the sandy beach and ruined temple from Raina's vision, and push past idle water creatures toward the shrine.

#### Intermediate
The underwater opening leads into the hidden sea cave exactly as Raina described it: warm blue light, a white shrine, and a sandy beach under the cliff. The party also finds lounging water creatures and fresh signs that something blasted deeper into the temple before they arrived.

#### Long
The party dives through the newly blasted underwater opening and surfaces inside a hidden sea cave whose interior matches Raina's vision: a warm blue glow, a strip of sandy beach, and a white shrine building on the shore. They quickly realize the collapse was not the only recent disturbance, because the breach and the shattered doorway deeper inside both look as if magic blasted straight through stone and timber. A cluster of watery, frog-like shrine creatures loiters around the beach and temple, and after sizing up the scene the party presses inward instead of retreating for a longer plan.

### recap-005 | Secrets of Guzu's Shrine

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-005
- Date: 1749-05-27
- Time: midday
- Transcript Range: u1038 -> u1404
- Locations: Guzu shrine inner temple
- NPCs: none
- Organizations: none
- Items: prayer book, saints' story book, prayer ledger, song fragment crystal
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Inside the shrine they uncover prayer books and offerings, sing Guzu's ancestor prayer, and open the hidden chamber where the crystal waits in a trapped elemental's hand.

#### Intermediate
Searches of the sanctuary and chapel turn up old offerings, preserved books, and the wording needed to open the sealed passage. Nerissa's sung prayer makes the hidden stone door vanish and reveals the crystal in the grasp of a large water elemental pinned by the shrine's blue light.

#### Long
With the outer approach secured for the moment, the party searches the main temple and the side chapel in earnest. They study the statue of Guzu, the glowing altar, scattered offerings, and a damaged stone chest that still preserves a prayer book, a saintly story collection, and a ledger of prayers left at the shrine. The Mawarin inscription about kneeling and praying Guzu's ancestor prayer leads Nerissa to the right text, and when she sings it the hidden slab door vanishes to expose a deeper chamber. Inside waits a large water elemental figure pinned by the shrine's blue light and holding the crystal fragment the party came to recover.

### recap-006 | A Bad Deal Offered

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-006
- Date: 1749-05-27
- Time: midday
- Transcript Range: u1405 -> u1612
- Locations: hidden chamber of Guzu's shrine
- NPCs: none
- Organizations: none
- Items: song fragment crystal
- Enemies: none

#### Short
The elemental offers a bad bargain, but once the party learns the blue light has pinned it in place and that it will keep hunting the shard, they choose to kill it instead.

#### Intermediate
At the chamber threshold the elemental tries to bargain its way free, claiming it only needs to get clear of the blue light to leave with the crystal. The offer collapses as the party realizes the creature serves Gazankoa, triggered its own prison by seizing the shard, and will simply keep pursuing whoever carries it.

#### Long
Before steel is drawn, the party talks with the elemental in the hidden chamber and learns how badly things have gone inside the shrine. The creature explains that it forced its way in, seized the crystal fragment, and triggered the blue light bound to the altar, which now prevents its teleportation and keeps it trapped despite the opened passage. It offers to return to the plane of water and even let the party present the crystal to Gazankoa, but the more it talks the clearer it becomes that it will simply keep hunting the shard and anyone carrying it. By the end of the exchange, the party settles on bottling the creature up inside the shrine and killing it rather than trusting any bargain.

### recap-007 | Jet of Water to The Song Fragment Returns

- Kind: combat
- Beat IDs: beat-007, beat-008, beat-009, beat-010
- Date: 1749-05-27
- Time: afternoon
- Transcript Range: u1613 -> u2755
- Locations: Guzu shrine inner temple, Guzu shrine sea cave, Leviathan Inn
- NPCs: Raina
- Organizations: none
- Items: song fragment crystal
- Enemies: trapped water elemental, shrine water creatures, remaining shrine water creatures

#### Short
The shrine fight turns vicious, but the blue light helps wear down the elemental and its servants until the party takes the crystal, kills the last pursuers, and returns it to Raina.

#### Intermediate
The elemental opens with a crushing water jet that wrecks the party's neat doorway plan and drops Geo, but the fight slowly turns as the shrine's blue light drags against it and Nerissa's magic drives it backward. Even after the elemental turns to mist and tries to flee, the party keeps pressure on it and the surviving shrine creatures, then kills the last of them before they can break for Raina's boat and recovers the singing crystal.

#### Long
When the parley breaks down, the trapped elemental lashes the shrine interior with a massive jet of frigid water and turns the opening rounds into a hard scramble for space, footing, and survival. The fight stays dangerous through whipping strikes, grapples, boiling water, and a desperate mist-form retreat, yet the same blue glow that binds the elemental also begins helping the party, especially when Nerissa's spell drives it back into the chamber and the group can keep it under pressure. By the time the elemental finally collapses into a dying swirl and the last shrine creatures are cut down, the party has held the choke point, kept the danger away from Raina, and recovered the crystal. Its grateful song then leads straight into the handoff on the beach, the shared vision of Omni beneath the sea, and the return to the Leviathan as Raina prepares to sail in the morning.
