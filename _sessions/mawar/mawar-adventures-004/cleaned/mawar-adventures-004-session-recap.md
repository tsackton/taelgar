Ander reunites with Ryu at the towers, where Jaeson brings word that a beast north of Hamri is extorting lizardfolk gatza.

Ander finds Ryu at his cliffside tower and the two old friends catch up over tea and talk of Mawar, the sea, and gatza. Jaeson interrupts them with a rumor from Eskorola: something in the water is killing lizardfolk and demanding tribute.

Ryu, Ander, and Nerissa sail to Eskorola and hear from Itzal that a steam-shrouded killer has already left a written demand for gatza.

Jaeson's story draws Nerissa into the expedition, and the three of them head north along the coast. In Eskorola, Itzal explains that several villagers are dead, a burned body washed ashore with a warning, and the village has only days to decide whether to pay, flee, or fight.

#### Short
Amaya leads the party to the attack site, where Ryu finds an immense webbed claw print and a boiled shoreline before the group waits in Eskorola for dawn.

#### Long
At the marsh edge, the party studies the signs left behind and realizes they are dealing with something far stranger than an ordinary sea predator. Blu's later scouting reveals a huge mist-wrapped mound deeper in the marsh, so the party returns to Eskorola, listens to a failed gatza rite for the dead, and sets out again at first light.

### Nov 9th, 1747 (morning)

- Timeline Segment: timeline-004

- NPCs: steam imps, fire cult guardian, Chaessenth, fire mage

- Combat Beats: beat-005, beat-006, beat-008

#### Short
At dawn the party enters the misty marsh, survives an ambush by steam creatures and a fire cult guardian, then climbs the mound and discovers a chained dragon turtle and its mage handler below.

#### Long
The morning push into the marsh begins cautiously, with the party rechecking the threat note and following Blu's report toward the hidden lair. Their canoe is hit by steam imps and then by a towering fire cult guardian whose death explosion leaves them bloodied, but instead of turning back they heal just enough to climb over the mound and look down into the nest, where a black spear pins a dragon turtle under a fire mage's control.

### Nov 9th, 1747 (afternoon)

- Timeline Segment: timeline-005
- Timeline Key: (DR:: 1747-11-09), afternoon
- Resolution: part-of-day
- Beat IDs: beat-009, beat-010
- Locations: mist-nest interior pool, open western sea beyond Eskorola
- NPCs: fire mage, Chaessenth
- Organizations: none
- Items: black spear, golden amulet, Hiyasa's treasure map
- Combat Beats: beat-009

#### Short
By afternoon the party frees Chaessenth from the black spear, kills the fire mage, learns of Khathayi and the Wanui Teora, and watches the young dragon turtle head west into the deep.

#### Long
The second battle turns on the party's insistence on freeing the creature rather than simply slaying it. Once the spear comes loose, Chaessenth helps them break the mage's hold, tears him apart, and then shares what he knows of Khathayi, the songs of the ocean, and the westward call of the deep before parting from them.

Ander finds Ryu at his tower just as old loyalties and older rumors begin to pull them toward the sea again.

#### Intermediate
Ander comes to Ryu's tower to renew a friendship that has been dormant for twenty years, and the two spend a quiet morning taking stock of what Hamri has become. Jaeson then arrives with a story too ugly to ignore: something north of town is killing lizardfolk for gatza and may soon force a reckoning at Eskorola.

#### Long
Ander finds Ryu settled into his cliffside life above Hamri, where talk of the ocean, the town, and the strange pull of gatza fills the morning until Jaeson cuts in with urgent news. The rumor he brings is ugly enough to overpower nostalgia: a beast is killing lizardfolk near Eskorola and demanding tribute, and whether the story means profit or disaster, it cannot be left alone.

### recap-003 | Tracks in the Marsh

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-003
- Date: 1747-11-08 to 1747-11-09
- Time: none
- Transcript Range: u0650 -> u0937
- Locations: Eskorola
- NPCs: Amaya
- Organizations: none
- Items: threat note
- Enemies: none

#### Short
At the attack site the trail grows stranger, and by dawn the party has enough evidence to know the marsh is hiding something unnatural.

#### Intermediate
Amaya ferries the party to the place where the latest victim was taken, and Ryu finds a webbed claw print far too large for any local beast along with plants that look boiled by scalding water. Blu's later flight over the marsh reveals a huge mound hidden in the mist, so the party returns to Eskorola, hears a failed gatza ceremony for the dead, and resolves to move at dawn.

#### Long
At the attack site the evidence refuses any easy answer, as Ryu finds tracks that suggest a dragonlike swimmer and damage that looks more like boiling than simple flame. Blu's reconnaissance gives them a direction but not a solution, so the party spends the night in Eskorola with the dead unquiet and the village still undecided, then rises before sunrise to face the marsh.

### recap-004 | Into the Mists

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-004
- Date: 1747-11-09
- Time: morning
- Transcript Range: u0938 -> u1389
- Locations: Eskorola, salt marsh north of Eskorola
- NPCs: none
- Organizations: none
- Items: threat note
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Dawn brings no safety, only a slower, colder approach into the mists around the hidden mound.

#### Intermediate
The next morning begins with a hard paddle through fog, rain, and narrow channels as the party tries to turn rumor into a workable plan. They revisit the threat note, compare what little they know, and close on the mound Blu saw the night before, knowing they are entering someone else's chosen ground.

#### Long
Dawn gives them only enough light to see how little they truly understand, and the marsh answers every question with fog, drifting heat, and silence. Even so, they keep pressing inward, working from the note and Blu's report until the hidden mound is close enough that any further movement might trigger an attack.

### recap-005 | Steam in the Fog to The Burning Guardian

- Kind: combat
- Beat IDs: beat-005, beat-006
- Date: 1747-11-09
- Time: morning
- Transcript Range: u1390 -> u2029
- Locations: salt marsh north of Eskorola
- NPCs: steam imps, fire cult guardian
- Organizations: none
- Items: none
- Enemies: steam imps, fire cult guardian

#### Short
The marsh answers first with steam and shrieking wings, and the party is forced to survive an ambush before they can even reach the nest.

#### Intermediate
Nerissa's spell finally stirs the hidden defenders, and steam creatures rush the canoe out of the mist. Ryu and Nerissa cut down the first wave, but the attack is only a screen for the larger threat waiting at the tunnel.

#### Long
The mist erupts into violence the moment Nerissa probes it, and the party finds itself fighting steam imps from a cramped, unstable canoe. They handle the opening rush with skill, but the ambush makes clear that the marsh is defended in layers and that the way forward will not remain a skirmish for long.

### recap-006 | Over the Nest

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-007
- Date: 1747-11-09
- Time: morning
- Transcript Range: u2030 -> u2185
- Locations: outer marsh tunnel mouth, top of the mist-nest above the interior pool
- NPCs: Chaessenth, fire mage
- Organizations: none
- Items: black spear
- Enemies: none

#### Short
Instead of retreating, the party climbs over the mound and finally sees what the cult has chained inside.

#### Intermediate
With the guardian dead and nothing else rushing from the water, the party snatches a short breath, patches their wounds, and abandons the underwater route for a climb over the mound itself. A weak section of the nest roof gives them a view into the chamber below, where a black spear burns in the neck of a captive dragon turtle while a fire mage drives it onward from shore.

#### Long
The brief recovery after the tunnel fight gives them just enough strength to choose boldness over caution and climb the mound instead of turning back. From above they see the truth of the whole affair at once: the beast is no extortionist mastermind at all, but a chained dragon turtle named only later as Chaessenth, pinned and controlled by the fire mage who has been using him as weapon and warning.

### recap-007 | Arrow in the Neck to Breaking the Charm

- Kind: combat
- Beat IDs: beat-008, beat-009
- Date: 1747-11-09
- Time: none
- Transcript Range: u2186 -> u2914
- Locations: mist-nest interior pool
- NPCs: fire mage, Chaessenth
- Organizations: none
- Items: black spear
- Enemies: fire mage

#### Short
The second battle begins as a rescue, with the party gambling that the chained beast can become an ally if they can free it in time.

#### Intermediate
Ryu, Blu, and Nerissa throw themselves at the problem of the burning spear while Ander closes on the mage and tries to keep the spell pressure off them. Every attempt to pull the weapon free hurts, the dragon turtle thrashes under compulsion, and the mage keeps pressure on the party while trying to maintain control of his captive monster.

#### Long
The second fight is shaped from the first moment by a refusal to accept appearances: the party treats the dragon turtle as prisoner rather than monster and acts accordingly. That choice makes everything harder, since every hand laid on the spear burns and every moment spent helping Chaessenth is a moment the mage can use to punish them, but it is the only reason the battle ever turns.

### recap-008 | Songs of the Deep

- Kind: beat
- Beat IDs: beat-010
- Date: 1747-11-09
- Time: afternoon
- Transcript Range: u2915 -> u3192
- Locations: mist-nest interior pool, open western sea beyond Eskorola
- NPCs: Chaessenth
- Organizations: none
- Items: golden amulet, Hiyasa's treasure map
- Enemies: none

#### Short
The violence gives way to wonder, and the freed dragon turtle leaves the party with a song and a clue pointing farther north.

#### Intermediate
Nerissa heals Chaessenth and the chamber settles into conversation instead of combat as the young dragon turtle explains what little he knows of his captivity and the songs carried by the sea. From him the party learns that the slain mage sought enough gatza to reach Khathayi the Flametongue, and they recognize that the golden amulet recovered from the fight matches the mark already shown on Hiyasa's treasure map.

#### Long
Chaessenth, no longer a weapon, becomes the party's witness to a much larger mystery. He speaks of the Wanui Teora and the songs of the deep, confirms that the slain mage was seeking Khathayi through gatza, and then heads west into the open sea while the party is left holding a matched pair of clues that point toward an island farther north.
