# Emberwine

Emberwine is a fey realm, south of [[Amberglow]], west of the [[Sunset Gate]], and east of [[Everlight's Bloom]]. It is a realm of revelry and dance, a place where music and wine flow freely. It is a land of rolling vineyards and gentle hills, bathed in the constant golden light of the late afternoon sun.

%%SECRET

Fey realm south of Amberglow. 

A **realm of eternal revelry and indulgence**, where the **fey lords are too drunk on their own excess to act**, but where something darker is **rotting beneath the surface.**  

A land of **eternal feasting, intoxication, and excess**, where rivers flow with **smoldering, ember-red wine** that **warms the soul but clouds the mind.** The fey who dwell here have **forgotten what it means to be sober, and perhaps what it means to be themselves.** It is a place of **celebration that never ends—because to stop is to remember.**  



### Landscape

A grand **open-air feast stretches before them**, but it is not contained within walls—**this is not a castle, nor a court, but something older, something half-wild.**

#### **The Golden Vineyards**

- The **hills roll endlessly** under a sky of **deep amber and twilight rose**, filled with **rows of grapevines that shimmer in unnatural golds, silvers, and deep plums.**
- Great stone **banquet tables** are set between the vines, their surfaces **etched with swirling fey patterns** and piled high with **bowls of overripe fruit, platters of spiced meats, and goblets of deep crimson wine.**
- Lanterns **hang from ivy-draped trellises**, their light flickering in time with the **distant, unseen music.**
- At the highest ridge, **a ruined stone amphitheater overlooks the revel**, its old elven carvings **worn but not forgotten**, as if waiting for an audience that has not returned.

#### **The River of Wine**

- Flowing through the heart of Emberwine is **a slow, winding river—deep red, its surface catching the firelight of the feast.**
- **Low wooden barges drift lazily downstream**, carrying **masked revelers reclining on silk cushions, sipping goblets from trays carried by unseen hands.**
- Along the banks, fey sit **half-submerged**, drinking straight from the current, their laughter **echoing through the reeds.**
- At the river’s edge, **stone steps lead down into the water—but where they go, none can say.**

#### **The Forgotten Stage**

- Near the feast, beneath the towering vines, lies a **great stage**, its wooden planks **darkened with age, its curtains threadbare but still shifting with unseen movement.**
- The revelers **avoid it without seeming to notice**—never stepping too close, never acknowledging it at all.
- Sometimes, a **figure appears there—silent, watching—but gone when looked at directly.**
- A **violin, untouched, sits on a chair at center stage.**

#### #### **A Celebration That Never Ends—But Should Have**

- At first glance, **Emberwine is alive with joy**—laughter, music, revelry without consequence.
- **Goblets never empty, food never spoils, the wine never runs dry.**
- Guests **move between feasting and dancing**, each moment spilling into the next, **forever perfect, forever golden.**

But something **lingers beneath the revelry.**

- **Some smiles last a second too long.**
- **Some dancers move as if they have done so for centuries, no longer thinking.**
- **Some toasts feel hollow, as if spoken by habit rather than joy.**
