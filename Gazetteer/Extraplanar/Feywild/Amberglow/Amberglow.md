# Amberglow

Amberglow is a fey realm, located by many sources, including [[The Lore of the Feywild]], on the southern and eastern borders of [[Shimmersong]]. This realm features frequently in the tales and legends of [[Drankorian Empire|Drankor]] as a place of great beauty, a land of constant sunsets and golden skies on wide open plains of amber grasses and lush flowers. However, more recent sources suggest that it has fallen into decay. Many fey ballads sing the tragedy of the [[Cloudspinner]], the Queen of Sunsets, who vanished long ago, and the slow destruction of her once-marvelous realm that followed. 

![[amberglow-plains.png]]

%%^Campaign:dufr%%
There are, however, some pockets of the [[Cloudspinner]]'s court that remain. [[Lady Eventide]] hides in the crumbled remains of the [[Cloud Palace]]. [[Caulaus]], a trapped Drankorian solider who once wronged the [[Cloudspinner]] but has forgotten his mistakes, now guards the [[Heartwood Grove]] where [[Agata|Agata Dustmother]] remains imprisoned. Rumors of other holdouts and defenders exist as well, although what truth there is to these tales is not clear. Nonetheless, the land remains a bleak place, where the vibrant colors of sunset are bleached and desaturated, and those that linger struggle against malicious fey.

### Amberlights

During Cloudspinner's reign, she created Amberlights, lanterns of frozen sunset that contained seeds of her power. She would give them to favored allies, and even sometimes those who simply sought a boon - she was generous and cared for mortals oppressed by fate, in particular (hence her particular fondness for orcs). 

(Side note - this might be part of the conflict with Everlight's Bloom - whose fey ruler is the opposite, believes strongly in fate and not interfering; he probably blames the fate of Amberglow on Cloudspinner's meddling)

The power of the Amberlights faded when Cloudspinner was imprisoned. Not evenly, and not uniformly - those who had strong innate powers, such as [[Aelirios Sunblade]], could fan the embers longer. As Amberglow faded to darkness, many of more powerful fey in Cloudspinner's court established refuges, often relying on the power of the Amberlights. Others, who didn't flee, faded and were drained. 

[[Lady Eventide]] is an example of one who was drained of color and faded with her queen's realm.
[[Aelirios Sunblade]] is an example of one who fled.

The [[Refuge of the Gossamer Veil]] is perhaps typical of the refuges established by wielders of the Amberlights. There were likely others, some of which have since been captured by Umbraeth. 

Unlike others were the eastern watches: [[Night's Edge Keep]] and [[Redsun Watch]] are two, perhaps there were more. 

Lirien, the Lanternkeeper of Redsun Watch, was a minor member of Redsun Watch, charged with using the Amberlight to light the Sunset Gate, sealing cracks that would sometimes appear from Darkfall. She was not the main fey lord, though , that was Thyrian Duskward. 

Redsun Watch held out for years after Amberglow fell. For four hundred years, they valiantly held out. Lirien was young when Redsun Watch fell. Thyrian Duskward was killed by a river of shadow that cracked through the Sunset Gate, darkness flowing and surrounding Redsun Watch. Lirien, not knowing what else to do, grabbed the Amberlight and fled, passing through Emberwine before reaching Everlight's Bloom, where she has been hiding for the past few hundred years. 

Soon after Redsun Watch fell -- within years, maybe a decade or two -- the Amberlight, which Lirien had kept safe, went dim. Until 10 days ago, when it started glowing faintly, getting steadiily stronger until a week ago it was flickering bright and pulsing, pointing the direction to Cloudspinner. So Lirien left and came here.
