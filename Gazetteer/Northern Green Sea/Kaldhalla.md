# Kaldhalla
*(KAHL-hal-lah)*

Kaldhalla is a frost giant kingdom on the northern slopes of the [[Svinjo Mountains]], known as the [[Svinjo Mountains|Hrimthur]] to the giants. It is an ancient kingdom, founded long before humans settled the Green Sea, but since [[Vimfrost's War]] and the death of [[Bjarnfrost]] the Mighty, Kaldhalla has shrunk compared to its former glory. While once the might of Kaldhalla protected much of the [[~Northern Boreal Forest~]] from the dragons and other dangers of the north, now, the frost giants rarely leave their mountain strongholds. 

Under the rule of King [[Brimskarda]], the giants of Kaldhalla have come to dominate the cultures of the northern [[Svinjo Mountains]], and many dwarves and stoneborn exchange labor, sometimes hard and difficult, for the right to live in the protected steadings of the giants.

The largest and strongest of these, and Brimskarda's chief fortress, is [[Isenborg]].

For eons, frost giants have lived in the northern parts of the [[Svinjo Mountains]], which they call the Hrimthur (HRIM-thur). While Kaldhalla is extremely old, pre-dating human settlement of the Green Sea, for our purposes the recent history is all that matter.

In the days after the fall of Drankor, as dragons stirred in the Far North, a great king arose in Kaldhalla, Bjarnfrost the Mighty. He established Kaldhalla as a refuge, a place of safety, and in these days many dwarves, stoneborn, and even the Vargald of the forests to the east offered their allegiance. Jokulvarr as a mighty warrior, and also a poet of great renown. Some frost giants refused his rule, turned away and made alliances with Cha'mutte, but they were not welcome in Kaldhalla or the grand halls of [[Isenborg]] (EE-zen-borg), the ancient palace of the frost giants. 

In DR 1505, rumors of war and of dragons stirring reach Bjarnfrost, and he begins to gather an army. From 1505-1510 (approx), Vimfrost's war is fought, between the ancient white dragon Vimfrost and allies on one side, and the united army of Kaldhalla, Sembara, Zimka/Voskald, and the Skaer. However, while Vimfrost is defeated, few frost giants return to Kaldhalla, and Jokulvarr is killed. 

[[Storvalda]] (STOHR-vahl-dah), Bjarnfrost's daughter, claims the throne, and holds the remnants of [[Kaldhalla]] together for 40 years. But, in secret, a many frost giants who had fled a century before and pledged loyalty to Cha'mutte are massing an army. In DR 1549, Freyvidra (FRAY-vee-drah), marches east from her strongholds in the Sentinels, rampaging across the Vostok highlands and leading a horde of (orcs? something else?) south to punish those who supported Bjarnfrost against Cha'mutte. She gives little thought to these rampagers, and while rumors of chaos reach Embry in DR 1550, and some aid is send, little but garbled rumors reaches Tollen or the coast. In DR 1552, Freyvidra defeats Storvalda and claims Kaldhalla.

[[Freyvidra]] rules with an iron fist for 121 years, until DR 1673. During her reign, there are frequent raids on Stoneborn, dwarves, Vos, and Vargald alike, all to claim tribute that Freyvidra says is owed to the children of Vimfrost as weregild for Vimfrost's death. During this time, the vampire moves in, and Ursk expands, and probably some stuff happens in Vostok too, but little of it reaches the south. But the amount of tribute flowing north to the dragons is such that [[Isenborg]] falls into disrepair, and there is grumbling among the younger giants. 

In DR 1673, tired of what he perceives as the weakneess of Freyvidra, Brimskarda launches a coup, and defeats Freyvidra in single combat, claiming the kingship of Kaldhalla. From DR 1673-1692, Brimskarda fights the dragon wars, and defeats three of Vimfrost's children, reclaiming their treasure for his hall.

Over the course of these 20 years, Brimskarda slowly conquers a number of dwarven and stoneborn settlements of the [[Svinjo Mountains]]. He turns his back on Freyvidra's meddling in the lowlands, and brings back what he considers a traditional frost giant ethos, where prowess in arms is how the worth of a person is judged, and those who do not fight are only good to be servants, or worse. Nonetheless, since DR 1692, when the last dragon was repealed, no dragon has dared attack the [[Svinjo Mountains]]. 

For the past 60 years, Brimskarda has ruled, fat and happy, the palace of [[Isenborg]].
