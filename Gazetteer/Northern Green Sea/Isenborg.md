# Isenborg
*(EE-zen-borg)*

Isenborg is the primary steading of [[Kaldhalla]], and the hall of the king, [[Brimskarda]]. 

## Approach

![[steading-entrance.jpg|right|500]]Isenborg is located on the shoulder of a tall peak in the Hrimthur range, in the shadow of the mountain the frost giants call Spirenevald (SPEER-ehn-vahld). The northern lights constantly dance over the steading, even in daytime, giving it a strange magical glow, and illuminating the massive walls of ice and stone that encircle the steading. 

The entrance to Isenborg is marked by a grand, frozen gate, adorned with intricate carvings of frost giants' myths and battles. Sentinel ice sculptures, crafted to look like fearsome beasts and legendary giants, guard the pathways.

## The Steading

Within the walls of the steading lies a spacious courtyard, covered in snow, cold firelight flickering everywhere, providing light but not warmth. Training grounds, arenas for combat practice, and pens that house giant mammoths and massive goats, all dot the interior, along with several massive giant-sized dwellings. Carved into the glacier walls are warrens of smaller housing for servants, lined with fur.

## Brimskarda's Palace

![[brimskarda-palace-interior.jpg|right|500]]Brimskarda's Palace is the most notable landmark in Isenbord. The palace is a marvel of ice architecture, towering spires and domes crafted from blue and white ice, shimmering like a mirage. Giant-sized steps lead up to the main entrance, flanked by ice columns that radiate a soft, ethereal glow. Enormous ice sculptures of the frost giant's gods and ancestors decorate the palace facade.

Inside, the great hall is vast, with ceilings lost in shadow, supported by pillars of ice. The hall is dominated by the view of Brimskarda's throne, carved from glacial ice, seated upon a raised dais, with a long, frost-encrusted carpet leading up to it. Tapestries depicting Brimskarda's victories in battle line the walls, and everything is illuminated with a icy blue light. 

Many of the frost giants of Isenborg live in the palace itself, in spacious quarters, with furnishings carved from ice and stone, covered in furs and pelts of mythical beasts. The royal chambers are especially lavish, with tapestries made from the feathers of frost phoenixes and beds of polar bear fur.

## Atmosphere and Life
- Echoes of deep, rumbling voices and the clanging of metal on metal fill the air, alongside the howl of the wind outside.
- The cold is pervasive, and while fire is plentiful, it gives light and little heat
- Frost giants move about their daily tasks, training, crafting weapons from ice and black iron, and feasting on the spoils of their hunts.
 - Great halls dedicated to feasting and storytelling, where giants gather to share tales of glory and honor their ancestors.
- Shrines to the frost giants' ancient ancestors, and especially Tyravarr (TEER-ah-varr), considered the greatest of the elder titans who gave rise to the frost giants. Here, offerings of ice sculptures and jewels are made to seek favor or guidance.

## Population of Isenbord

There are 18 giants that live in / around Isenborg, and another ~30 that live in [[Kaldhalla]], but relatively far from Isenborg. 

The giants in Isenborg include:

- Brimskarda, his wife, his young daughter whom he is training to rule, his sister, her husband, and their two twin boys (Brimskarda's nephews). This group of 4 adults + 3 children are the center of the court.  (4 + 3)
- Three unattached adult giants (two men, one woman) who are loyalists to Brimskarda and hang around his hall.  (3) 
- An older couple, a man and a woman, who were crucial to Brimskarda's takeover, and are now mostly retired, plus their ~30-40 year old son (approx late teen / early 20s, roughly the age Brimskarda was when he launched his coup) who is a poet and a historian and is treated very poorly, but has not been driven away out of debts owed his parents (his parents are quite displeased with him as well). He likely has hidden depths, improvise.  (2 + 1)
- Four guards / tribute collectors that are responsible for managing tribute, guarding the dwarves/stoneborn, etc. Usually no more than one is present in the steading at any time. (0-1)
- The chief steward of the palace, who is responsible for overseeing the domestic staff and tending the animals. She broke her arm when she was young, and it didn't heal correctly, so she does not fight well and is also very low status in court, but kept around because tending the animals and the food is something you need a low status servant to handle. (1) 

Thus, at any time, there are likely 7-8 adults of fighting shape in the steading, including Brimskarda who is quite powerful. 

There are also 6 mammoths, a dozen or more giant goats, and about 30 dwarves and stoneborn who tend the kitchens, work in workshops, etc. 

The king also controls the loose network of a few dozen frost giants, some who willingly pledge allegiance and others who are more outcasts

## Names

### Male Names

1. **Bjarnfrost** - Combining "bjarn" (bear) and "frost," suggesting a figure as formidable as a bear with the chill of frost.
2. **Halgurd** - A name that merges "hal" (stone) and "gurd" (guard), implying a guardian as solid and unyielding as stone.
3. **Snjorik** - From "snjór" (snow) and "rik" (ruler), indicating a ruler with the purity and expansiveness of snow.
4. **Vetrulf** - Blending "vetr" (winter) and "ulf" (wolf), this name suggests a wolf of winter, cunning and fierce.
5. **Frosthildr** - A combination of "frost" and "hildr" (battle), denoting a warrior as cold and relentless as frost itself.

### Female Names

1. **Isgerdr** - Merging "is" (ice) with "gerdr" (enclosure), suggesting a protective figure as encompassing and resilient as ice.
2. **Skadisynja** - Inspired by Skadi, the Norse goddess of winter, and "synja" (sight), for a figure with the clarity and foresight of winter.
3. **Hrimnirja** - Combining "hrim" (frost) with "nirja" (daughter), indicating a daughter of frost, embodying its beauty and chill.
4. **Jokulmey** - From "jökull" (glacier) and "mey" (maiden), suggesting a maiden with the strength and majesty of a glacier.
5. **Vetrdis** - Blending "vetr" (winter) with "dis" (goddess), for a figure who embodies the essence of winter as a deity.
