# History of the Island

- **Escape from Hkar**: During the Downfall Wars, the destruction of Hkar led to a miraculous escape as a chunk of land carried survivors across the ocean to safety.
- **Memorial to the Downfall Wars**: The island became a monument, featuring massive statues and a pilgrimage path dedicated to the Eight Divines.
- **Magical Laboratory**: The island evolved into a center for scholarly research, with a port town and various Drankorian societies establishing communities.
- **Apollyon's Domain**: Control shifted to the Cleansed, with Apollyon constructing a secret tower in preparation for conflict.
- **Downfall**: A conflict between Cha'mutte and Apollyon led to a devastating plague and the destruction of Drankor, altering the island forever.

---

## Escape from Hkar

- **Downfall Wars**:
  - Agents of the Void Mind launched a devastating world war to reach Hkar and corrupt the Divine Spark embodied in the land.
  - Many mages of Hkar sailed in a great armada to aid the elves in **Alcarinque**, engaging in numerous battles.

- **Destruction of Hkar**:
  - It was deemed too risky to allow the Divine Spark to remain, leading to its destruction.
  - This act shattered and sank Hkar, causing incredible upheaval across Taelgar.
  - Many humans on Hkar faced imminent death in the chaos.

- **Miraculous Escape**:
  - Clerics and mages of Hkar and the Eight Divines, aided by the divine power of **Mos Numena**, caused a chunk of land to grow roots and walk across the ocean.
  - This landmass carried between 1 and 2 million people (maybe more?), though many perished in the chaos.
  - The island journeyed until it reached within sight of the coast (about 60 miles offshore), then stopped and grew roots.
  - The survivors settled in Drankor, explaining how it developed so rapidly.

---

## Memorial to the Downfall Wars

- **Island as a Monument**:
  - In the early days of Drankor, the island became a monument to Hkar, the Downfall Wars, the Eight Divines, and those who died.
  - It served as a pilgrimage site, especially associated with the Days of Salvation or Days of Thanks, marking the saving of people from Hkar.
  - Religious orders established themselves here, and for a few hundred years, it was primarily a shrine.

- **Massive Statues and Pilgrim's Path**:
  - At eight equidistant points on the shoreline, massive 150-foot-tall statues of the Eight Divines were constructed.
  - From these points, a magical stone path—the Pilgrim's Path—leads to the center of the island, spiraling along the central peak to the top.
    - **Total Distance**: About 50 miles.
    - **Pilgrimage**: Pilgrims were expected to rest at the beginning and then walk the path over 24 hours, typically starting at dawn and aiming to reach the peak before the next dawn.
  - At the peak is a temple complex with one building for each of the Eight Divines, arranged around a central courtyard housing a massive beacon and bell.
    - Includes rest facilities and administrative buildings.
  - Quarters and facilities were also established at the starting point of each path.

---

## Magical Laboratory

- **Shift to Scholarship**:
  - Over the years, especially starting during the Western Wars (DR ~600) and increasing during the Second Golden Age, the island evolved from a memorial to a place where the glory of Hkar could be studied and recovered.
  - A port town, **Alectia** (_ah-LEK-tee-ah_), grew just north of the statue of [[The Wanderer]], and became the center of the non-pilgrim community on the island.

- **Establishment of Research Communities**:
  - Various Drankorian societies established research communities, often organized like manors, cloisters, monasteries, or ancient universities.
  - Developments during this time included:
    - Numerous scholarly cloisters founded across the island.
    - A major library was established.
    - A magical forge was built.
    - A grand forum was constructed on the outskirts of Alectia.

- **Ruins and Remnants**:
  - Most ruins from this period are now destroyed.
  - A few teleportation circles still exist, particularly in the manors around Alectia and the Grand Forum. Perhaps especially including the [[Fides Lucaris]] manor?
  - The port city is still largely intact, and one manor has been reused as Cloudspinner's prison.
  - The library was burned down and is mostly destroyed, though some information could potentially be gleaned (presumably anything interesting is now in [[Ra'ghemdros Lair]])

---
## Apollyon's Domain

- **Rise of the Cleansed**:
  - As the Cleansed ([[Omnis Pura]]) rose to power, the island increasingly became their domain, eventually involving **Cha'mutte**.
  - Research cloisters were shut down, and most activity shifted to the western half of the island, around the forge and what becomes [[Apollyon's Tower]]
  - Pilgrimages do not stop, but this becomes somewhat fraught, and there is some degree of glorification of the primacy of "ancient Hkar" that creeps into the pilgrimages and monuments despite the best efforts of the priests of [[Mos Numena]]. 

---

## Downfall

- **Collaboration and Betrayal**:
  - During this time, Cha'mutte began collaborating with the Cleansed, using each other despite diametrically opposed goals.
    - **Cha'mutte's Goal**: To destroy the gods.
    - **Cleansed's Goal**: To make their leader the new god of humans and subjugate those deemed impure.
  - Cha'mutte discovered the Cloudspinner and her cloak for the orcs, something neither he nor the Cleansed could replicate.
  - Both collaborated on imprisoning the Cloudspinner to force her to reveal the cloak's secrets.

- **Escalation to War**:
  - The attempt to extract the cloak's secrets failed.
  - Cha'mutte insisted more time was needed, but Apollyon disagreed and launched his war against the orcs, stealing the cloak.
  - Cha'mutte saw this as an unforgivable betrayal and demanded Apollyon's surrender.
  - He moved in secret to gain complete control of Cloudspinner's prison on the Circular Island.
  - Apollyon had prepared a secret tower on the far side of the island in anticipation, taking over the magical forge.

- **Destruction and Plague**:
  - The conflict escalated, leading to the Plague and the destruction of Drankor as Cha'mutte deemed Apollyon's desires irredeemable. This is described above.

	  - The temple complex remained largely untouched, and the Pilgrim's Paths were protected by divine influence.
	  - Apollyon's tower remained protected from the Plague and Cha'mutte's wrath through protective magic. Cha'mutte attempted to destroy the tower, but triggered a trap long prepared, that triggered the storm and wounded Cha'mutte.

- **Aftermath and Isolation**:
	- A powerful storm, laid as a defense and a trap by Apollyon, shielded the island.

    - Eventually, Cha'mutte died, and the lieutenant remained complacent, content to torment Cloudspinner and enjoy her hoard

# Current Day Overview of the Island

- **Major Locations**:
  - **Pilgrim's Path and Divine Statues**: Still intact, protected by divine influence, along with elven memorials.
  - **Temple Complex**: At the mountain's peak, remains untouched.
  - **Ruins of Alectia**: The town, grand forum, and docks are visible but badly destroyed.
  - **Apollyon's Tower and Hidden Forge**: Still exist, protected by strong magic, undisturbed by Ra'ghemdros.
  - **Cloudspinner's Prison**: Remains, along with the base of the prison guards, overseen by [[Ra'ghemdros]].
  - **Natural Features**: Cliffs, rivers, forests, and the singing beaches.

- **Resting Rules**:
  - **Short Rests**: Can be safely taken anywhere not in an active lair.
  - **Long Rests**: Possible if well-concealed away from lairs; or in magically protected places, such as lingering refugee camps or pilgrimage locations.
  - **Restrictions**: Long rests cannot be taken within the three major lair areas: the ruined port, Ra'ghemdros's territory, and Apollyon's territory.

## Major Locations

### Pilgrim's Path and Divine Statues

- **Intact and Protected**:
  - The Pilgrim's Path and the massive statues of the Eight Divines remain intact, safeguarded by divine influence.

    - **M**: Eternal Leaves Garden.
    - **V**: Arch of Stars.
  - **Untouched by Ra'ghemdros**: These sacred sites have been left undisturbed by the dragon.

### Temple Complex at the Mountain Peak

- **Preserved Sanctuary**:
  - Located at the top of the central mountain.
  - Remains intact, protected by divine magic.
  - **Structures**:
    - One building for each of the Eight Divines, arranged around a central courtyard.
    - A massive beacon and bell in the courtyard.
    - Rest facilities and administrative buildings.
  - **Significance**: Continues to serve as a symbol of hope and reverence.

- **Visible Remnants**:
  - The ruins of the port town, including the grand forum and docks, are still visible.
  - **Destruction**: Badly damaged during the war between Cha'mutte and Apollyon.
  - **Potential Discoveries**:
    - May still hold oddities or remnants that provide insights into the island's history.
    - Areas for exploration and gathering information.

### Apollyon's Tower and Hidden Forge

- **Hidden and Protected**:
  - Apollyon's tower remains protected by strong magic.
  - The hidden forge still exists.
  - **Undisturbed**:
    - Ra'ghemdros has left these sites alone, either unaware of them or uninterested.
    - Potentially holds powerful artifacts or knowledge.

  - **Significance**:

## Resting Rules

- **Short Rests**:
  - Can be safely taken anywhere that is not an active lair.
  - Allows players to recover and prepare without significant risk.

- **Long Rests**:
  - Possible if the party is well-concealed away from lairs.
  - Alternatively, long rests can be taken in magically protected places, especially those related to:
    - Refugee camps with lingering magic.
    - Pilgrimage locations protected by divine influence.

- **Areas to Avoid for Long Rests**:
  - **Ruined Port**: Active lair with potential dangers.
  - **Ra'ghemdros's Territory**: The dragon's domain, fraught with risks.
  - **Apollyon's Territory**: Protected and possibly hostile area.

- **Strategic Resting**:
  - Players should plan rest periods carefully, utilizing safe zones.
  - Taking advantage of protected sites can provide benefits and reduce encounters.
