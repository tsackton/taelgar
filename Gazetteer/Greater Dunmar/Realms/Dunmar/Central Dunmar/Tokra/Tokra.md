*The city itself is surrounded by a solid, thick wall of stone, with several striking gates. A scattering of buildings stand outside the walls, mostly inns and merchant houses, but also including a small halfling neighborhood near the river.**

Near the temple, two other buildings, both tinged with Dwarven architectural elements stand. Rising over all the buildings in Tokra is a great tree, hundreds of feet tall and seemingly made of stone.

### Markets

There are three major markets in Tokra, each based in an open plaza.

The River Market, to the east, is the smallest, and functions mostly as a daily market for food and sundries for locals, operating roughly 8am - 11 am every day.

The Trader's Market, to the west, is the largest market and the most exotic. Operates three days a week, and is a major source of civic revenue as it costs money to set up a stall here. This is where booksellers, potion makers, other exotic wares, and goods from [[Chardon]], [[Sembara]], and beyond are sold. One addtiional day a week, this serves as an animal market.

The City Market, south of [[Jita]]'s square, is located in the midst of the artisan's quarter, and is lined with small shops, especially local artisans, smiths, etc. A weekly market brings various high-end goods for sale here. But regularly active with artisanal shops even when market is not operating.

### Jita's Square

The large, central plaza on which the temple, [[Archives]], and palace sit is known as [[Jita]]'s Square. In the center of the plaza is a statue/fountain of [[Jita]] riding a horse, [[Chidya]] standing in front with her hands raised, guiding [[Jita]].

### Temple

The temple is the largest building in Tokra, topped with a large dome that rises 300 feet into the sky, towered over the nearby buildings, even the palace and the [[Archives]].

Actually a temple complex. The main worship space is a pentagonal space beneath the dome, with significant altars to [[Five Siblings]]. There is also an entryway area, and private prayer space, clerical offices, and storerooms in the upper floors and the basement levels.

Attached to the temple is a major administrative complex, where acolytes manage funds, taxes, census data, and service obligations for eastern [[Dunmar]], technically now from [[Songara]] to Tokra.

The temple was constructed during the early years of the Dharajun dynasty, and shares the grand, almost ostentatious, architectural style that characterized the first part of the Dharajun years. Horse motifs are frequent, as is mosaic designs, and few surfaces are left undecorated.

### Palace 

The palace is a large rectangular building with a large central courtyard, originally constructed at the same time as the [[Archives]] for the use of the traveling court of the Darajun dynastic court. During the [[Great War]], this was used as the headquarters of the western army of the Dunmari, coordinating defenses against the eldritch horrors in the mountains to the north. During this time it was expanded and used as a barracks as well, so it is a bit of a clash of architectural styles, with a older "Dharajunic" building and a newer more utilitarian design.

During the [[Fire War]] it was damaged, but not as badly as the [[Archives]], and required only cosmetic repairs, which were done in a Dwarvish style.

### History

Tokra was originally the site of a Drankorian settlement, a river crossing and trade post associated with a pair of forts guarding [[Extraplanar Weak Point|extraplanar weak points]] to the north and south. After the [[First Plague|First Plague]] and the [[Settlement of Dunmar]], was resettled by the Dunmari but remained little more than a small village associated with the monastery, until Tokra became the center of administration of the Dharajun. After surviving the [[Great War]] relatively unscathed, Tokra was severely damaged in the [[Fire War]], but rebuilt and became a notable center of administration during the Nayan dynasty.

## Tokra History

Site of a Drankorian city, river crossing, and trade outpost, along with forts to the north and south protecting weak spots where other planes leak in (to the north, Pandemonium; to the south, where monastery is now, a major ley line node that was also connected to elven ritual magic).

Destroyed utterly during the first plague and the subsequent chaos.

Resettled as a minor outpost by Dunmari in DR 1183; Lakan Monastery built out of Drankorian ruins to the south in DR 1194.

For many years, little more than a small village associated with the monastery and a bit of trade, especially up and down the river which was navigable for much of the year.

In DR 1405, Samraat Dharajun Jita made Tokra her central court, ordering construction of a large temple, a palace, and archives. The palace was finished first, in DR 1409; the archives soon after, in DR 1411; the temple, a generation later in DR 1434.

For the next 100 years, Tokra was a major center of Dunmari adminstration and power, until the Great War.

Shortly after the Great War, in DR 1552, the fire giant Mavdyr attacked with [[Orcs]] and others, and Tokra was largely overrun and suffered much destruction. While the fire giant was defeated with the aid of dwarves and stoneborn, and Tokra rebuilt, the power center of Dunmar shifted west once again, to the new lands and to Nayahar.

Over the next 200 years, Tokra maintained significant administrative capacity as a frontier / provincial capitol, but as interest in eastern Dunmar waned in the west, Tokra's significance waned as well.

Nonetheless, it remains a prosperous city, sitting on one of the major long distance trade routes from Sembara to Chardon, and benefiting from the Hara river, navigable for about half the year. Aided in part by the present of the Lakan monks, Tokra has gained something of a reputation as a city of scholars, and the archives here store not just records of the Dharajun dynasty, but many earlier records rescued from Kharsan and points east during the Great War.

The horse herders and traders descended from the Dharajun rulers remain prominent and important here, and the Lakan monks hold considerable sway as well, having preserved the archives and history here.

## Tokra Locations

### Temple

The temple is the largest building in Tokra, topped with a large dome that rises 300 feet into the sky, towered over the nearby buildings, even the palace and the archives.

Actually a temple complex. The main worship space is a pentagonal space beneath the dome, with significant altars to [[Five Siblings]]. There is also an entryway area, and private prayer space, clerical offices, and storerooms in the upper floors and the basement levels.

Attached to the temple is a major administrative complex, where acolytes manage funds, taxes, census data, and service obligations for western Dunmar, technically now from [[Songara]] to Tokra.

The temple was constructed during the early years of the Dharajun dynasty, and shares the grand, almost ostentatious, architectural style that characterized the first part of the Dharajun years. Horse motifs are frequent, as is mosaic designs, and few surfaces are left undecorated (kind of Gothic Revival I guess?).

### Archives

The archives are a large square building with a significant tower rising 100 feet above the city, across the Dharajun Plaza from the temple. Also constructed during the early years of the Dharajun dynasty, this building suffered more damage during the Fire War than the temple, and was reconstructed in the simpler, less ostentatious, more flowing, melancholic, and organic style of post-Great-War Dunmari architecture.

Currently, the archives hold historical records from the pre-Great War era Dunmar, with a focus on military and religious records from the Dharajun dynasty. They also hold contemporary records of the western army of Dunmar during the post-Great-War period, although contemporary religious and civil records are kept in the temple administration complex.

The archives also have small holdings of books on history pre-dating Dunmari, especially Drankorian history, as well as collected records of the Great War assembled by archivists in the years after Mavdyr's invasion and the Fire War.

Access to the archives is heavily restricted and controlled by the Head Priest of the Tokra Temple.

### Palace

The palace is a large rectangular building with a large central courtyard, originally constructed at the same time as the archives for the use of the traveling court of the Darajun dynastic court. During the Great War, this was used as the headquarters of the western army of the Dunmari, coordinating defenses against the eldritch horrors in the mountains to the north. During this time it was expanded and used as a barracks as well, so it is a bit of a clash of architectural styles, with a older "Dharajunic" building and a newer more utilitarian design.

During the Fire War it was damaged, but not as badly as the archives, and required only cosmetic repairs, which were done in a Dwarvish style.

Currently, the palace is used for four purposes:

1.  It is the dwelling of the higher ranking members of the civil/religious adminstration
2.  The "royal quarters" are maintained for the Samraat as needed, although visits have been rare
3.  It is used as barracks and quarters for officers and commanders of the army
4.  The great hall and courtyard are used for civic ceremonies, holidays, and dispensing justice

On the west side of town, outside the walls, a large, open caravanserai that handles much of the merchant traffic, and serves as a significant marketplace for out of towners and travelers who don't pay the fees to sell in the market squares in town. Rooms, shops, stables.

Run by Darshana, a middle aged Dunmari woman, and her husband Yash. They have done well with trade and are "don't rock the boat" types.

The Red Lily

A halfling inn, also on the west side of town, just inside the walls, near the western gate. Run by Wes Strongbones and his twin sons Tye and Cade. Wes is a widower, having lost his wife maybe 10 years ago to illness. The Charmhearts are staying here, nursing Ander back to health. A cozy, comfortable place, with about 10 halfing sized rooms and another 20 human sized ones, plus a bunk room and stables, and a renowned kitchen. Mostly a merchant / out of town crowd, though.

A dwarven tavern, towards the northern part of town, in the (small) dwarven quarters behind the Archives. Has a few rooms reserved for traveling dwarves; otherwise, exclusively a tavern. Dark, cramped, interior, but serves great beer. Run by Vistra Fireforge, a retired mercenary / adventurer. Originally from the Yuvanti Range, but spent most of her time traveling, and fighting in the jungles, before settling in Tokra. Traveler caste, definitely touched by wanderlust.

Kirian's

 A substantial, three-story inn near the main market square. One of the few inns in town. Caters strongly to merchants and the wealthy, and especially Chardonian's and the like. Dunmari tend to camp outside, or stay at Darshana's. Run by Kirian, a retired Dunmari solider from the west. Definitely a strong "things are good the way they are" crowd here. 3 gp/day for party for decent rooms and plain food.

Vimal's

A fairly disreputable tavern on the east (river) side of town, outside the gates. Owned by Dunmari called Vimal, a middle aged man from Tokra. Bartender is Tannis, also Dunmari, center of a bit of a circle of people who fleece travelers, gamble. Knows Kenzo. Vimal's is not exactly a dissident bar, but doesn't ask too many questions.

### The Giant Tree

Has grown here since forever -- the city grew up around it. Has deep roots, but seems to be sustained mostly or entirely by magic. During the winter and summer dry seasons, turns to stone, or at least seemingly so, but then bursts into bud, flower, and leaf quickly after the monsoons. The few days after the first rain the emerging buds make the whole city smell of vanilla.

The leaves bud out in August/September, and it is fully leafed out by the autumnal equinox, usually. Then, by Dec/Jan, the leaves dry out, and fall, carpeting this part of the city. They are used for many things, including some local decorative arts.

In the fall, a daring few will scale the tree to harvest the seed pods for culinary purposes; these command a high value on the market. By city and Dunmari convention, the first harvest is tithed to the temple, but otherwise this is treated as communal property that anyone can harvest.

By Feb, the tree is dormant again, slowly turning to stone until the monsoon.

No one knows why it is here, how it survives, or what it means -- they just enjoy it.

## The Archives

Further notes on the archives.

Access: the Head Priest of Tokra, [[Kaleha]], formally controls access, but realistically this falls to Ardan, the chief archivist. He is deeply suspicious of any access requests right now, given the theft and the murders. Probably will not allow anyone in, without a deeply good reason, and especially without constant escort.

Organization: most people who work in the archives work as scribes, transcribed works, copying, etc. Various sections each have a head scribe, and there are also a small handful of Archivists who are sort of floating senior people.
