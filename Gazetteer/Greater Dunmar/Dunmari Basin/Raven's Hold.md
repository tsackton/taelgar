## History

Raven's Hold has its origin as a stronghold and defensive fortification in the early days of Dunmar. Nestled against the eastern edge of the central mountains, it provided a safe harbor during the long wars against hobgoblins and other dangers west and north, especially during the time when the bulk of Dunmari lived on the fertile plains east and south of here. Over time, as the might of Dunmar expanded, it because less a defensive fortification, and served more as a summer residence for pastoralists and herders.

During the Great War, Raven's Hold became a major forward outpost, calling back to its military history and use as a defensive fortification. Many settlements that had grown up around the mountains were cleared, and the outer curtain wall repaired and strengthened.

In the years after the Great War, when the Dunmari fell back in disarray, this became an outpost of the Blackened Claw hobgoblin tribe, who established a major fortification here, and had a war camp on the plains outside. During this time, [[Agata]] Dustmother traveled through here, and learned of the location. Over the course of a brutal and bloody generation, the Dunmari fought back the hobgoblins, driving them east into the Blasted Plains. As the magical defenses on the border were developed, Raven's Hold fell into quiet, as it was on the western side of the defensive enchantments. After this point, it was never used as a fortress by intelligent humanoids, although over the years monsters of various strips made Raven's Hold their lair for a time. Mostly, these were minor creatures of no import, although for many years Raven's Hold was the lair of a powerful pair of chimera.

## Dwarven Outpost

Sharing a valley with Raven's Hold is a dwarven fort, built into the mountain.

### History of the Outpost

In better days, this was a major trading outpost, iron mine, and forge, serving as a point of communication and trade between the dwarves from the southern part of the central mountains and the Dunmari. While the Brawnanvils had a major presence here, numerous other clans either lived or spent time here, including the Strongaxe, Boulderbeard, Redpeak, and Deepcavern clans.

Since the outpost was abandoned by dwarves, it sat empty for many years. While the hobgoblins had control of the Dunmari fort, the dwarven stronghold was occupied as a defensive fortification but never lived in or explored. After the hobgoblins were driven out and the fort fell under the sway of the border defenses, it sat empty for many years.

Gradually, creatures of the deep dark crept into the abandoned spaces, establishing lairs in places where they could hunt and find prey or food. Slowly, a few thralls (grimlocks and derros) of the mind flayers in the deeps, crept up here, some to escape and live in peace, others as spies on the outside world.

In late May 1748 (approx confirm date) the creatures inhabiting this place were killed by dwarves from Yuvanti.
