## L7: Lich Transformation / Tower

"With its phylactery prepared, the future lich drinks a potion of transformation—a vile concoction of poison mixed with the blood of a sentient creature whose soul is sacrificed to the phylactery. The wizard falls dead, then rises as a lich as its soul is drawn into the phylactery, where it forever remains."

This is where Apollyon prepared the potion of transformation, which he drunk in a panic during the fight with Cha'mutte.

This is a dark place. Apollyon sacrificed many people to create a potion. There are manacles on the wall, and a contraption that allows one to drain the blood of a creature and have it feed through a series of channels on the floor to a central well. 

The process of killing and stealing souls creates a haunt:
https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/4485876-haunt

Probably two haunts - run as one massive one, 2x HP etc.

But also should be clues about phlyactery here, as it is also where he constructed his phylactery.

Phylactery is a dagger. Made of sheets of adamantine, etched with runes, built up and up over years by using the growing dagger to murder more and more people. It is made to look plain, uninteresting, a jagged edge and an unadorned handle, the only thing about it is a slight reddish tinge from the blood. 

In the transformation tower, you can see the remnants of a small forge, obviously heated by magic. Anvil. Some adamantine. 

Clues:
- A **weathered anvil**, rusted and scarred by use.
- **Corroded tools**, such as hammer heads and tongs.
- Small **adamantine steel fragments** in cracks and corners of the room.
- Remains of **dagger parts**, such as blades or tangs.
- **Heat damage** or discoloration on the floor and walls from the magical forge.
- **Wear patterns** in the stone floor around the forge and anvil.
