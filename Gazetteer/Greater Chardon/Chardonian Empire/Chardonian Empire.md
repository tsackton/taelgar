The Chardonian Empire is a large and powerful realm ruled from the city of [[Chardon]]. Its power rests on the legions, the city’s institutions of learning and magic, and the wealth of the [[Chalyte|chalyte]] trade. The Chardonian Empire grew from the city of Chardon in the years after the Great War, expanding in fits and starts until it stretched across the entire western coast from [[Voltara]] in the north to [[Illoria]] in the south. Today, the Chardonian Empire is vast and powerful, the dominant cultural, academic, and military force in the west, a place of learning and magic and innovation, that sees itself as the defender of civilization against the forces of evil and the heir to the [[Drankorian Empire]] 

## The Empire and Its Lands

The empire’s heartland lies around [[Chardon]] and the [[Chasa]]: river valleys of grain and wine, coastal plains of wheat, and the busy maritime highway of the [[Gulf of Chardon]]. Beyond, Chardonian rule extends west, into [[Apporia]]; south, to [[Illoria]] and the [[Chataan Mountains]]; and north as far as [[Greater Voltara]]. 

Major regions of the empire include:

- The [[Chasa River Valley]], a broad agricultural basin whose river towns and market charters feed Chardon and carry goods to and from the interior.
- The [[Coastlands]], the long western littoral of farms, rivers, beaches, and wetlands that runs north and south of Chardon.
- The shores and tributaries of [[Lake Valandros]], a key inland route linking the northern frontier to the imperial core.
- The [[Northern Provinces]] beyond Lake Valandros, a lightly settled frontier whose wealth is tied to [[Voltara]] and [[Chalyte|chalyte]].
- The Apporian provinces on the [[Apporia|Apporian Peninsula]], a culturally distinct region tied to the sea.
- The southern mining province of [[Alta Tonaro]], centered on [[Argento]] in the northern [[Chataan Mountains]].
- The archipelago of [[Illoria]], recently conquered and governed as a protectorate.

## Rule and Administration

The far-flung Chardonian Empire is controlled and protected by the power of the [[Chardonian Legion]], and ruled by the imperial bureaucracy based in Chardon itself. The Magistros is both the ruler of the city of [[Chardon]] and the head of the vast imperial state, but control is exercised primarily through the extensive civil service and, in the provinces, by governors proconsuls, and chartered officials, often supported by legionary garrisons. The [[Curia of 42]], the elected assembly of the city of [[Chardon]], exercises limited control over the imperial bureaucracy, but retains the power to shape the economy of the Chardonian Empire via itse control over charter monopolies, administered by the [[Chamber of Stamps]].

Some provinces, such as [[Greater Voltara]] and much of the [[Northern Provinces]], have a strong and direct imperial presence; other places, such as [[Cedrano]] and [[Raziolo]] in [[Apporia]], have substantial local autonomy. [[Illoria]] is a special case: it exists as a vassal state with [[Chardon]] as suzerain, controlling naval affairs and foreign policy but allowing relative independence to govern local matters.

## Economy, Trade, and Chalyte

Chardon’s wealth arises from the combination of rich agricultural heartlands, a vast navy and mercantile fleet, and magical scholarship. Chardon trades grain and timber from river valleys, fish and salt from the coast, and the high‑value products of a city that prizes learning, craft, and arcane industry.
### The Chalyte Economy

[[Chalyte]]—a rare mineral imbued with the essence of the [[Plane of Magic]]—is the cornerstone of modern imperial power. It is a charter monopoly in the empire: refined chalyte can only be produced by license holders, and is regulated through civic offices in Chardon, allowing the city to profit from every chalyte transaction. The [[Politics of Chalyte (Chardonian Empire)|chalyte trade]] is complex, and historically controlled by a small number of [[Chalyte Oligarchs of Chardon|powerful oligarchic families]] with ties both to chalyte mining and imperial power, as well as the semi-secret magical society known as the [[Hetaeri Magica]]. 

Chalyte’s impact is not confined to furnaces and workshops. In Chardon, it powers civic marvels, including clean water and public lights, and cements the Imperial Navy's reach via the chalyte magic of the [[Windcallers]], who guide ships and bend winds with magic. 

### The Maritime Economy

The Chardonian Empire is the dominant maritime and naval power in the [[Gulf of Chardon]] and the [[Nevos Sea]]. The [[Old Chardon Canal]] connects the empire’s western coast to the [[Nevos Sea]]: built with magic by the Drankorians long ago, it is now a regulated and taxed passage. The canal’s tolls and traffic enrich [[Portalia]], and the approaches are guarded by forts and naval works; ships making the transit rely on windcaller pilots through the narrow, windless reaches.

The sheltered [[Gulf of Chardon]] is the empire’s maritime corridor, busy with fishing boats, merchantmen, and naval vessels bound for Chardon, Apporia, and beyond.

## Faith and Language

Across much of the empire, the Way of the [[Mos Numena|Eight Divines]] is widespread, and Chardonian scholars commonly speak of a distinct Chardonian tradition of the Way.

The dominant tongue of the empire is [[Languages#Chardonian|Chardonian]], a descendant of Drankorian speech written in the Drankorian alphabet. Chardon’s scholars and officials also preserve older Drankorian learning, though much scholarship is now written in Chardonian rather than the classical tongue.

## A Brief History

The empire is young by the standards of Chardon, whose own founding as a Drankorian fortress‑town predates it by more than a millennium. In the centuries after the [[Fall of Drankor]], Chardon grew as a city of learning and trade; in the generations after the [[Great War]], it began to consolidate power beyond its walls.

In the most commonly told imperial chronology:

- DR 1584–1597: The [[War of the Dark Rift]] erupts on [[Apporia]]. In DR 1597 the Umbral Covenant launches the Shadow Armada against Chardon and is destroyed; Chardonian writers often treat this as the decisive turning point, the moment when Chardon began to see itself not as an isolated city-state, but the seat of a nascent empire. 
- DR 1597–1619: Chardon’s campaigns in Apporia secure [[Portalia]] and establish imperial control over the [[Old Chardon Canal]]; the canal is reopened under Chardonian authority in DR 1619, and cities like [[Nevium]] flourish anew.
- Late DR 1600s onward: The discovery and exploitation of [[Chalyte|chalyte]] near [[Voltara]] transforms the empire’s finances and reach, tightening imperial control over the [[Northern Provinces]] and fueling the rise of chalyte‑powered magic and industry.
- DR 1689–1720: The chalyte boom in [[Alta Tonaro]] leads to rapid growth around [[Argento]], followed by decline as surface chalyte deposits dwindle and the [[Chataan Mountains]] are increasingly treated as unsafe for travel.
- The current era: Chardon’s legions have recently conquered [[Illoria]], turning a pirate archipelago into a protectorate.
