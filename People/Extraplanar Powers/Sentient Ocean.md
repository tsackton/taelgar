# Sentient Ocean
>[!info]+ Information  
> A living ocean

Along the coasts of the [[Mawar Confederacy]], the [[Apporia|Apporian Peninsula]], and other regions facing the [[Endless Ocean]], sailors and storytellers sometimes speak of waters far beyond the shore that sing, dream, or remember. These stories do not agree on what this power is, but all speak of a strange, alien power, a living mind of the ocean. 

This entity has been called many names, including Yi'weti by the old mages of the north; Ur Biyiak the name used by salt lizardfolk of the [[Mawakel Peninsula]]; Wanui Teora by the great beasts of the deep; and Ma'haya Kabir by the Mawaran saint h [[Guzo the Mariner]]. But most folks just call it the Sentient Ocean. It is said that that [[Society of Ocean Watchers|Ocean Watchers]] of [[Mawar Confederacy|Mawar]] know more than any land-dweller of the Sentient Ocean, but even among them it remains a fleeting presence glimpsed in waves, dreams, old songs, and the strange behavior of creatures from the western sea.

%%^Campaign:dufr%%
Those who have traveled among the [[Merfolk]] often report distinctive rumors and tales of the Sentient Ocean. For many years, the merfolk city of [[Omi]] was enclosed by a barrier created by the Sentient Ocean; passing through triggered seeming memories and visions of its history. A flight from a destroyed home, far away in another realm; the creation, or awakening, of the merfolk; a decision to become their protector after finding life in Taelgar's seas. Though these rumors are far from clear, they paint a picture of an alien entity from some distant extraplanar realm, possessing unfathomable power, serving as the creator, protector, and divinity of the merfolk.

### Canon to preserve

- The Sentient Ocean is a real intelligent oceanic power, not merely a sailor's metaphor.
- It is not a normal elemental and should not be treated as the essential power of water or as a native of the [[Elemental Plane of Water]].
- Its mind is alien but not inherently evil; its communication works through images, currents, pressure, salinity, temperature, music, and memory.
- It fled from another place after its homewaters were destroyed or collapsed, and found refuge in Taelgar's oceans.
- It created, awakened, or profoundly shaped the [[Merfolk]], who retain an intimate connection to its magic.
- It became a protector of merfolk and western ocean life, though its understanding of individuality, loyalty, worship, and mortal cultures is not humanlike.
- [[Omi]] was dear to it because of the Garden of Names, a sacred memory garden where names and stories were preserved in kelp, coral, current, and song.
- The [[Jade Piece of Rai's Hand]] amplified the Garden of Names and helped the merfolk channel the dreams or memories of the Sentient Ocean.
- [[Buruli]] corrupted Omi through [[Gazankoa]], seized the jade, and opened a portal to the [[Elemental Plane of Water]].
- The Sentient Ocean sealed Omi in a protective bubble to prevent Gazankoa's corruption from spreading, even though that trapped many merfolk inside.
- The [[Dunmar Fellowship]] defeated the Herald of Gazankoa at Omi, recovered the jade from Buruli on the Plane of Water, and enabled Omi's restoration.
- Mawaran lore preserves names, songs, drops, and fragments associated with the Sentient Ocean, but the relation between the Sentient Ocean and [[Mawaran Religion]] remains deliberately undefined.
- The Sentient Ocean's enemies seek fragments, songs, essence, mechanisms, or routes that expose its nature; possessing even a fraction of its essence may endanger the whole.

### Names and perspectives

- Yi'weti: old mages' name for living ocean water, especially in [[Airion]]'s lore.
- Ur Biyiak: lizardfolk sages' name for the living spirit of water heard in dreams.
- Wanui Teora: name used by the great beasts of the deep, tied to ocean songs.
- Ma'haya Kabir: name associated with [[Guzo the Mariner]], "the ancestor of voyages and the western horizon," protector of those lost at sea.
- Amidrayal Agura: an incomplete name preserved in the old stub and in related [[Yaz]] / Sentient Ocean material; the source does not yet say who uses it or what it means.

These should be treated as culturally situated names, not necessarily exact synonyms. Different speakers may be referring to different aspects, fragments, manifestations, or interpretations of the same larger power.

### Mawar-facing material

- [[Mawar Adventures Episode 01]] establishes a stolen drop or vial of the Sentient Ocean near [[Hamri]], recovered by the party and returned to [[Illius]].
- [[Mawar Adventures Episode 02]] and related notes connect [[Azar the Lost]], [[Nimessa]], [[Airion]], [[Gazankoa]], and Yi'weti/living ocean water.
- [[Yaz]] preserves the clearest in-world Mawaran lore about the names of the Sentient Ocean.
- [[Rayna]] and [[Caleb]] establish a Mawaran ocean-watching tradition in which the ocean can be heard through songs and visions.
- [[Mawar Adventures Episode 05]] and [[The Lost Legacy]] connect a Guzo shrine, a song fragment, Rayna's visions, and a water-plane attempt to seize a crystal connected to the Sentient Ocean.
- [[Guzo the Mariner]] and the name Ma'haya Kabir suggest a loose connection between Mawaran saints, voyages, and the Sentient Ocean, but this should not yet be formalized as doctrine.

### Omi and Dunmar Frontier material

- [[Ballad of the Jade Garden]] and [[Luminastra's Gift]] preserve surface-world rumors of the Jade Garden before the Fellowship found Omi.
- [[Dorian Almare]] preserves the grief-facing, surface-dweller edge of Omi lore: rumors that those lost to the sea could be heard in the garden, and a dangerous connection between mourning, the Sentient Ocean, and magical water.
- [[Omi]] is the best current public summary of the city, the Garden of Names, Buruli's betrayal, the protective bubble, and the aftermath.
- [[Visions of the Sentient Ocean]] is the best source for the Sentient Ocean's self-presentation, origin, emotional texture, relationship to the merfolk, and account of Omi's fall.
- [[Milo's Story]] gives an external adventurer's account of passing through the Sentient Ocean's strange waters, hearing a garbled version of the merfolk and Gazankoa story, and being captured by Buruli's forces.
- [[Kelp City Ruins]] preserves the DM map and city-state details: dead kelp fields, bleached coral, the rift, the city hall portal, captives, patrols, and the Herald of Gazankoa.
- [[Buruli Kingdom - Overview]], [[Buruli Kingdom - Portal Area]], [[Buruli Kingdom - Work Complex]], and [[Buruli Kingdom - Temple of Gazankoa]] preserve the Plane of Water side of the Omi arc.

### Conflicts and inconsistencies

- Public merfolk material calls the Sentient Ocean a psychic alien entity from an unknown plane; DM/worldbuilding material says it came from a Far Realm. Keep the Far Realm origin out of broad public-facing text unless intentionally canonized.
- The vision says the Sentient Ocean created or awakened the merfolk, while metaphysics discussions question how a Far Realm being could interact with Taelgarian soulstuff. Preserve the origin story without settling the soul mechanics.
- Some discussion suggests merfolk might worship the Sentient Ocean; the Omi vision says merfolk do not have religion in the usual land-dweller sense and that their mystic life centered on memory, names, and the Garden of Names. Treat "worship" as unresolved and probably imprecise.
- The Elemental Plane of Water note frames Gazankoa as a legendary leviathan or symbol of the plane's darker mysteries; Gazankoa's own note treats it as an elder elemental, kraken-like power, faction leader, or deeper abyssal intelligence. Do not overdefine Gazankoa in this note.
- [[The Lost Legacy]] says the Sentient Ocean and at least one faction of the Plane of Water have "always" been at war, while Omi material presents a specific conflict around Buruli, Gazankoa, and Omi. Treat "always" as mythic or shorthand unless a deep timeline is established.
- Session 100 DM notes say gatza is related to the Garden of Names through animals eating the blooming parts of the garden. The public [[Gatza]] note says gatza is harvested from large predatory fish of the [[Endless Ocean]] and used in lizardfolk ancestor rites. This connection is promising but not public canon yet.
- It is unclear whether physical "drops," "fragments," "shards," "songs," and "living water" are all the same phenomenon or different manifestations of Sentient Ocean essence.
- [[Rayna]]'s song fragment appears after Buruli's defeat and seems to guide her toward Omi, but the exact route, timing, and consequences of Rayna's journey are not fixed.
- The old stub preserved "Amidrayal Agura by the" as an unfinished name note. Do not complete it without a source.
- The scale of the Sentient Ocean is unresolved: it may be a region, a distributed mind in the [[Endless Ocean]], a power with fragments and currents, or some combination.
- The relationship between the Sentient Ocean, the [[Society of Ocean Watchers]], and [[Mawaran Religion]] is explicitly open. Current canon allows lore, visions, and songs, but not a formal religious order or doctrine.
- Dorian's account and the Omi/Garden material blur memorial, afterlife, memory, and resurrection imagery. Do not treat the Garden of Names as proof that drowned souls literally become merfolk or live eternally in the ocean.

### Open questions

- What exactly was the Sentient Ocean's original home, and how did the Riving destroy or expose it?
- Was the Sentient Ocean alone when it fled, or one survivor of a larger kind of oceanic Far Realm intelligence?
- Did the Sentient Ocean intentionally create merfolk, accidentally awaken sea life, or slowly shape an existing aquatic people?
- What is the metaphysical status of merfolk souls, and how does that interact with the Sentient Ocean's alien origin?
- How large is the Sentient Ocean now, and where are its boundaries in the [[Endless Ocean]]?
- How many merfolk cities are watched over by it, and what happened to the cities shown in the Omi vision?
- What are the practical limits of its power after Omi's restoration?
- Can it communicate reliably with surface dwellers, or only through rare intermediaries like [[Rayna]], [[Yaz]], [[Dorian Almare]], and merfolk?
- Are awakened sea beasts a side effect of contact with the Sentient Ocean, a deliberate act, or only an ocean-watcher belief?
- What exactly are the song fragments and crystalline shards, and how many exist?
- Why was a Sentient Ocean-linked shard hidden in a shrine of [[Guzo the Mariner]]?
- How does Ma'haya Kabir fit with Guzo's sainthood and Mawaran saintly intercession?
- What does Gazankoa want: destruction, control, consumption, containment, or access to the Sentient Ocean's essence?
- Are Gazankoa's followers one faction among many on the Plane of Water, or the main organized enemy of the Sentient Ocean?
- What happened to Dorian after being cured, and does he retain a connection to the Sentient Ocean?
- What happens to Rayna after she sails toward Omi with the recovered song fragment?
- What is the status of Omi after rebuilding begins, and how much has the Garden of Names been restored?
