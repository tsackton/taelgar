# Gazankoa
>[!info]+ Information  
> An elder elemental  
>> `$=dv.view("_scripts/view/get_Whereabouts")`

The entity called Gazankoa, frequently mentioned in the lore of [[Mawar Confederacy|Mawar]], [[Apporia]], and the [[Coastlands|Chardonian Coastlands]], is a powerful elemental being, rumored to gift magic to those desperate, foolish, or mad enough to seek it. Though sources differ on Gazankoa's exact identity, all agree on a deep connection to the [[Elemental Plane of Water]] and a longstanding hatred of the [[Sentient Ocean]].

## DM Secrets

### Nature

Gazankoa should be treated as a deep, ancient water-plane power: an elder elemental, kraken, leviathan, or possibly a name mortals attach to a broader abyssal intelligence within the [[Elemental Plane of Water]]. The strongest Dunmari notes describe Gazankoa as "an impossibly ancient monster of the deep," "an elemental of the plane of water but so much more," and "a kraken of the ancient days." The public [[Elemental Plane of Water]] note frames the same ambiguity more safely, with Gazankoa either a single vast leviathan or a symbol of the plane's darker mysteries.

The Mawar notes sometimes call Gazankoa an aberration rather than an elemental. This can be read as Azar-facing or DM shorthand for its alien malice, not necessarily as a final taxonomy. A worldbuilding chat also cautions against forcing Gazankoa too neatly into the same category as other elder powers such as [[Cha'mutte]] or [[Archfey Ethlenn]].

### Conflict with the Sentient Ocean

Gazankoa is part of the long war between certain powers of the [[Elemental Plane of Water]] and the [[Sentient Ocean]]. The Sentient Ocean is not simply water or a water elemental; the DM model describes it as an alien psionic power, possibly from the [[Far Realms]], that exists in oceanic form but is not the essential essence of water. Gazankoa's faction sees it as a competitor or invader to be destroyed, contained, or controlled. Other water-plane factions are indifferent to it or might even protect it.

Mawar complicates this picture. [[The Lost Legacy]] says that the [[Sentient Ocean]] and at least one faction of the [[Elemental Plane of Water]], including Gazankoa, have always been at war, and that Mawar was long a domain of elemental powers from which the Sentient Ocean kept its distance. The timing of any shift in that balance is unresolved, but the songs and fragments of the Sentient Ocean around Mawar are a continuing point of interest for both sides.

### Omi, Buruli, and the Jade

The clearest Gazankoa arc is the fall of [[Omi]]. In DR 1718, a piece of [[Rai]]'s jade hand landed in Omi's kelp forests. Over the next years, the merfolk learned to use it to channel the dreams of the [[Sentient Ocean]] and created the Garden of Names. In DR 1739, [[Buruli]], already jealous of the garden's power, made sacrifices and promises to Gazankoa. Gazankoa's power grew in him, a cult formed, and Buruli took control of the jade and the garden, corrupted it, and opened a portal to the [[Elemental Plane of Water]].

The Sentient Ocean sealed Omi in a protective bubble to contain the breach and prevent Gazankoa's faction from gaining access to too much of its essence. Buruli fled through the portal, leaving guards and a Herald of Gazankoa on the Material Plane side. His water-plane domain became a militarized outpost dedicated to extracting Omi's secrets, studying the weaknesses of the Sentient Ocean, and using Gazankoa's magic to transform captives into servants.

The [[Dunmar Fellowship]] broke this arrangement in DR 1749: they defeated the Herald of Gazankoa guarding the Omi portal, entered the [[Elemental Plane of Water]], rescued captured halflings from the [[Stormdancer]], infiltrated Buruli's temple to Gazankoa, killed Buruli, and recovered the [[Jade Piece of Rai's Hand]]. The campaign notes imply that this broke Buruli's power, disbanded his Gazankoa cult, and allowed the garden to be restored, though Gazankoa itself was not destroyed.

### Mawar, Azar, and the Blood Knife

In the Mawar material, Gazankoa works through grief, [[Gatza|gatza]], dreams, and predatory promises. [[Azar the Lost]] lost his wife [[Nadine]] and their sons in a storm, then became addicted to gatza and heard what he believed was Nadine's voice. That voice introduced Gazankoa as a power that could help him rescue his family from the ocean. Azar pledged himself to Gazankoa and soon received the [[Blood Knife]], which he used to murder victims, drain their blood to dust, and attempt to fill their bodies with transformed ocean water.

Azar's later recovery of [[Airion's Elemental Research|Airion's annotated books]] led him to the idea of [[Sentient Ocean|Yi'weti]], living ocean water that could make the ritual work. He used Airion's ring to compel [[Nimessa]], a marid, to steal a fragment of the Sentient Ocean and bring it to [[Hamri]], where planar geometry made arrival easier. Azar then used the living water to create drowned servants in the Drowned Flats and Splinters. Gazankoa also seems to have granted Azar a hard-to-see phantom boat in exchange for sacrifices, and Azar says the boat was tainted with blood and should be destroyed.

The Azar story is important for Mawar religion because it shows a dangerous, heterodox edge of Mawaran ancestor-seeking. Gatza, grief, and the spiritual closeness of the sea made Azar vulnerable to hostile impersonation or manipulation. Gazankoa did not need to appear as itself; it exploited the voice of Nadine, the promise that the dead were reachable through the ocean, and Azar's willingness to sacrifice outsiders before turning toward "better" victims.

Azar survived Episode 02 and left [[Hamri]] with [[Benat]] in DR 1747, but Episode 04 DM notes say he still had daily dreams and nightmares from "Garzankoa" while recovering. Treat "Garzankoa" as a spelling error for Gazankoa unless intentionally developed otherwise.

### Guzo's Shrine and the Song Fragment

The Mawar Episode 05 material connects Gazankoa's faction to the song shard kept in the [[Shrine to Guzo the Mariner]]. [[Guzo the Mariner]] had recovered a strange warm, ice-like singing shard linked to the [[Sentient Ocean]], and the shard was hidden in or remembered through the shrine. While Buruli still held the jade, the shard was quiet or inert. After Buruli's defeat, the shard became stronger and began drawing attention from both the Sentient Ocean and water-plane powers.

A marid broke into the shrine about a week before the party arrived, seized the crystal, and triggered a blue-light hallow or trap that blocked teleportation and extraplanar escape. Its lesser servants invoked Gazankoa outside the shrine, and the trapped creature offered to return to the [[Elemental Plane of Water]] and let the party present the crystal to Gazankoa. This should be read as a current Mawar hook: Gazankoa's faction still wants fragments, songs, or mechanisms connected to the Sentient Ocean, even after Buruli's defeat.

### Servants, Cult, and Symbols

Gazankoa's repeated signs are tentacles, dark water, altered dreams, sacrifice, and transformation. In Omi and Buruli's kingdom, the servants include loyal merfolk, mindless or bound elementals, transformed captives, Gazankoa-linked scholars, and a Herald of Gazankoa guarding the portal. Captives can be reshaped into shark-headed or tentacle-armed workers and fighters. Tentacles or corrupted kelp can touch the mind, causing haunted sleep and exhaustion.

The physical cult-symbol is an amulet with a pearl in the center and eight tentacles curling in a spiral, made in shell, resin, ivory, silver, or richer materials for higher-status servants. These amulets were not magical treasure in the usual sense, but they helped navigate the living pathways and hazards of Buruli's water-plane kingdom because those features responded to Gazankoa's symbols and the bearer's intent. Buruli wore an especially elaborate amulet of ivory, gold, and sapphires.

Buruli's temple to Gazankoa was built inside a massive leviathan skeleton, roughly 200 feet long. The temple included a whirlpool altar that served as a focal point for Gazankoa's power, a conch used to communicate with Gazankoa's other followers, a chamber for transforming prisoners, and the shell-mounted jade. The temple's imagery supports the idea that Gazankoa is both a worshiped power and a source of practical corrupting magic, not just a remote planar monster.

### Open Threads

- Gazankoa's exact nature remains deliberately unresolved: single elder elemental, kraken god, abyssal faction leader, or mythic name for a deeper water-plane intelligence.
- Sources differ on faction status. [[The Lost Legacy]] says Gazankoa is a member of at least one water-plane faction; [[Plane of Water Notes]] calls Gazankoa the leader of a faction that wants to destroy the Sentient Ocean.
- The relationship between the Omi jade, the Guzo song shard, and other Sentient Ocean fragments is intentionally not fully systematized. The strongest through-line is that Gazankoa's side seeks pieces, songs, mechanisms, or routes that expose the Sentient Ocean's nature.
- Azar's Blood Knife, phantom boat, and dream contact are still loose hooks. They show Gazankoa can act across distance through objects, dreams, sacrifice, and seawater, but the limits of that influence are not fixed.
