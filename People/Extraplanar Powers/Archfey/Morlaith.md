# Morlaith
>[!info]+ Information  
> An archfey (he/him)

Morlaith, also called the Briarheart, the Keeper of the Thornweald, and the Hidden Lord, is an ancient archfey who rules over a domain that lies congruent with parts of Addermarch, west of [[Twilight's Grace]] and east of [[Duskmire]]. He is old, strange, and remote: a faded remnant of the deep wild, an ancient echo of some forgotten dark forest of brambles, old paths, and hidden hollows. 

Morlaith's influence now lingers most strongly in the hills and river valleys of western [[Addermarch]], but old stories tell that his reach was once far greater, perhaps even stretching across much of what is now Maseau, Sembara, and Addermarch. His present domain is shrunken and uneven, with forgotten gates and crossings left behind where his attention has faded. This history is ancient, indistinct, and poorly recorded, more a matter of Addermarian folk legend than proper history. Standing stones, old warnings, and village rhymes preserve warnings of Morlaith, both the dangers of the careless and the songs and rituals to appease the forest. 

Morlaith does not maintain a court, or a manor, and does not engage with the politics of the Feywild. He is of the forest itself, dangerous unless appeased, but dangerous in the way the uncharted wilds are dangerous: not malevolent, but uncaring and perhaps even not fully aware of the small mortal things passing through his lands. His realms are not evil in any simple sense, but nor are they safe. Travelers who keep the old courtesies may pass where the careless are taken, changed, or lost.

## Addermarch Folklore

In [[Addermarch]], especially in the [[Highmoor]] and the older hill villages of the west, Morlaith is feared and placated rather than worshiped. Villagers set wards on ridgelines, leave small offerings, and repeat warnings and tales, out of respect for the ancient ways that kept their ancestors safe from the strangeness of the [[Fey]]. 

Around [[Haldrenn]], Morlaith is remembered as the Briarheart, the fey power behind old bargains and dangerous crossings. Local stories say he helps keep worse things beyond the passages, but they also warn that a broken rule can bring ruin on a village. Children know the Hollow Man rhyme, associated with the minions the forest sends against those who take from it:

> Knock, knock, knock;  
> The Hollow Man calls.  
> Swing, swing, swing;  
> His greedy sickle falls.  
> Chop, chop, chop;  
> He takes another head.  
> Burn, burn, burn;  
> The Hollow Man is dead!

## DM notes

### Canonical DM Secrets

- Morlaith's role in the Haldrenn crisis is not direct cruelty. [[Duncan of Haldrenn]] stole the heart of the Haldrenn Singing Tree while under the influence of the [[Serpentine Battleaxe]], and Morlaith allowed the neglected passage to open and allowed any to pass through rather than maintaining the old boundary -- normally Morlaith, when appeased, keeps the local fey from bothering the people of Addermarch. 
- Morlaith normally keeps some dangerous Feywild things, including the servants of other fey lords such as [[Lord Umbraeth]], on the far side of the crossings. When angry, or mistreated, or when the proper rites are not conducted, all bets are off. 
- [[Helen of Haldrenn]]'s account is the best player-facing formulation: the villages of the [[Highmoor]] have an uneasy bargain with the Briarheart, who helps keep worse things from coming through the old passages. See [[Addermarch - Session 11]], [[Addermarch - Session 12]]. 

#### Addermarch Role
- [[Caradoc]] entered Morlaith's realm as a young man, using the name Aurenxis, and has watched for signs of trouble connected to Morlaith for decades. The exact cause of his concern remains unresolved. Also open: what exactly happened to [[Caradoc]] in Morlaith's realm, and did he steal, bargain for, or merely witness something there?
- Returning the stolen heart to the Singing Tree is framed in adventure notes as the way to calm Morlaith and end the danger to [[Haldrenn]] and the Highmoor.

### Open Questions

- How much of Morlaith's former domain is settled canon? Sources consistently point to western [[Addermarch]] as his present core, but the older extent toward [[Cleenseau Region|Cleenseau]], the [[Enst]], and related borderlands is still flexible. Check with Cleenseau notes might be useful here. The Cleenseau notes suggest that thorn-maze gates in the Cleenseau Region may once have been tied to Morlaith before Duskmire expanded, but those notes are speculative and should not be treated as settled public canon yet. They are useful for the recurring image of Morlaith's old passages as thorn mazes that test, confuse, and sometimes release travelers rather than simply devouring them.
- Are the ancient standing stones around Addermarch specifically warnings against Morlaith, boundary markers against his influence, or broader fey warnings?
- How directly does Morlaith oppose Umbraeth? Current notes support Morlaith as a barrier to Umbraeth's expansion, but not as an ally of Ethlenn or Serenveil.
- Is Morlaith distinct from a traditional archfey? If so, how? Some notes suggest he might be older even than Ethlenn. 

### Vibes and Design Ideas

- Morlaith should feel like old fairy-tale danger: brambles, roots, hollows, songs, warnings, and rules whose reasons are no longer understood.
- His attitude toward mortals is indifference, not sport. Humans matter only when they disturb an old bargain, take what is not theirs, or break a pattern that kept a boundary intact.
- Addermarch's fey traditions should feel distinct from [[Tyrwingha]]. Tyrwingha has Ethlenn's patronage and protection; Addermarch has fear, courtesies, warning stones, offerings, and survival customs.
- Morlaith is a fading ancient power, but not a weak one. His realm has shrunk, and his attention is uneven, yet the rooted places that remain his are still dangerous.
- Morlaith works best as a setting force rather than a conventional antagonist. The Haldrenn incident is an example of what happens around him, not the definition of who he is.
- Addermarch should feel fey-heavy but unlike Tyrwingha: more fear, warning, and Germanic fairy-tale danger than gratitude toward a protector.
- Morlaith has an "Old God" or ancient forest entity feeling, and may once have ruled a much larger region before newer fey realms displaced him.
- Morlaith is agnostic to humans rather than actively mischievous. Humans are beneath him; the danger comes from old laws and broken supports, not personal malice.
- Morlaith's control of portals is ancient and unexplained. Umbraeth has reason to care about that power, but Morlaith is still not an Umbraeth ally.
- Morlaith and the Wilds should keep Umbraeth, Serenveil, and Ethlenn's lieutenants confined to the edges of the Addermarian Feywild.
