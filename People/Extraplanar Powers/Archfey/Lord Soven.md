### **Lord Soven, Master of the Revels**

Lord Soven is the dance, the music, the celebration—**the master of the feast who cannot let it end**. His power lies in **motion, beauty, and joy**, but it is also **his prison**. He is **the center of Emberwine**, and **its gilded mask hides the hollow beneath**.

Epithets: "Keeper of the Dance"; "The Antlered Host"; "The Warden of the Endless Song"

#### **Appearance**

- Towering and elegant, standing over seven feet, with the grace of a dancer and the bearing of a king.
- Antlers gilded, twisted with ivy and trailing crimson ribbons that flutter as he moves.
- Hooves polished like gold, tapping lightly with the rhythm of the music—his movements always in time, part of the dance.
- His face is warm, smiling—perfectly welcoming—but his eyes, if caught in stillness, hold a deep weariness. He does not rest, because rest is the end.

#### **Immediate Mannerisms and Player Impressions**

- Rhythmic: He moves with the music—every gesture flows into the dance, every word lands with the beat.
    - He might turn a question into a sweeping hand toward a dancer, or clink his goblet along the edge of a harp as he passes.
- Physical Touch: He guides with gentle contact—a hand lightly on the back, fingers adjusting a cloak, turning a wrist to raise a cup.
    - Always inviting them into the rhythm, drawing them into the revel.
- Redirecting Laughter: When pressed with something uncomfortable, his response is a laugh—a rising cheer, a lifted glass, a playful nudge toward the dancers.
- Vivid Imagery: His speech is rich with sensory details, but nothing is ever quite precise—he describes how the wine feels on the tongue, the warmth of the sun, but avoids names, places, hard facts.
    - “The bloom of the vine, the rush of the strings, the way the sun kisses your skin here—better than any sunset, yes?”

#### Personality & Emotional Undercurrents

- He genuinely loves the revel—beauty, music, laughter—this is his power.
- The feast is his shield against despair.
    - If the dance falters, the silence will come—and he dreads it more than anything.
- He knows the feast is rotting, but he cannot admit it.
    - The beauty must be real, or what is left?

#### Knowledge (And His Gaps)

- He does not know the location of the Prismwell.
- He knows the places where knowledge might be found, but there are gaps in his mind—holes he cannot fill, even as he tries to gloss over them.
    - Shrouded Archive (Everlight’s Bloom): “Ah, yes... the Archive. The Bloom holds it... somewhere. I’ve never... but someone found it, didn’t they? I remember... no, I don’t.”
    - Count Vashan: “The Broken Mask danced in her court. He remembers... if he dares.”
    - Eryndel: “Ah, he’s dueled half the realms. He’s crossed blades with captains at the edge of the light. Perhaps... Redsun... Night’s Edge... but who can say?”
    - Sunset Gate Keeps (Redsun, Night’s Edge): “The last embers... her court held there, I think. Secrets locked in stone. Or is that just a tale? No, no, it was real... wasn’t it?”

#### Getting Him Talking

Soven responds best to guests who buy into the feast—who join the dance, raise a cup, admire the beauty.

- Compliments on the revel: “The music here... there is nowhere like it.”
- Praising beauty, even when they see the rot: “Such richness, such color... even as the fruit softens.”
- Participating in the dance, even lightly.
- Sharing a story of joy found in sorrow—but it must end in celebration, not grief.

When guests play along, he will open up, and the gaps in his knowledge will surface naturally, though he will try to cover them.

- “The Archive... yes... there’s a path, I’m sure of it. Or there was. Someone... yes, someone brought a book once... didn’t they?”

#### What Puts Him Off

- Questions that break the illusion—anything that implies the feast is false.
    - “How long have you been dancing?”
    - “What happens when the music stops?”
- Pressing too hard on his memory gaps—he will cover with laughter, offer wine, pull them into the music.
- Rejecting the dance or feast outright—standing apart, refusing to join.
    - He will never grow hostile, but they will find themselves guided away from answers.

#### Cloudspinner Reaction

- A carefully veiled ache.
    - He will speak of her beauty, her light—but in the past tense, and quickly pivot back to the present.
    - “She spun the sky into gold... the embers lit our steps... but that was long ago. The music is here. Let it be enough.”

#### Sample Lines for Flow

- Greeting:  
    “Ah! New guests! Your step is light—good, good, the wine is sweet, the sun is warm. Come, come, you are welcome.”
- Deflecting Pain:  
    “Ah, secrets... there are always secrets. But this—this is life. Feel it, drink it, let it hold you.”
- Covering a Memory Hole:  
    “Redsun... yes, I think. The captain had a fine blade... or was it gold cups? No matter—there is music, and we are here.”

#### Final Impression for the Players

- The Perfect Host—until they see what he’s hiding.
- Every laugh is half a plea: Don’t let the music stop.
- Every dance is half a distraction: Don’t look too closely.
- But if they listen—between the notes—they will hear the cracks.


### Lord Soven, Master of the Revels

Lord Soven - normally known simply as the Master of the Revels - is a towering, regal figure. Elegant and graceful, standing seven feet tall, with hooves and graceful antlers but a human face and body. 

- His **antlers are gilded, gleaming like candlelight**, and **wine-dark ivy twines around them**—**a crown, or a weight he cannot remove.**
- His **hooves are polished gold**, tapping lightly as he moves among the guests, never still for long.
- His **smile is warm**—but **beneath the surface, his eyes show a deep weariness, an old sorrow buried under the mask of a smiling host.**
- His **goblet is always full,** but **he never drinks.**

#### **How Soven Acts**

- He is **welcoming, warm, and effortlessly charming**—his voice like **a melody, his laughter full-bodied and rich.**
- He **greets all newcomers as honored guests**, offering them **wine, song, and a place at the table.**
- But if someone lingers **too long on the silence between notes, on the cracks in the feast’s grandeur**, his voice turns **gentle, quiet, almost pleading.**
    - **“Why do you look for something more? The wine is full, the music plays. Isn’t that enough?”**
- He **does not lie**—but he does not tell the full truth.
- He **will never ask the party to stay—but he will never tell them how to leave.**
#### **What Soven Wants**

Soven has **forgotten why the feast began.**

- The revel was **once a celebration of something true—but that truth has been lost.**
- The **Shrouded Vault** may hold the answer—or **Count Vashan, the Broken Mask**, may be the only one who remembers.
- If someone could **remind him of what Emberwine was meant to be**, he might **find the will to stop.**
- But he **does not know if the realm can survive without the revel.**
