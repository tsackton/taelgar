Valeris

A **satyr musician and gambler**, cursed to **speak only the absolute truth** after losing a high-stakes wager. He came to **Sunwine Hall to drown his frustrations**, but his inability to lie has **ruined his charm, his games, and his patience.** Despite this, Lord Soven keeps him around **because his music is excellent and his curse is amusing.**

Calthis **wants out** but **needs a reason to leave.** His curse can only be broken by **a true selfless act**, but he has **no idea what that means** and keeps **getting it wrong.** If the party convinces him that guiding them might **count as selfless—or at least be more fun than wasting away here—he’ll come along.**

#### **Why He Might Leave With the Party:**

- **He’s already sick of the revel and looking for an excuse to go.**
- **If they convince him that guiding them is a chance to break his curse, he’ll seize the opportunity.**
- **If they offer a compelling adventure, he might follow just for the thrill.**
- **He literally cannot deceive them, making him an invaluable ally—whether he likes it or not.**

#### **Backstory: A Gambler Who Played the Wrong Hand**

Calthis grew up in **the rolling vineyards of Emberwine**, not far from Sunwine Hall but distant enough that the revel was an **occasional indulgence rather than a way of life.** He was a **musician, a gambler, and a charmer**, the kind of satyr who could **talk his way into anywhere and play his way out of anything.** He roved between **wine-soaked courts, roadside feasts, and hidden gambling dens**, living off the thrill of **high-stakes wagers and whispered promises.**

Then, **he played one wager too far.**

The details change with every telling—**some say he tried to cheat the Lord of the Revels himself, others whisper it was a sorcerer of Duskmire, and a few claim he gambled against the night itself**—but the end result was the same:

**He lost.** And the consequence was **this curse of absolute, scrupulous, insufferable honesty.**

At first, he thought he could **outwit it.** But he quickly learned that **his tongue betrays him the moment he tries to deceive.** If someone asks a question, he must **answer it fully, plainly, and truthfully.** If he tries to hold something back, **his mouth forces it out anyway.**

With his tricks ruined, his charm blunted, and his reputation **shattered**, he did what anyone in Emberwine would:

**He came to Sunwine Hall to drink until it stopped hurting.**

#### **Why He’s Still at Sunwine Hall**

Calthis **hates the revel now.** It should be a paradise for a satyr like him, but without his usual flair, he **can’t enjoy the game.** He can’t flirt without immediately ruining it, can’t bluff at cards, can’t fake a laugh at a joke that isn’t funny. **Everything feels flat and exposed.**

Yet, he isn’t **kicked out**, because even with his misery, he **brings something valuable to the revel**—

**He is a phenomenal musician.**

When he plays, his fingers still move like they always did, and his voice still carries the warmth of Emberwine’s golden afternoons. His curse does not stop him from singing songs that aren’t his own, and when his frustration boils over, his improvisations **are cutting, hilarious, and uncomfortably true.**

**Lord Soven keeps him around because he finds him entertaining.**

- He likes **watching him struggle**, but he also **genuinely enjoys his music.**
- The guests **laugh at his misfortune**, but they **linger when he sings.**
- The irony is that Calthis has **never performed with more honesty**—and the revel loves him for it.

#### **Why He Might Guide the Party**

Calthis **wants out**, but he **cannot leave without a reason.**

**His curse can only be broken by a selfless act**, but the problem is:

**He has no idea what a selfless act actually looks like.**

- “I don’t suppose letting a mortal win at cards would count?” _(It did not.)_
- “Giving up my best bottle of wine? No?” _(Still cursed.)_
- “Sincerely complimenting someone without tearing them down in the next sentence? Harder than it looks.” _(And still didn’t work.)_

### **Ysara the Golden & Valeris**

Ysara was **beautiful, aloof, and radiant**—the kind of fey who **never should have left Everlight’s Bloom.** She would arrive at the revels **like a comet streaking across the sky**, dazzling and impossible to ignore.

She wasn’t **cold, exactly, but distant**—as if the world she walked through was **a story she was only half-aware of.** She danced with abandon, laughed like **nothing could touch her**, and spoke in **wild, golden promises** that never seemed to cost her anything.

And Valeris—**who knew better, who understood the weight of words**—watched with **fascination and frustration.**

He knew **her type.**

**The ones who make oaths like they’re tossing petals into the wind.**  
**The ones who think nothing will ever catch up to them.**

And, of course, **something did.**

She swore **love and forever** to someone who knew **exactly what they were doing.** A promise that **should have been impossible to break**—except she did, **as easily as she discarded a dance partner.**

But the Feywild does **not** forget.

Somewhere, the fabric of things **began to pull tight.** The price of her oath **was coming due.**

And that’s when **Valeris stepped in.**

Was it **arrogance?** Yes.  
Was it **a thrill, a challenge, a chance to prove he could bend fate itself?** Absolutely.  
But was it **also just a little bit of real emotion, the tiniest flicker of something almost like care?**

Maybe.

He didn’t do it **for her.** He did it **because the idea of someone like Ysara getting caught by something as mundane as consequence felt wrong.**

She was **a golden thing, a streak of light through the revels, a figure that should never be caged.**

And so **he did what he did best.**

He **spoke a lie so perfect that the world itself listened.**

And then—**Ysara, being Ysara, ruined it.**
