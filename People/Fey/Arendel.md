# Arendel
*(AARON-dell)*
>[!info]+ Biographical Info  
> A [[Fey|fey]] (he/him)  
>> `$=dv.view("_scripts/view/get_Whereabouts")`

Arendel is a boisterous and friendly member of [[Lord Soven]]'s court. He is friendly, and loud, and looms larger than life, full of laughter and a quick wit. He loves to duel, and is extremely good at it, having rarely been beaten in his many years of fighting. He is a gracious winner, though, pleased and happy to be challenged. Though he often wagers on his duels, he almost never demands a payment that would be a hardship for his challenger to pay.

%%SECRET

### **Eryndell, the Laughing Blade**

(AARON-dell)

Eryndel is **boisterous, loud, and larger than life**, **the kind of presence that draws a crowd just by laughing**. He is **always in the center of things—telling tales, raising toasts, challenging someone to a friendly bout**. He **acts like he has nothing to forget and no regrets**, but **the truth is more unsettling**:

- **He cannot remember where he came from**.
- **He cannot remember when he arrived at the revel**.
- **All he remembers is the dance, the drink, and the duels**.

That **emptiness is covered over with noise—more wine, more stories, more laughter, more steel**.

---

#### **Appearance**

- Broad-shouldered, golden-haired, face **weathered but handsome**—though **perhaps it is just the wine giving him that glow**.
- Wears **a dented, gilded breastplate**, **scuffed from duels but polished enough to gleam**.
- **Sword always at his side**—**silver, slender, elegant**, but **a faint rust at the hilt where wine was spilled long ago**.
- **Eyes bright and merry**, but **if you catch him alone, staring into his cup, there’s nothing there—just a flicker of confusion before he laughs again**.

---

#### **Immediate Mannerisms and Player Impressions**

- **Raises a toast to everything**: A greeting, a question, a fight, a joke—**all are excuses to lift a cup.**
    - “To new friends! To good steel! To the sun that never sets!”
- **Claps shoulders, laughs loudly**—**physical presence**, **always close**, **always pressing**.
- **Tells tales that are increasingly absurd**—**each story bigger, wilder**, and **almost certainly false**.
    - “Did I tell you about the time I dueled a dragon? Fine bladesman. Green one. Very polite.”
- **Sometimes freezes, mid-sentence**, if **asked about his past**.
    - **Frown, hand tightening on his goblet—then laughter, louder than before.**

---

#### **Personality & Emotional Undercurrents**

- **Lives as though he has no regrets—but that’s because he has nothing left.**
- **Filling the emptiness with noise, drink, and victory**.
- **His skill with a blade is real—because it is the one thing that grounds him**.

---

#### **Knowledge**

- **Knows of Night’s Edge Keep / Sunset Gate Keeps**:
    - “Dueled a captain there—fine blade, steady hand. I took his coin, his pride... and a story. He spoke of the Amberlights—secrets hidden in the keeps.”
    - **When pressed about the Prismwell:**
        - “I don’t know the well, but if I were you, I’d walk where the sun sets. That captain said the lights led there... and the lights were hers.”
- **Does Not Know the Prismwell’s Location.**
- **Does Not Know His Own Story.**
    - If asked about his past:
        - “Oh, I’ve been here forever! Or... well... a long time. Since before the wine turned red. Or... no, that’s not right. I came after the storm? No, that was someone else. Doesn’t matter! We drink, we fight!”

---

#### **Getting Him Talking**

- **Join the drinking. Laugh with him. Play along.**
- **Challenge his tales, but with humor—not to expose him, but to match him.**
    - “A dragon, was it? I fought his brother—a fine singer, terrible hand with cards.”
- **Express admiration for dueling and swordsmanship.**
- **If they best him in a duel, he becomes more honest—vulnerable, but grateful.**

---

#### **What Makes Him Retreat**

- **Serious questions about his past—too hard, too quickly.**
- **Prying into his empty memories in front of others.**
- **Refusing his toast without a good-natured reason.**
- **Mocking his dueling skill—this will provoke, not make him talk.**

---

#### **Cloudspinner Reaction**

- **Detached, almost theoretical.**
    - “She was a queen, was she not? Light on her fingers, I imagine. But I never danced with her.”

#### **Duel Mechanic – Quick Combat Challenge for a Level 16 PC**

**The Duel is 3 Rounds. Whoever deals the most total damage wins.**

##### **Eryndel, Fey Duelist**

- **AC 19 (Breastplate, Shield)**
- **HP 150 (medium challenge for 3 rounds)**
- **Speed 40 ft**
- **Attack (Multiattack):**
    - **Silver Saber:** +10 to hit, 2d10+6 slashing damage.
    - If he hits **with both attacks in a turn**, he can **add 2d8 psychic damage once per round** (called **“Laughing Cut”**).
- **Bonus Action:**
    - **Flourish:** Disengage + Dash (can close gaps or reposition gracefully).
- **Reaction:**
    - **Parry:** +3 to AC against **one melee attack that would hit**.

##### **Combat Notes:**

- **Fights aggressively**, but **with showmanship—flourishes, feints, crowd-pleasing spins**.
- **Cheering Satyrs gather around, jeering and encouraging both sides.**
- **Winning:**
    - Eryndel respects the victor, calls for a drink, offers **the story of Night’s Edge Keep**.
- **Losing:**
    - Laughs it off, but **will respect the PC later**.
    - **Satyrs mock the loser lightly**, but **the fight is sport, not hatred**.

##### **Sample Duel Flavors / Lines**

- “Ah! Good strike—worthy of a song! But I’ve not spilled a drop of my wine yet!”
- “To the next cut—may it be clean, and may it hurt!”
- “Win or lose, we drink after. That’s the rule. That’s always been the rule... hasn’t it?”

#### **Final Player Impression**

- **Fun, boisterous—easy to like.**
- **A peerless duelist—but hollow beneath the laughter.**
- **When the wine wears off, and he sits alone, he remembers nothing—and that terrifies him.**
- **But tomorrow, he will drink, fight, and laugh again—because it is all he has.**
