The owner of the caravanserai in Karawa, the eponymously named [[Ikram’s]]. He is proud of his establishment, the food and drink it serves, and the commerce it attracts. Perennially optimistic, sociable, and friendly. Always ready with a drink and a story, to tell or to hear; prone to gossip and particularly fond of travelers and new stories. 

%%SECRET
Secrets: 
He has a magical amulet to Laka that his son found several years ago on the trade road; he keeps it secret because he believes it blesses his wine and doesn't want people to think he is cheating. He can be convinced to reveal it if he trusts the party not to repeat the secret (DC 15 Persuasion check)

Stories and rumors:
Ikram is fond of telling stories about the hyena attacks of March 1748, embellishing them somewhat in the telling. Has taken to playing up the Elf's role and exaggerating his relationship with him. Will point to Delwath and say 'but the Elf can tell you more, here was there, I just heard of it second hand'

Ikram can also tell them about Devana; if they are particularly friendly / charming can warn them to approach carefully as he is distraught over losing his son and livelihood. If they are asking about other attacks can bring this up.

Ikram can also tell them about more strangers than usual — one in particular seemed odd, a lone traveler passing through, seemed unusual, but he just stayed a night and sometimes we get all types. His name was [[Arcus]]. His coin was good, albeit foreign, and he kept to himself. Wearing traveling clothes, just stayed the one night and headed east. Paid in strange coin -- the silver is true, but they have a portrait in profile on the front of a strange man, and a the head of staff shining light on the reverse.

Since the gnoll attacks, the flight to Tokra, and [[Sura]]'s return, these old tales seem quaint and minor in comparison.
