Nayan Sura is the younger sister of Samraat [[Nayan Karnas]]. Once seen as a future Samraat and a unifier of [[Eastern Dunmar|eastern]] and [[Western Dunmar]], she vanished eight years ago, trapped by [[Agata]] Dustmother in the [[Mirror of Soul Trapping]]. In her absence, her brother, [[Nayan Karnas]], claimed the throne of [[Dunmar]]. In DR 1748, Nayan Sura was freed from [[Agata]]'s imprisonment, and now seeks to reclaim her destiny. 
## Overview

Sura was born in [[Darba]], but traveled widely in her youth, including spending several years at the [[Lakan Monastery]] in [[Tokra]]. From a young age, she was seen as god-touched, and perceived as a potential heir to the Dunmari throne, one who could unite the eastern and western factions of the divided country. Her future was cut short in DR 1740, when she was just 20 years old: she was trapped by [[Agata]] Dustmother in the [[Mirror of Soul Trapping]], and vanished. In DR 1748, she was freed by [[Dunmar Fellowship]], and began to contemplate reclaiming the throne, especially in light of her perception that Karnas had neglected the east, most notably in his decision to leave the eastern nomads to their fate during the [[Summer Gnoll Raids of 1748]]. In December of DR 1748, she defeated a small force of Karnas' warriors and their Chardonian allies in the [[Battle of Tokra]], laying claim to central and [[Eastern Dunmar]].

## Events

- Spent two years learning under the guidance of the [[Lakan Mystai]] at the monastery outside [[Tokra]], from DR 1735 - DR 1737. 
- Traversed across [[Eastern Dunmar]] in DR 1740 with the then Samraat, Nayan Marathu, as part of a great census of all [[Dunmar]], the first census to travel east in many years. Between [[Bas Udda]] and [[Askandi]], she was kidnapped by [[Agata]]'s servants - lured out of her tent by a magically disguised [[Orcs|orc]] and then knocked unconscious and brought to [[Agata's lair]] by [[Samerki]], where she was imprisoned in the [[Mirror of Soul Trapping]]. 
- Freed from the [[Mirror of Soul Trapping]] by [[Dunmar Fellowship]] in the summer of DR 1748. Seeing what seemed to be continued neglect of the needs of the east by her brother, she prepared to press her claim to rule, hopefully avoiding war and relying on the gods to give a clear sign of her favor. 
- Led her troops to victory against the Chardonian battle mages and a small group of Dunmari warriors loyal to Karnas during the [[Battle of Tokra]] on December 14th, 1748.

### Backstory

Circumstances of capture: Eight years ago (DR 1740), Sura and the then Samraat, Nayan Marathu, were traveling with the court across [[Eastern Dunmar]] in support of the great census, the first in many years. They made it to [[Askandi]], and were traveling north across the edge of the desert towards [[Bas Udda]] and the surrounding villages. One night as they were approaching the southern edge of the [[Red Mesa]], after speaking with one of her court attendants (actually an [[Orcs|orc]] in disguise, using Suggestion on her) she had a sudden urge to go for a walk and look at the stars. After a few minutes walking, realizing she was out of camp with no guard, she looked around with a start, saw a giant, spiked club swinging for her head, and then remembers little else. A vague memory of being half awake in a dark, smoky room, someone holding her face towards a mirror, wondering why her head and arm were bloody, and then a sensation of flying and being squeezed and a few minutes of a blank, foggy mist until she emerged, here.

The Political Situation:

In DR 1721, Samraat Nayan Lina comes to power at 19, a fifth-generation descendent of Yarad. Her rule is turbulent and marked by increasing dissent among the eastern clans, and increasing doubt that the gods are truly guiding the decisions of the Dunmari priesthood and Samraat. 

When she dies young and childless, at 34 in DR 1736, the succession is thrown into doubt. Her cousin is pushed by the western temples, but the eastern clans refuse to acknowledge him and to everyone’s surprise, he agrees with the critics. He claims not to feel the presence of the gods in his actions, that he has felt no divine spark, no inspiration to rule, no calling to be Samraat, and he cannot in good conscience take this power. 

After weeks of prayer and debate, an elder of one of the western clans from the high plains between [[Darba]] and [[Tokra]] is asked to lead. Samraat Nayan Marathu takes over as Samraat in DR 1736, at the age of 60, seen as a caretaker to begin the process of building unity across [[Dunmar]]. 

Although he takes the Nayan dynastic name, he quickly establishes that his court will be a traditional one, not based out of a palace in Nayanhar, but rather moving across the country with the seasons. He allies himself with prominent families from [[Central Dunmar]], but also disrupts the relationship with [[Chardon]], taking a less obsequious and more independent (although not adversarial) stance. He also brings the court east of [[Tokra]] for the first time in generations, organizes a census of all [[Dunmar]], proposes a new capitol between [[Tokra]] and [[Darba]], and a reorganization of border defenses, especially to the south. While these priorities involve a heavy tax, and it is an ambitious agenda for someone who was seen as a caretaker, he is widely supported. 

In DR 1740, he travels east with Sura (a woman from a prominent family near [[Darba]], but who spent some time in the [[Lakan Monastery]] in her youth and is widely seen as the obvious heir to Marathu) leaving her brother Karnas in [[Nayahar]] to take command of the southern armies and solidify the southern defenses. He is murdered between [[Askandi]] and [[Bas Udda]], and Sura goes missing, presumed to be dead. 

Karnas, Sura’s brother and in de facto control of the army in [[Nayahar]], proclaims himself Samraat, with the support of the western church and his family, a prominent [[Darba]] clan. This causes a lot of discontent in the east, especially as Karnas increasingly puts [[Dunmar]] under [[Chardon]]’s sphere of influence. 

With the troubles on the eastern border in DR 1748, Karnas effectively abandons the territory west of the [[Hara]], establishing [[Tokra]] and the [[Hara]] river as the eastern defensible border of [[Dunmar]] proper.
