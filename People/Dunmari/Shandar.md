# Shandar
>[!info]+ Biographical Info  
> A [[Dunmar|Dunmari]] [[Humans|human]] (he/him)

An old Dunmari man who spent many years as [[Agata]]'s table. Freed in [[Session 30 (DuFr)]].

%%SECRET

Shandar's Story
Father and daughter, typical Dunmari nomads from the early 1600s (~150 years ago). [[Kaya]] had three older siblings who went to war during the [[Northern War]] against the [[Blackened Claw]] clan. Sonal, Shandar's wife and [[Kaya]]'s mother, had already lost a child to sickness earlier and couldn't bear the thought of losing more of her children, so she forbade her youngest ([[Kaya]]) from joining the war and secretly went to [[Agata]], asking her for something to keep her children safe in battle. [[Agata]] gave her some charms or other minor magic and promised her children would return from the war. In exchange she asked for her dreams. She then proceeded to give her nightmares that made her edgy and paranoid although she could never remember them (and was never able to remember a dream again). When her children's bodies were returned from the war, dead, she snapped and threatened [[Agata]], who did something to break her mind (no one knows exactly what). She returned to town witless and confused, remembering little but a few memories of her family. [[Kaya]] thought she could trick [[Agata]], and went to beg for her mother back, offering a year of her life in service. [[Agata]] accepted, gave her a potion to give to her mother, and put a spell on her compelling her to return in 10 days time. [[Kaya]] knew she could not beat the spell, but convinced her father they could fight (Shandar was a skilled bowman from the wars). So they returned, armed and ready to do what they could, and found themselves totally outmatched. [[Agata]] offered them a deal: surrender, and I will let you live, as long as you promise that as long as you live I will be your guests in [[Dunmar]], allowing [[Agata]] to easily ignore [[Shakun]]'s protection. They never returned to the Dunmari plains, and Sonal spend the rest of her days dreaming of them slowly turning to wood in various horrible ways.
