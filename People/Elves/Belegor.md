Belegor is a solitary [[Elves|elf]] wanderer, who has travelled across the forests of the western region of Taelgar for many years, not wanting hide in isolation as many of his generation chose after The [[Great War]]. 
## Overview

Belegor is an [[Elves|elf]] of the 36th ka, the generation that came of age during the [[Great War]]. He chose to wander in his later years, after fathering children, and is driven by a sense of wanderlust but also melancholy for what was lost. 
## Description

Belegor is a tall, ageless [[Elves|elf]], with medium length copper hair, pale skin, and green eyes. He wears elegant clothes in greens and yellows, with high collar and long, wide sleeves. Graceful.

## Roleplaying Notes

- Belegor is marked by both wanderlust and despair. His interactions are often imbued with a deep sense of loss, especially for the past and the future he believes will never materialize.
- While he shares the pain of his generation, he chose not to go into hiding after fathering children, unlike many of his contemporaries. His beliefs align with the isolationist factions, and he firmly believes that the world owes the [[Elves|elves]] some respite.
- Despite the changing times, Belegor remains a constant reminder of an era bygone. His presence brings forth the tales of the [[Great War]], the cities that were lost, and the elven civilization that once flourished.
## Secrets

Belegor is from same generation as [[Delwath]], the 36th ka, born in 5601 / 1468. He was young when the [[Great War]] started, in the midst of his Age of Discovery. Like many of his generation-mates, he spent his Age of Discovery at war, and saw many [[Elves|elves]] die to dragonfire, to [[Hobgoblins|hobgoblin]] hordes as far as the eye could see, to the creeping darkness of the monsters of the [[Shadowfolds]] spawned when [[Cha'mutte]] fell. Like many of his generation-mates, he returned from war to a society changed, cities destroyed, many lives lost. And even more cities not destroyed, but simply vanished -- to where, no one knows, although some think that whole cities 'fled' to other planes, others think that many [[Elves|elves]] went to fight evil and protect Taelgar from the dangers of the multiverse, and still others think that the unraveling of [[Riving]] that [[Cha'mutte]] started but never finished (since he was destroyed) drew many of those closest to [[Pandar]] and the center of his power to other realms against their will, and the bulk of the [[Elves|elves]] may still yet survive, scattered across the multiverse.

Belegor, like many of his generation, mourned deeply the lost of the great cities of the [[Ainumarya]]: Oromandi, Valindir, Tiressa, Eled-tor, Carnande. With the weight of elvish civilization feeling lost to many, his generation turned inward, seeking to preserve what they could. Some fled to the [[Feywild]]; others built isolated cities deep in the most hidden parts of the forest, and hid them with magic. Many of [[Delwath]]'s generation did not return -- even some of those who survived became refugees among the [[Humans|human]]. The next generation is very small, and has motivated an inward turn among the [[Elves|elves]] who remain. Those who do seek adventure, and who search for glory and meaning, tend more to search for the lost [[Elves|elves]] than trouble themselves with the doings of [[Humans]].

Belegor sides with the isolationist factions, but unlike many of his generation-mates he did not want to go into hiding after having children. However, he also did not want to return to the days of old when the [[Elves|elves]] were protectors of the world, and felt and still feels the world owes the [[Elves|elves]] some respite for all they have sacrificed. Taken by wanderlust and despair, he left the isolated, magically hidden city in the north where his children were born. He came to [[Elderwood|the Elderwood]] about 15 years ago, and stayed with the Deno'qai to here their stories and legends and learn their tales. When he encountered [[Mezzar|Grimbaskal]] in "elven" form, he was immediately suspicious, and when he learned the truth he fled for fear of his life. He is now in hiding among the [[Te'kula]], with a target on his back -- should he ever leave he is sure he will be killed by [[Mezzar|Grimbaskal]].
