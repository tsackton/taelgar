He is the author of [[The Lore of the Feywild]], and is acknowledged as something of an expert on the lore of the [[Feywild]], although he has never had the courage to travel there himself.

He is an older man, maybe 200 years old, a neatly trimmed white beard and wavy white hair, with a big nose and blue eyes.

## Notes/Info

Ulfgar reminds me a bit of the character from The Neverending Story, Engywook - a gnome who spends his life studying the oracle and watching from a distance. He's fascinated about it in theory ("it's my scientific speciality!") but also very scared of it and nervous about the whole thing. So maybe Ulfgar would be sort of anxious in this way - attracted to the idea of planar gates, eager to share his theories and diagrams, but completely panicky when it comes to actually getting anywhere near them.

Ulfgar may know, and heartily disapprove of, some interdepartmental mixing, quite inappropriate really, among the faculties of magic and metaphysics, resulting in actual experiments in planar magic being conducted right here at the university! Of course he will vote to deny tenure to all involved. And the rumored catastrophes (e.g. at a small faculty retreat facility outside the city, on a picturesque farm owned by the university, where a complex of buildings was mysteriously replaced one night by a dense shadowy forest copse, within which the buildings can now be found completely upside down), those are just the inevitable outcome of the university getting involved in nonsense like "applied metaphysics". 

All that said, privately, he did have a chance to meet a creature from another plane recently, through the inquiries of another member of the metaphysics faculty, but really we shouldn't talk about that...

## Relationship to Galen

Ulfgar knows all about Galen - Galen is among those dangerous experimental practitioners who seek to actually experiment with the nature of the planes themselves! Galen is also more famous, more well connected, and more successful than Ulfgar. The latest indignity, of course, is Galen getting the Chalice, when it should have been given to Ulfgar - not to experiment with, of course, but simply to study and document.

## Motives

Will be quite happy to help them get the chalice from Galen and can offer plenty of advice, although will be a little biased. But in part motivated by the academic coup of getting the chalice to study, and will be disappointed (although understanding) if they want to take it.

Easiest way to get him fully on their side is offer some alternative way to build his academic reputation.
