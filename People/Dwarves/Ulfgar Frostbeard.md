A dwarven scholar and theoretical metaphysicist, on the Faculty of Metaphysics at the [[University of Chardon]].  Author of [[The Lore of the Feywild]]. 

Met [[Seeker]] in December 1747, when [[Seeker]] passed through [[Chardon]] before arriving in [[Karawa]].

%%SECRET
from email to Eric:
Ulfgar Frostbeard. Ulfgar is a dwarven professor in the Faculty of Metaphysics, who lives in a compact, two-story house in the Academic Quarter, near the University. He is an older man, maybe 200 years old. His family and much of his clan fled west from the destruction of the old kingdom of [[Ardith]] (along the southern edge of the mountains; Raven's Hold was part of this kingdom originally) during the Great War, and ended up settling in Chardon when he was an infant. His parents were both of the Traveler caste, and opened a small inn in the Dwarven quarter. Ulfgar was called to be Runemaster. After his uncle and older cousins (presumably) died by mysterious magic in the hobgoblin wars during the Blood Years, vanishing into a maelstrom of chaos, never to return, he took up the study of the planes to try to understand how gates and connections between this world and the other planes form, grow, and vanish. Seeker and Ulfgar thus probably bonded over lost family and strange extraplanar events. Ulfgar would have been the one to direct Seeker towards Karawa, with the idea that Karawa itself somehow had a connection to the Dreamworld, which Ulfgar did and does not know much about - his focus is really on the echo realms, the Feywild and the Shadowfell and how the world of Taelgar can be mirrored and yet distorted in their reflections. His scholarship, though, is entirely academic, consisting of writing treatises on older scholarly works discussing these planes. Beyond his knowledge of the planes, he is on the Faculty of Metaphysics and so has a number of contacts at the University and is connected to the academic gossip mill, should that be useful.
