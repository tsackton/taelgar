Whether he was broken by battle or his mind was corrupted by those evils, he returned a changed man. 

He reluctantly left his retirement to help lead a group of warriors, including the young solider [[Nora Silverspark]], on a rescue mission to find refugees in [[Ardith]], bringing the [[Chalice of the Runepriest]] to aid in the question. 

The rescue was somewhat successful, but on the return journey across [[Dunmar]], the [[Dwarves]] encountered the fire giant [[Odim Mavdyrson]], the son of [[Mavdyr]], who had fought and been defeated by an army of [[Dwarves]] and humans outside [[Tokra]] in the [[Fire War]]. Odim sought revenge for his father's defeat, and drove the [[Dwarves]] to take refuge in fortifications constructed  [[Stoneborn Statue Dungeon|beneath the ancient Stoneborn Warrior statue]] on the plains north of [[Tokra]]. 

During the ensuing battle, [[Hagrim]] broke and turned on [[Nora Silverspark]], claiming she was about to betray him. As dwarf fought dwarf, Odim laughed in victory. As Hagrim died, his betrayal and anger, and possibly the corruption in his mind from the aberrations of the [[Great War]], created the [[Shadowfolds]] domain of [[Morkalan]] around him, trapping the surviving [[Dwarves]] in his nightmare.

## History

Originally Hagrim Firebrand, a skilled warrior and leader who had made a name for himself as a war chief in the Great War, fighting mind flayers and evil aberrations deep under the settlements of Ardith.

He was in the early bands of refugees leaving Ardith, traveling across the plains of Dunmar and making a home in Tharn Todor, where he settled down, unable to forget and move past the horrors of the mind flayers he fought in the war. Secretly, he became bitter and depressed, blaming the gods for his fate, and turning against them.

Called out of retirement, he reluctantly agreed to join an large expedition to Ardith, bearing the Chalice, in hopes of at least a heroic death for himself. This warband made it to the ruins of Ardith, and found several pockets of refugees, while also fighting against the eldritch horrors that remained. Wounded but not defeated, the warband fled south with about 500 dwarves, hoping to lead them to safety in Tharn Todor.

But it was not to be. North of Tokra, they encountered the son of the fire giant Mavdyr, and his troll army, set on revenge. The dwarves holed up in a fortification built around the base of the Statue of the Stoneborn Warrior, where they hoped to outlast, if not defeat, the giant.

For a while, it appears as if the dwarves could hold out, until the young commander of the expedition, Nora Silverspark, broke under pressure. Commanding the exposed flank of the warband, she ordered a retreat, which turned into a rout, and then attempted to flee the battlefield after yelling at Hargrim to take command.

The position was hopeless, and Hargrim snapped, turning on Nora. As they fought on the battlefield, both taken by panic, the fire giant broke through their position and the dwarves were overrun. Nora managed to kill the fire giant in response, but Hargrim slew her and the few dwarves who survived, and then burned the giant and the fort, killing himself in the process.

There is probably an element of "The Broken Solider" here, where Nora and Hargrim perceived different acceptable outcomes and sacrifices

In death, he turned from the gods and fell, brooding in his dark Domain of Morkalan, the Land of the Dishonored Dead.

## Flaws and Corruption

Driven by betrayal, Hargrim constantly assumes his subjects are lying, and continuedly tests their loyalty. This is a grim reflection of the bonds of honor that normally hold Dwarven society together, turned from a reflection of genuine trust and mutual respect to bitter, mocking suspicion.

Now, trapped in his dark Domain, he can feel no positive emotion, just flat, blank, endless despair (cf Despair Event Horizon). He torments those in his domain, out of jealously for their hope, endlessly seeking ways to taunt his subjects with the possibility of escape before crushing their hope.

To control the spirits of Morkalan, he has sacrificed his memories, and know no longer remembers anything before his life in Morkalan, does not even remember he is a dwarf.

## The Recent Past

Since coming to, and becoming trapped in Morkalan, Hagrim has slowly drawn in those who explore the Statue, given it a reputation as a haunted place. This might include relatives or friends of the PCs.

## Hagrim's Defeat

Hagrim's weakness is his memory. The only way for the domain to fall is for Hagrim to remember his failures, and acknowledge his evil. Atonement is, for now, out of reach, but should he acknowledge his past, the waters of the Chalice can send him to the Heart of the Mountain to meet his fate.

This can be voluntary, if he is convinced to accept the consequences of his actions, or involuntary, if he is defeated after regaining his memories, and the waters of the Chalice are poured on his fallen spirit as part of a ritual requiring 1 minute, before he reforms.
