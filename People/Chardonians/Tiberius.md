![[tiberius.png|left|400]]Tiberius is a [[Chardonian Empire|Chardonian]] [[Windcallers|Windcaller]] in the service of [[Mitus Verina Auratan]], the Magistros of [[Chardon]]. He serves aboard the [[Auratan's Pride]], one of the Magistros' naval flagships, and is one of the most powerful Windcallers in active service. 

He is a tall, lean man with shoulder-length silver-white hair and unnatural storm-blue eyes, lacking ordinary visible whites or pupils. He carries a polished oak staff tipped with a small sapphire orb. His eyes flare bright blue when he works magic or reacts strongly, and his spellcasting is marked by blue light, crackling lightning, and precise command of wind.

Tiberius is disciplined, wary, and cautious, though honest and open with those he trusts. Although he is a loyal confidant and friend of [[Mitus Verina Auratan]], he is not a politician, and has little patience for the details of civic bureaucracy. He is, however, forceful in his views on [[Chalyte|chalyte]]. He believes chalyte is dangerous when controlled by profit-seeking refineries, the [[Hetaeri Magica]], or anyone who does not understand how to use it safely. He is especially critical of the [[Chalyte Oligarchs of Chardon]] and repeatedly argues that the [[Windcallers]] should have greater control over chalyte production and use. While this appears to be a genuinely held belief, his critics are quick to note it is also a belief that seems purpose-built to increase the power and influence of the secretive and mysterious Windcallers.

**Inferred Portrait**  
Tiberius reads as a disciplined Chardonian naval mage-officer: tall, lean, silver-haired, storm-eyed, controlled, wary, and security-minded. He is not warm, but he is not cruel or corrupt. His vibe is “competent high-level magical security chief”: cautious, procedural, loyal to the Magistros, and deeply convinced that dangerous power should be handled only by people trained to use it responsibly.

**Physical / Visual Details**

- Tall, lean frame. The raw transcript says “Tall, lean flame,” almost certainly ASR for “frame.”
- Shoulder-length silver-white hair.
- Eyes are unnaturally blue: no visible whites/pupils, “almost sort of storm blue.”
- Carries a polished oak staff with a small sapphire orb.
- About six feet tall, with a “commanding vibe.”
- His eyes flare blue/bright when emotionally or magically engaged, especially around chalyte burns/secrets.
- Mannerism: rubs a hand through his silvery hair when working through a difficult situation.

Key source: Session 125 raw transcript (line 6221)

**Mannerisms / Behavior**

- On first contact, he immediately raises his hand and staff and casts a force cage around the party.
- He puts the staff down after the cage is established, then approaches and talks rather than escalating.
- He is explicitly described as “cautious,” “wary,” and “somewhat curious.”
- He prefers layered precautions: cage first, move the ship away from the city, have the party set aside magic items, then read thoughts.
- When using wind magic, he closes his eyes, raises the staff, blue lightning crackles, and he says: “The wind is always waiting.”
- Once convinced, he becomes more candid and even hospitable, offering berths aboard the ship.

Sources: force cage / cautious read (line 6277), wind magic (line 7373)

**Vibe / Personality**

- Security-first, almost paranoid, but for understandable reasons: he has read Fausto’s notes and is genuinely worried about clones, doppelgangers, and the Crown.
- Not deceptive; Kenzo’s read is that he is genuinely concerned and trying to solve a real problem.
- Nervous throughout the confrontation, but not panicked.
- Proud Chardonian: when asked where he is from, he says he is from “here, the greatest city in the world.”
- Not especially grateful at first, though later clarified as grateful once he understands the party’s role.
- Strongly technocratic about chalyte: not anti-power, but believes many people are incapable of using power responsibly.
- Not a politician, and glad of it. He seems somewhat incurious about civic machinery like the Curia, except where it affects chalyte control.
- Loyal to the Magistros. He may be annoyed by the Magistros’ reluctance to change chalyte rules, but he will not act independently against him.

Sources: clone/crown concern (line 7749), political read (line 333)
