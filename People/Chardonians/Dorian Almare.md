### Story of Dorian, the Merfolk, and the Jade

In DR 1718, Rai's hand was scattered, and one piece landed in the kelp forests of Omi. 

In was found by the merfolk of Omi, and over the next five years they learned how to harness the magic of the jade to channel the dreams of the [[Sentient Ocean]]. Eventually, this let the merfolk to create the Garden of Names, a magical place.

> The Garden of Names
> 	The garden of names is a hidden and sacred area within the kelp forest, where vibrant corals and bioluminescent kelp create an enchanting underwater landscape. Each piece of kelp and coral is inscribed with the name of a lost soul, written in an ancient merfolk script that glows faintly in the dim ocean light. These inscriptions are not merely decorative; they are integral to the magic of the garden.
> The Blooming Names
	 The names of the lost souls cause the kelp and coral to bloom in unique and intricate ways. Each name produces a distinct flower or growth, with colors and patterns that reflect the personality and life of the soul it represents. Some blooms might be luminescent, glowing softly in the dark waters, while others might emit gentle currents or soothing sounds. The flowers are not just beautiful; they are also imbued with the essence of the souls, creating a living memorial that is both visually stunning and deeply meaningful.
> The Whispering Garden
> 	The garden is also known for the whispers that emanate from the inscribed names. When the water is still, or during certain times of the day or phases of the moon, the names can be heard whispering. These whispers are believed to be the voices of the lost souls, sharing their thoughts, memories, and emotions. The whispers can be soft and comforting, or urgent and warning, depending on the mood and needs of the souls.

This is was a hard and difficult place to find, but rumors began to spread, that people lost to the ocean were reborn as merfolk, and you could hear them speak and whisper in the waves of Tamaro Bay. 

In DR 1731, Dorian lost his husband to a shipwreck. He traveled to Tamaro Bay, and found comfort and hope in the songs and whispers of the garden of jade. He became something of a guide, helping those who sought the garden, guiding them through their grief. 

By DR 1736, Dorian was well established on (Tamaro Island), and had managed to become friends with several merfolk. He was fascinated with the Sentient Ocean, and it pleased him that his husband was, he thought, now part of the endless sea. 

In DR 1739, Buruli, a merfolk warlock who had made a bargain with Gazankoa, a powerful creature of the elemental plane of water with a penchance for evil. Falling under Gazankoa's sway, he took control of the jade and the garden of names, corrupting it, and opening a portal to the Elemental Plane of Water. A few merfolk escaped to Dorian, wounded and dying, but were able to report broken fragments of what happened, and bring a fragment of the Sentient Ocean to Dorian, which he consumed. 

Now, Dorian has lost all sense of time, and will mostly speak in riddles and nonsense. 

But, with careful coaxing, can learn from him about the events of DR 1739, the name Gazankoa, a few other details. Especially that it is dangerous now. The garden eats you, that sort of thing. 

Still retains some of the magic of the ocean though.
