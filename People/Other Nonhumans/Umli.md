![[umli-the-exile-portrait.png|right|320]]Umli is tall, imposing stoneborn, with gray skin marked with intricate patterns reminiscent of intertwining metalwork and intense obsidian-like eyes. She is a master metalworker and smith, known for her unparalleled mastery of metallurgy and her knowledge of the elemental plane of fire. 
%%^Date:1732%%
Born in the southern [[Svinjo Mountains]], she was exiled from her Stoneborn community for reasons she keeps private, and has lived in [[Tollen]] since DR 1732. 
%%^End%%
%%^Date:1738%%
Though loosely affiliated with the [[University of Tollen]], she does not teach open lectures. She only takes private students who are the most skilled and dedicated at working with rare metals. 
## Rumors and Information
- There are murmurs of Umli once attempting a creation using a metal sourced from a location she keeps secret. Whether this is related to her exile, none will speculate, for Umli is quick to anger if she learns anyone speculating about the reasons for her exile. 
- Umli is exceedingly private. She never takes visitors in her home/forge, and usually takes her meals alone.
- Once a week, on Tuesdays, she takes interviews for new students in a dwarven tavern near campus called [[The Fire and Stone]], and will also sometimes speak to clients desiring her skills in smithing then as well. 
- Once a week, on Fridays, she tests her student's work at her forge. No one is allowed in, but a crowd gathers outside and she takes each item presented to examine.

## Roleplaying Notes

- Cautious Teacher: Umli's past drives her to ensure her students do not repeat her mistakes. She's meticulous in her teachings and expects the same diligence from her pupils.
- Driven by Redemption: Umli possesses a quiet determination, hoping to one day right the wrongs she perceives she committed.
- Guarded Secrets: Umli goes to great lengths to keep her past and current projects secret, ensuring they remain undiscovered.
- Master Craftsman: Emphasize her proficiency and unparalleled skill in metallurgy, often working on complex and intricate projects that challenge even her abilities.

## Secrets

- In her past, Umli discovered a portal to the elemental plane of fire. Her experiments with it caused environmental disturbances, leading to her current estrangement from many in Stoneborn territory.
- She hopes to eventually correct the disturbances caused by the portal, and this drives her desire to keep its existence a secret.
- Umli believes that by mastering certain metals, she might find a way to stabilize or close the portal permanently.

## Backstory

While Umli was always recognized as an adept master, her insatiable curiosity often led her to explore deeper and more profound secrets of the mountains. During one of her exploration missions within a secluded cave, she stumbled upon a shimmering portal, pulsating with intense heat and flame. As she approached, she realized it was a direct gateway to the elemental plane of fire.

Seeing potential for new forms of mastery and understanding, Umli began to experiment with the portal. She harnessed the intense heat and energy from the plane, integrating it into her crafts and disciplines. This newfound power allowed her to craft items and tools that radiated warmth, which could be invaluable in the colder regions of the Stoneborn territory. She also believed that by studying the elemental plane of fire, she might discover new insights into the Stoneborn's relationship with the mountains and the earth.

However, as she continued her experiments, the portal grew unstable. Heatwaves began to emanate from the cave, causing nearby glaciers to melt at an unprecedented rate, resulting in flash floods in the lowland areas. The Stoneborn, always respecting the balance of nature, saw this as a grave disturbance. The elders believed that such meddling with the elemental planes was beyond their understanding and too dangerous.

Many Stoneborn were against sealing the portal, fearing that the backlash of energy might destroy their mountain homes. The only solution, they believed, was to stop Umli's experiments. Given the grave consequences of her actions, the elders decided that exile was the only appropriate punishment.

Umli, realizing the unintentional havoc she wreaked, accepted the decision of exile, but not before sealing the cave entrance with powerful wards and enchantments. Despite the community's fears, she vowed to find a way to safely harness or close the portal, hoping one day to return to her homeland as its protector, rather than its outcast.
