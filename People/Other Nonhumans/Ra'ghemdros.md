## Overview

**Ra'ghemdros** is an ancient black dragon and the daughter of **Cha'mutte**. Once a trusted lieutenant in her father's campaigns, she now resides on the Circular Island, overseeing the imprisonment of the **Cloudspinner**. Consumed by hatred, greed, and a deep-seated fear of failure, Ra'ghemdros presents a complex figure for adventurers to encounter.

"Who are you who dare to speak to me? I am the daughter of Cha'mutte, I am the Light Stealer and the Final Shadow, the Devourer of Drankor, the Last Empress of Hkar, known as the Eternal Night and Enslaver of the Secret Sunset, her highness Ra'ghemdros."

## Backstory

### Early Life and Allegiance

- **Daughter of Cha'mutte**: Ra'ghemdros one of the spawn of Cha'mutte and grew into power in secret during the early days of the [[Drankorian Era]]. As Cha'mutte's progeny, she quickly became a trusted lieutenant, aiding in his quest to undermine the gods and gain ultimate power.
  
- **Amberglow**: She participated in key battles, including the raid on Amberglow, where she faced the Cloudspinner.

### The Scar and Hatred for Cloudspinner

- **Wounded by Cloudspinner**: During the raid on Amberglow, Ra'ghemdros was grievously wounded by the Cloudspinner, leaving a visible gash along her side. The scar shimmers with reds and oranges, resembling a sunset—a stark contrast to her dark, shadowy appearance.
  
- **An Undying Grudge**: This wound became a symbol of her perceived humiliation, fueling her intense hatred for the Cloudspinner.

### Disillusionment and Isolation

- **Disinterest in Cha'mutte's Goals**: Over time, Ra'ghemdros grew disillusioned with the conflict between Cha'mutte and Apollyon, seeing it as pointless.

- **Seizing Control of the Island**: She remained on the Circular Island when Cha'mutte retreated to Pandar to begin marshalling forces for the Great War, and focused on maintaining control over the Cloudspinner's prison on the Circular Island, focusing her efforts on tormenting her nemesis.

- **Abandonment of Larger Ambitions**: When Cha'mutte was defeated and eventually died, Ra'ghemdros withdrew from broader conflicts, content with her dominion over the island.

## Personality

### Surface Traits

- **Arrogant and Dismissive**: Ra'ghemdros views herself as superior to mortals and lesser creatures, often speaking down to those she deems insignificant.

- **Lazy and Complacent**: Preferring the comfort of her lair, she avoids unnecessary exertion, delegating tasks to her minions.

- **Greedy and Possessive**: She is obsessed with collecting treasures and relics, especially those she believes are out of reach. She spends hours each day obsessing over her hoard, rearranging her treasures. 

### Underlying Traits

- **Hatred Masking Fear**: Her intense loathing of the Cloudspinner conceals a profound fear of her and other powerful beings like Apollyon, who wounded Cha'mutte when at the full might of his power. 

- **Nihilistic Outlook**: She questions the purpose of grand schemes and cruelty, seeing them as ultimately futile.

- **Insecurity and Doubt**: Despite her bravado, she harbors deep insecurities about her own strength and fears failure. 

### Relationships with Minions

- **Detachment**: She is largely indifferent to her dragonet followers, who find her easy to appease through flattery.

- **Uninvolved Leadership**: Ra'ghemdros doesn't micromanage, allowing her minions considerable autonomy as long as they don't bother her.

## Goals and Motivations

1. **Hatred of Cloudspinner**

   - **Primary Goal**: Keep the Cloudspinner imprisoned forever.
   - **Desire to Destroy**: She longs to find a way to annihilate the Cloudspinner completely.
   - **Fear of Retaliation**: Aware that freeing the Cloudspinner could enable her enemy to strike back, she avoids tampering with the prison.

2. **Amassing a Grand Hoard**

   - **Collecting Relics**: Ra'ghemdros seeks to gather all the island's relics and treasures, displaying them in a museum-like hoard.
   - **Trade Temptations**: She is enticed by offers of rare items, especially those from places she cannot or is unwilling to access, like Apollyon's tower.

3. **Avoidance of Failure**

   - **Laziness Rooted in Fear**: Her reluctance to act stems from a fear of failure; she avoids challenges that might expose her weaknesses.
   - **Delegation to Minions**: Prefers sending dragonets and other creatures to do her bidding.

## Tactics and Strategies

### In Combat

- **Underestimation of Foes**: She initially treats adversaries as insignificant, possibly underestimating their capabilities.

- **Willingness to Negotiate**: If the battle turns against her or proves challenging, she may quickly offer a truce or propose an alliance.

- **Use of Lair Advantages**: Utilizes the environmental hazards of her lair to her benefit, such as acidic swamps and corrosive gases.

### Out of Combat

- **Diplomatic Opportunist**: Open to negotiation if it serves her interests, especially if offered something she desires.

- **Intimidation and Flattery**: While she attempts to intimidate, she is also susceptible to flattery, which can be used to manipulate her.

- **Avoidance of Direct Confrontation**: Prefers not to engage with entities she fears, like Apollyon or divine forces.

## Interaction with Players

### First Impressions

- **Intimidating Presence**: Ra'ghemdros is an imposing figure, her very presence exuding power and menace.

- **Arrogant Demeanor**: She often begins interactions with condescension, questioning the worth of those before her.

### Potential Scenarios

- **Seeking the Jade**: The party needs an item in her possession, prompting an encounter.

- **Negotiation**: Players may attempt to trade treasures or information for her assistance or the release of the Cloudspinner.

- **Common Enemies**: An opportunity arises if both the party and Ra'ghemdros see Apollyon as a mutual threat.

## Role-Playing Tips

- **Maintain Superiority**: Always portray Ra'ghemdros as confident in her power, even when hints of insecurity emerge.

- **React to Flattery**: She is susceptible to praise, which can be used to the players' advantage.

- **Avoid Direct Admissions of Fear**: While she fears certain entities, she masks this with disdain or indifference.

- **Leverage Her Greed**: Offers of unique or unattainable treasures can sway her decisions.

- **Show Reluctance for Effort**: She prefers solutions that require minimal exertion on her part.

## Additional Information

### Understanding Her Fears

- **Cloudspinner's Revenge**: She is terrified of the possibility that the Cloudspinner could escape and retaliate.

- **Apollyon's Return**: The idea of facing Apollyon or his forces causes her significant anxiety.

### Potential Weaknesses

- **Inaction Due to Fear**: Her reluctance to act can be exploited, creating opportunities for the party to maneuver.

- **Overconfidence in Her Lair**: She believes her lair is impregnable, which may lead to complacency.

### Possible Outcomes

- **Diplomatic Resolution**: If handled carefully, the party could negotiate with Ra'ghemdros, gaining what they need without conflict.

- **Forced Confrontation**: Should negotiations fail, a strategic battle utilizing her lair's environment may ensue.

- **Aid Against Greater Threats**: She might provide assistance or information if convinced it serves her interests in eliminating a mutual enemy.

### Motivation Details

(1) Hatred of Cloudspinner. She finds cruelty for its own sake to be kind of exhausting, why bother? But she has an undying hatred for Cloudspinner and will never let her go. Her greatest desire is to find a way to destroy Cloudspinner once and for all, but the way the prison works is it completely invulnerable in both directions: to open the prison to free her would also risk the Cloudspinner being able to retaliate. Despite her attitude, underneath her hatred of the Cloudspinner is a deep, deep fear, and so she does not push and is content to see the Cloudspinner trapped. 

(2) Greed. She is strongly motivated by a desire to gather all the relics of the island, and even what she can find on the mainland, into a huge monument to herself. She occasionally sends dragonet missions to scour the jungle, but doesn't dare approach Drankor itself for fear of Apollyon (underneath a lot of her motivations are hidden fears that she is not as strong as she thinks, that Cloudspinner beat her, Apollyon could beat her). For the same reason, she is reluctant to push into Apollyon's tower. And she has not been able to defeat the divine magic protecting the statues and the various pilgrim locations. She would be very, very tempted by a trade, treasure for treasure, if the party gave her something she couldn't otherwise get, e.g. from Apollyon's tower. 

(3) Lazy. She is lazy, largely because she is reluctant to do anything too hard for fear that she will fail. So she sends her minions out and rarely leaves her lair, where she can enjoy the sight of her hoard, which is arranged basically like a museum. But despite this, she figures mere mortals cannot touch her, and will be arrogant when faced with "weaklings". But, in combat, she will relatively quickly offer a truce - either "you are no match for my might, but you are no good to me dead" or "you are indeed mighty, there is no need for this conflict, we can help each other."
