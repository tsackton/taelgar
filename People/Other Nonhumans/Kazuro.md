## Kazuro

Kazuro is a recluse. He lives in a simple stone structure carved out of the limestone, in a cave. It is dark most of the time, although he seems to have cultivated some kind of bioluminescence that lights everything with a pale moonlight blue glow. The cave has a dock built and steps carved that lead up to a porch/deck, and Kazuro's house. It is too small for the large ship to enter. 

Kazuro himself is a stoneborn, skin like bluish-gray limestone, wearing blue robes, with a distant look in his eyes. When they enter, he is likely sitting still, like a statue, water up to his chest. 

Will stand, grumbling, "Who are you to disturb my meditation?" But will look a bit taken aback when he sees dwarves, and elves. 

### Backstory

Kazuro is about 200 years old. He was born in the years after the Great War, in the central mountains of [[Apporia]], in a land he calls "Enderra." He was young when war broke out—barely an adult. Like everyone else, he fought and saw too much death. He emerged from the war broken. He loved the mountains of his home, but could no longer stay without seeing the blood and death.
#### The War of the Dark Rift

The [[War of the Dark Rift]] began deep within the central mountains of Enderra. The conflict was sparked by what seemed to be an extraplanar incursion from the Shadowfell. Dwarven miners had inadvertently broken ancient seals, releasing dark energies that corrupted them. The malevolent force behind this corruption became known as the [[Umbral Covenant]], though the true nature of this group and its connection to the Void Mind was never fully understood by those who fought it.

To the people of Enderra, the [[Umbral Covenant]] appeared as a sinister alliance of corrupted dwarves and other dark beings. The Covenant exploited a weak point between the material world and the Shadowfell, spreading its influence and aiming to unravel the fabric of reality. The dwarves' attempts to contain this threat ultimately failed, leading to widespread chaos and destruction.

#### Kazuro's Journey

When Chardon defeated the Shadow Armada in 1597, marking the end of the War of Shadows, Kazuro left his war-torn home. For 20 years, he wandered aimlessly, learning from his travels. He spent time in Chardon, traveled up the coast, spent time in the Tawir Forest and Mawar, and lived among the salt lizardfolk. He even ventured to the far north, where few people live and there is ice even in summer.

During his travels, Kazuro began to fall in love with the ocean. Every trek inland was met with a yearning to be back at sea, to understand the changeable moods of the great endless waters, what his people called the Encircling Sea. He returned to the Apporian Peninsula, deciding he could not learn what he needed while constantly traveling, and chose to settle by the sea.

For over 100 years, Kazuro made his home by the ocean, learning its moods and how to hear its words. 

#### The Present

He grows old, now, and weary, and recently has wished for an apprentice, to pass on his knowledge before he becomes one with the stone, his body slowly weathered and turned to sand, to drift endlessly on the currents of the Encircling Sea, scattered across the world. But none who have come are worthy, or patient enough. Everyone wishes the secrets of the ocean could be told, simply, that her secrets would be revealed instantly and not with long, hard work. 

### Getting Help

Kazuro starts off pretty skeptical of anyone just asking for his help. Not hostile exactly, but cumudgeonly and doesn't want to just help people. Mostly concerned that to do things like construct potions that transform lungs into gills, or vice versa, takes from the ocean, and just giving it away is disrespectful. 

Initial: **DC 30 to help**, and requires an offer in exchange. this is basically impossible without assuaging his concerns. 

Insight can reveal something about his concerns, history, fascination with dwarves:
- deeply in tune with the ocean, will only help those he believes are seeking to help soothe the ocean, aid it; will talk about the ocean almost like a person
- separately, deeply misses his dwarven friends from his youth, so might be swayed by nostalgia
- offended by lies, deceit, hastiness

Can shift his disposition more friendly in a variety of ways. DC 15-30+ depending on approach and goal. 

Basically can shift the DC to help lower, (as low as ~10-15 which should be nearly impossible to fail) by:
- Convincing him of their worth as people and the nobility of their goal, perhaps first by drawing out his backstory and seeing the parallels to their own
- Convincing him that their goals are to help save the ocean

Remember: Source of Conflict (does not want to part with valuable potions); Disposition (unfriendly to start); Patience (probably pretty high); Motivation (the ocean, past trauma, honor, goodness); Mood (wing it). 

Most actions should either be about shifting disposition, understanding conflict, or ultimately about negotiation/convincing. 

### The Potions

Kazuro has on hand 6 potions of transformation, plus 6 doses of antidote. The potions of transformation are literally that, transformations. While under the effects, you gain gills but lose lungs. You can now breath water, but not air. Have no effect on swim speed, and last until the antidote is taken or dispelled. 

Can make more, but will take a day. Can make up to 4 doses in a day (a dose including transformation + antidote, which are made as part of the same process). 

Does not want money. If ultimately cannot be convinced, will insist that if they wish to receive his aid, they must demonstrate their commitment to the ocean with deeds. 

- Been wanting to construct a monument / temple / meditation retreat on top of the island, overlooking the ocean. But it is cursed. When the Chardonian navy drove out the (Shadow Armanda), they pulled down a watchtower that had stood here. But the land is now cursed by the evil fiends, and I cannot build until it is purified.
