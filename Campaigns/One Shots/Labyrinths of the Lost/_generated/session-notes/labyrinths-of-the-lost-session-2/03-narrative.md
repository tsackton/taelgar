# Narrative

<!-- SLOT: narrative.short -->
After an overnight rest, the party decides to search for a way into the manor via the chapel.

At dawn, they cross the overgrown grounds to the gardens, while the Dilion's magical pup companion distracts the guards.

From the garden, they find the chapel and a trapdoor into the crypt below. Hearing pursuit, they prepare a trap on the crypt stairs.

When the hobgoblins come down, the trap kills a smokebinder and a second guard.

With the crypt secure, they follow a secret tunnel into a hidden Perdoli archive, with a secret exit to the manor.

After a short rest in the hidden archive, the party opens a secret door into a ruined library, kills the hobgoblins alerted by their noise, and finds a chained creature in the courtyard -- a basilisk.

Avoiding the courtyard, they find a ghost of a pirate in a nearby room, who warns them and points out the trapdoor to [[Grimstone's Lair|Grimstone's lair]].

<!-- SLOT: narrative.intermediate -->
After deciding not to travel at night, the party sleeps in the abandoned smithy prison room and prepares for the next push before dawn. Folcan's information leaves the chapel looking like the best way into the manor.

At dawn, they move west from the smithy through overgrown vineyards toward the formal garden and chapel. When [[Loria Underbough|Loria]] spots a shadowy hobgoblin patrol angling toward them, [[Dilion]] sends his magical dog companion east to bark and holler, drawing the patrol and nearby goblins away long enough for the party to reach the garden path.

That path carries them past a fountain and a petrified medusa before they reach the ruined chapel. A square of flowers gives away the hidden trapdoor into the family crypt. When noise above suggests a pursuer has followed the steampup's trail, the party closes the trapdoor, greases the stair, sets a snare, and waits below.

The prepared stair trap works twice. First a wounded hobgoblin smokebinder slips, falls into the snare, and is killed in the crypt; then a shouted lure draws down a second hobgoblin, whose crossbow and alchemist's fire are not enough to keep it alive.

With quiet restored, they loot the second hobgoblin and search the crypt more carefully:[[Loria Underbough|Loria]] finds a Cure Wounds scroll and potion of watchful rest in a sliding niche. While searching, [[Dilion]] discovers a secret tunnel behind a loose torch bracket, and the party follows it into a sealed archive of Perdoli records. In the archive, they find a hidden exit to the manor, but pause for a rest before continuing.

After the rest, the party opens the secret archive door into a small ruined library. [[Loria Underbough|Loria]] accidentally kicks loose stone, alerting hobgoblin guards in the next room, but the party defeats them quickly enough to avoid a wider alarm. Continuing carefully, they hear hobgoblin recruits drilling and stirges buzzing without being discovered, then investigate the central courtyard and confirm that the chained reptile there is a basilisk when Gaudin's Mage Hand experiment nearly turns him to stone.

Avoiding the courtyard, they search the nearby private rooms, finding reveal fresh statues and old broken mirrors. A mourning sailor ghost refuses to talk, until further exploration uncovers a study with a silver locket, smuggler's map, and love letters, and [[Gaudin]] disguises himself in Petrona's image to calm the ghost. The ghost then names [[Grimstone]] as the white-faced man below the manor and points to the trapdoor into his old hideout as the way to [[Grimstone's Lair|Grimstone's lair]].

<!-- SLOT: narrative.long -->
The party spends the night back in the abandoned smithy prison room rather than crossing the manor grounds in the dark. They share rations and apple brandy, clean the food and drink, sleep, and use the pre-dawn hours to prepare. With Folcan's warnings still shaping the plan, they weigh the barracks, kitchen, and chapel routes before settling on the chapel as the likeliest way to reach the tunnels without walking into the main guard force.

At dawn, the party leaves the smithy side of the grounds and sneaks west through old vineyards and tangled agricultural plots. [[Loria Underbough|Loria]] catches sight of a shadowy hobgoblin patrol on the wall, and [[Gaudin]] keeps watching the pearl ring for signs of nearby goblins and hobgoblins. [[Dilion]] sends his magical dog companion east to bark and holler; the construct pulls attention away from the party even as they hear it being struck. That distraction gives them just enough room to cut through the hedge and reach the formal garden approach to the manor.

From the formal garden, the party passes a fountain and a stone medusa frozen while staring at her own reflection, then reaches the ruined chapel. The chapel still feels peaceful despite its open roof and broken walls; a search for a way in turns up a trapdoor hidden beneath a square of flowers. Below, the family crypt leads to a secondary altar room with tarnished silver, an icon of [[The Mother]], old coins, and a yellow topaz. When tapping and digging above warn that a pursuer has found the disturbed chapel floor, they cover the entrance, grease the stair, set a snare at the bottom, and wait in the dark.

The first hobgoblin through the trapdoor is the same shadow-bound scout that chased the [[Steampup|steampup]], and the party's setup catches it immediately. It slips on the greased stairs, falls into the snare, and is hammered by [[Loria Underbough|Loria]], [[Acescale]], [[Gaudin]], and the [[Steampup|steampup]] until the steel defender throws it down for good. [[Dilion]] recognizes it as a smokebinder, its shadow cloak more ritual effect than loot, and the party turns the same trap into a lure for the next patrol member. The second hobgoblin also comes down into the stairs, wounds [[Gaudin]] with a crossbow and alchemist's fire, and dies before the alarm can spread.

With the crypt quiet, the party collects the second hobgoblin's crossbow, bolts, coins, and alchemist's fire before searching the old rooms again. [[Loria Underbough|Loria]] finds a sliding niche behind the stairs holding a scroll of Cure Wounds and a potion of watchful rest, while [[Dilion]] discovers that a loose torch bracket opens a secret passage from the altar room. The tunnel runs under the manor to a ceiling trapdoor. The room above is an old hidden archive: they find [[Perdoli Family|Perdoli family]] papers, portraits, and shipping records revealing the ancient history of the manor. They also find a fake-book button in the middle bookcase, pointing to the secret exit, but pause for a short rest before pressing farther.

After the short rest, the party finds the mechanism that opens the archive's secret door, emerging into a small, destroyed library inside the main manor. Their first attempt at moving quietly fails when [[Loria Underbough|Loria]] kicks loose stone, and the noise brings hobgoblin guards charging in from the next room. The party kills them quickly and keeps the alarm from spreading, then resumes a much more cautious exploration. They hear hobgoblin recruits drilling elsewhere and the buzzing of stirges nearby, but avoid both groups. In the central courtyard, they find a chained reptile hidden behind a low wall. [[Gaudin]] tests the creature with Mage Hand, glimpses its face, and begins petrifying until the others hack the forming stone away from him. [[Acescale]] considers polishing the crypt's silver plate as a mirror, then realizes the creature is a basilisk, not a medusa, and that its gut enzyme could instead become a depetrification salve if they kill it.

Avoiding the courtyard, the search shifts from the basilisk to the nearby private chambers, where the party finds a great hall full of lifelike statues, a study with broken mirror glass and older headless statues, and a bedroom haunted by a sailor ghost. Deciding to explore a little, they search the other rooms, finding a sheaf of papers (apparently pirate maps from a generation ago), and a silver locket with a faded painting, inscribed to "[[Jacopo Marcelli|J.M.]], with love, P. Keep safe and come back." There is also a packet of love letters, apparently from whoever "P." is, suggesting that [[Jacopo Marcelli|J. M.]] and P. were perhaps both pirates. 

Feeling this must be a clue, the party goes to talk to the ghost again. Initially, this does not go well, and he is angry with [[Gaudin]]. However, in a moment of panic or inspiration, [[Gaudin]] uses disguise self to make himself look like P, the woman in the locket, and claims that the white-faced man, presumably [[Grimstone]], is trying to hurt her. This finally breaks through to the ghost, who is clearly [[Jacopo Marcelli|J. M.]], who died without knowing the fate of [[Petrona]] (P.), and was promised news and rest by [[Grimstone]]. Now, convinced [[Grimstone]] has been lying, the pirate ghost says he will guard no more, and reveals that [[Grimstone's Lair|Grimstone's lair]] is below this bedroom, in the old pirate hideout under the manor. His last words as he fades away are that  that [[Grimstone]] is crazy, constantly ranting about the immortality of stone.
