# The Dunmari Map of Pandemonium Ruins
>[!info]+ Information  
> (uncommon [[Dunmar|Dunmari]] magical map)  
> `$=dv.view("_scripts/view/get_Affiliations")`

A map of the ruins of the Pandemonium fort, north of [[Tokra]] created by the Dunmari during [[Exile of Fraternity of the Empty Moon]]. Contains notes on the secret paths to avoid the effects of [[Pandemonium]] while approaching.

Found in the [[Tokra]] [[Archives]] by [[Govir]], which led to his kidnapping and infection with lycanthropy. Recovered by the [[Dunmar Fellowship]], and kept for safekeeping.

%%SECRET
It shows the fort and the gash, and it outlines a curving path that one can walk that supposedly avoids the madness that emanates from the gash.

In invisible letters, it adds: "The key, is that one cannot approach the place while seeing the place. Only one blind, whether by cloth or a dark night with no moon, can follow the path outlaid above. But be careful, for should you stray from the path in your blindness the madness will take you."

"The safe passage is shaped so that should you pour enough water from the point marked, it will find a channel. Follow the water, for in so doing you can trace the path in the dark by the feel of the water."

## Information

The party found this information about the map in the Tokra Archives:

One item was not taken: the scroll the head scribe was working on, which is at his work desk. This is a faded, crumbling sheaf of letters originally written on poor paper that is decaying and needs to be copied over. They are copies of letters from a Commander Ila to Samraat Nayan Yarad. From context, Ila seems to have been the local garrison commander responsible for the defense of Tokra, mostly from the north, in the aftermath of the Second Hobgoblin War. There are about 50 letters, from the 21st-24th years of the reign of Samraat Nayan Yarad, in the 67th-69th years of the Nayan Dynasty. Many are already difficult to read, and most seem to concern raids against hobgoblins of the Blackened Claw tribe, seeking to exterminate even the few survivors of the 2nd Hobgoblin War. Depending on how long they spend studying and/or investigation check, will note that a few seem of interest and don't concern hobgoblins. One describes the discovery of a strange cult that had set up a base of operations in an old Dunmari fort about 10 miles north of the city. The place was hard to find -- those who approached seemed to grow confused, sometimes attacking their companions in madness. There was one approach that seemed safe, which I have carefully mapped and given to my troops (in handwritten annotations here, cf. bundle 24).

NOTE: events occurred in DE 1625, known as the Year of Joyful Rains in halfling reckoning
