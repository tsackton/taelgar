# The Chardon Chalyte Riots
>[!info]+ Information  
> `$=dv.view("_scripts/view/get_PageDatedValue")`  
>> `$=dv.view("_scripts/view/get_Whereabouts")`

The Chardon Chalyte Riots were a wave of sabotage, arson, street fighting, and general unrest in [[Chardon]] after the [[Session 116 (DuFr)|fall of Fausto]] and the collapse of the [[The Cleansed|Cleansed]] hold over the Chardonian chalyte trade. 

The riots began after [[Session 117 (DuFr)|chalyte bureaucrats and trade officials publicly exposed their own corruption]] and the structure of the chalyte monopoly, triggering massive riots and the rapid formation of a group calling itself the [[Eightfold Flame]]. The [[Eightfold Flame]], started by ex-bureaucrats, grew rapidly among chalyte workers and North Bank residents, quickly engufling much of the [[North Bank]], especially the [[Chalyte Levels]] and [[Ragwater Basin]]. 

## Background

Before the riots, the Chardonian chalyte economy was dominated by a small number of oligarchic families: the [[Auratan Family]], [[Patelios Family]], [[Caloren Family]], and [[Veldari Family]] controlled between them the entire chalyte supply, from mines to transport to refined product. Through corruption, bribery, and magical control via the [[Crown of Purity]], [[Fausto]] and [[The Cleansed]] were able to infiltrate and control the [[Hetaeri Magica]], turning it into the near-sole purchaser of refined chalyte, and a vehicle to fuel bribes back to the oligarch families and the [[Chamber of Stamps]]. 

On [[Session 116 (DuFr)|May 24th, 1749]], the [[Dunmar Fellowship]] defeated [[Fausto]] in [[Drankor]]. [[Kenzo]] claimed the Crown of Purity, quickly realized the extent of [[Fausto]]'s control, and ordered controlled Chardonian officials tied to the chalyte trade to dismantle the old system, expose corruption, and focus on reparations and social restructuring. [[Riswynn]], who later held the Crown, maintained the pressure on the same officials, though with a stronger and largely subconcious religious undertone. 

The result was not a managed reform process. Officials and factors who had been manipulated by Fausto, then by Kenzo, then by Riswynn, began exposing corruption via public pronouncements from the streets and squares of the city. Within days, riots were spreading across the [[North Bank]] as the city erupted in flames. 
## Course of the Riots

By [[Session 121 (DuFr)|early June]], the unrest in Chardon became a major crisis. With much of the [[Chardonian Legion]] away from the capital in [[Dunmar]] to prosecute the [[Chardon-Dunmar War]], and the [[Mitus Verina Auratan|Magistros]] with the fleet in [[Darba]] negotiating [[Nayan Karnas|Karnas]]'s surrender, chaos reigned in Chardon. 

The Eightfold Flame briefly controlled large sections of the North Bank. Fires burned across the refineries of the Ragwater Basin, and street fighting and vigilante justice spread through the chalyte districts. Martial law was declared soon after; [[Session 124 (DuFr)|legions blockaded]] the [[River Bridge (Chardon)|River Bridge]], cutting off movement between the North Bank and the rest of Chardon. 

The riot's decisive turn came in mid-June, when many [[Eightfold Flame]] leaders vanished for reasons never publicly explained. Later accounts often connected the timing to [[Session 123 (DuFr)|Riswynn's claiming and rededication of the Crown of Purity]] after the rescue of [[Cloudspinner]], when the Crown's old bonds of domination were broken. Whatever the cause, the disappearances took the wind out of the protests: by [[Session 125 (DuFr)|the end of June]], the North Bank remained scorched and hungry, but the uprising had lost much of its coordination.

## Aftermath

The riots forced the chalyte question into the open, and upended the chalyte trade. By [[Session 126 (DuFr)|late June]], incoming shipments had halted because merchants would not send raw ore into a city in chaos, and existing reserves could not sustain the system indefinitely. As Chardon still depended on chalyte for public works, military infrastructure, and urban life, it seemed a crisis was at hand that could leave thousands starving and suffering when chalyte magic failed around the city. 

The settlement came through the actions of the [[Dunmar Fellowship]] to secure a new [[Session 129 (DuFr)|chalyte source in the Chataan mountains]]. With an independent chalyte supply guaranteed, the Magistros moved against the old families, leading to the creation of the [[Chalyte Refining Corporation]] and the enactment of [[Chardonian Chalyte Trade Reforms|many trade reforms]].

## Conflicts and Unresolved Details

- The exact end date of the riots is not fixed. Violent unrest was active by DR 1749-05-27 and still shaped the city on DR 1749-06-27. This note uses DR 1749-07-09 as a rough endpoint because the Magistros's public restructuring of the chalyte order began then, but Chardon did not fully stabilize until early August.
- The Eightfold Flame is both a genuine worker movement and a movement shaped by the aftereffects of Crown compulsion. The balance between spontaneous anger, ideological organizing, and magical disruption is not resolved.
- The old chalyte officials were successively influenced by Fausto, Kenzo, and Riswynn through the Crown of Purity. The moral and political responsibility of those officials is not resolved in the campaign notes.
