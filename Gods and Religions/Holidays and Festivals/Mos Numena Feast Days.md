# Mos Numena Feast Days

Those who follow the way of [[Mos Numena]] celebrate many holidays and feast days. Some, such as [[Pyravela]], are ancient and widely celebrated; many others are local thanksgivings for miracles of the divine.

## Widely Observed 

- [[Viatela]] (Dec 11): a festival of homecoming, dedicated to [[The Wanderer]].
- [[Pyravela]] (Dec 29–31): an ancient year-end festival associated with [[The Wyrdling]].
- [[Quintarum]] (Jan 22): a feast day of [[The Wanderer]], often described as a leave-taking counterpart to Viatela.

## Local Miracles

Many communities keep additional feast days tied to local stories of divine intervention. Examples include:

- [[Festival of the Bridge]] (Nov 3) in [[Cleenseau]].
- [[Miracle of the Guided Wanderers]] (Sep 11) in [[Asineau]].
- [[Festival of the Miracle of the Mist]] (Apr 18) in [[Abenfyrd]].
- [[Miracle of the Hopeful Heat]] (Jan 7) in [[Asineau]].

The basic philosophy here is that in Sembara there are really several different cycles and sources of holidays:
* The ancient Drankorian ritual calendar (of which High Sun and Pyravela are meant to recall an even older Hkar magical calendar)
* The kestavo tradition, although most of the kestavo holidays are local and the only two significant universal ones were the spring and late fall bookends to winter.
* The kestavo tradition further contributes to the idea of local miracle remembrances, which, although miracle remembrances are a feature (most likely) of all Mos Numena traditions, the hyper-local nature is likely unique to Sembara
* Finally, there are a couple of harvest festivals that have purely agricultural roots and although likely have a religious flavor, have a more deeply local origin

A few open questions and ideas:
* The High Sun festival may have been repurposed during the late Drankorian period by Apolloyon. It is possible that the Sembaran and Chardonian versions are very different
* There is a small chance we should consider adding one more "core" Drankorian holiday
* The gods column is not final 
* It is very unclear how much of this applies elsewhere -- Addermarch, Tyrwingha, Zimka, Tollen, Cymea, Refounded Alliance of Auzbeg, Chardon as minimal examples -- some of these seem very unlikely to apply to i.e. Addermarch or Tollen for various reasons
