# Mawaran Religion

Mawaran religion is a regional form of [[Mos Numena]] practiced in the [[Mawar Confederacy]]. The [[Mos Numena|Eight Divines]] remain the ultimate source of miracles from the [[Divine Presence]], but Mawaran prayer commonly passes through saintly ancestors, remembered heroes, and local intercessors who bring the needs of the living before the Divines.

This ancestor-centered form of the Way reflects older [[Northern Folk Religions|northern intercessory traditions]], salt [[Lizardfolk|lizardfolk]] ritual practice, and the culture of the sea. [[Guzo the Mariner]], protector of ocean voyages and those lost at sea, is the clearest named Mawaran saint; the peninsula's lizardfolk [[Gatza|gatza]] rites also preserve a living tradition of controlled communion with ancestral spirits, while uncontrolled gatza use is regarded as dangerous and spiritually unreliable.

### Canonical synthesis to protect

- Mawaran religion is Mos Numena, not a separate pantheon.
- The Eight Divines remain the final divine authorities and the ultimate source of miracles in the current canonical interpretation.
- Mawaran saints, ancestors, and heroes are intercessors, not replacements for the Divines.
- Ancestor veneration is a major Mawaran religious feature.
- Guzo is the only fully developed named Mawaran saint.
- Lizardfolk gatza rites are definitively tied to communion with ancestral spirits and the dead.
- Uncontrolled gatza use is spiritually suspect: the voices may be real dead, delusions, or malevolent powers.
- Mawaran religion should feel sea-facing, coastal, and memorial, but the exact formal theology of the sea is not yet fixed.

### Conflicts and inconsistencies in sources

- High-god language is inconsistent. Older Mawar synthesis says ancestor spirits carried prayers to Arhat and Malik; [[Northern Folk Religions]] frames northern intercession as prayers to a single [[Divine Presence]]; current [[Mawar Confederacy]] canon routes intercession to the Eight Divines as the source of miracles.
- Chardonian Mos Numena rejects intercession in the [[Mos Numena]] brainstorming, while Mawaran practice canonically depends on intercessors. The cleanest current solution is to treat Mawar as Mos Numena with a distinct northern intercessory structure, not as Chardonian practice.
- The [[Mos Numena]] note also suggests places like Mawar may have temples hierarchically connected to Chardonian temples. That sits uneasily beside the local ancestor-heavy model and needs a decision about institutional control.
- [[Religious History of Taelgar]] says Mawar's variant mostly comes from tanshi-like ideas, while the top-level Mawar note also emphasizes lizardfolk ritual practice and the culture of the sea. These can coexist, but the relative importance of each source is unsettled.
- [[Human Cultures - Discussions]] preserves older ideas that Mawar may be part of a wider Great Bay religious culture, but it also says Mawar may be unique and should perhaps be left historically blank.
- Mawar origin material is unsettled. Current public text preserves a tradition of ancient ancestry from [[Hkar]], while discussion notes explicitly question whether a northerner origin is a good fit.
- [[Traditions around Death]] and [[Email - Wazir Backstory II]] strongly support Mawaran grave tokens and ancestor-guided funerals, but the former is explicitly marked not fully canonical and the latter is email/worldbuilding discussion. Do not make this public canon without confirmation.
- [[Guzo the Mariner]] presents Guzo as a saint and heroic ancestor within Mos Numena, while some campaign table language and comparison notes can sound like deification or replacement worship. Current continuity should keep Guzo as an intercessor, not a substitute Divine.
- The phrase "ancestor of voyages and the western horizon" may be title, mythic role, or literal ancestor-language. It should not be over-literalized until decided.
- Gatza both enables contact with spirits of the dead and is explicitly unreliable when abused. Any public claim that a given gatza voice is truly a dead spirit should be made carefully.
- [[Yerkir-khor]] and [[Khathayi|Khathayi the Flametongue]] imply old elemental cults or powers on the peninsula, but they are not yet integrated with Mawaran Mos Numena. Treat them as buried religious strata, not current Mawaran orthodoxy.
- [[Gazankoa]] and the [[Sentient Ocean]] material adds a water-plane conflict around Mawar that is not the same thing as Mawaran religion. It is relevant to heresy, danger, and campaign mysteries, but should not be folded into everyday religion without a deliberate choice.
- Ocean Watcher material shows real mystical practice around the sea, but no source yet defines the Ocean Watchers as clergy or a religious order.

### Working constraints for future pages

- Keep the top-level public framing simple: Mawar follows Mos Numena through saintly ancestor intercession.
- Do not describe Mawarans as worshipping Guzo or ancestors instead of the Eight unless the text is explicitly about a heterodox practice.
- Do not make the funerary-token material public canon until the noncanonical warnings are resolved.
- Do not collapse lizardfolk religion into human Mawaran religion. Shared influence is canon; identity is not.
- Use elemental cult material as ancient, buried, or campaign-facing unless a future history page canonizes it.
- Preserve uncertainty around the Sentient Ocean. Mawarans have lore, songs, and visions connected to it, but its place in formal religion is unclear.
- Treat Chardon/Mawar religious friction as a likely and useful theme, especially around saints, miracles, funerals, and institutional authority.
