# Mawaran Religion

%% does not yet incorporate notes about funerary traditions, but there is some brainstorming here %%

The Mawar Confederacy and related cultural areas practice a regional form of [[Mos Numena]], oriented around saintly intercessors. While the [[Mos Numena|Eight Divines]] remain the ultimate source of miracles from the [[Divine Presence]], Mawaran prayer commonly passes through saintly ancestors and heroes who bring the needs of the living before the Divines. This practice reflects a fusion of Mos Numena tradition with [[Northern Folk Religions|northern intercessory folk religions]], as well as the influence of salt [[Lizardfolk|lizardfolk]] ritual practice involving [[Gatza]]. 

[[Guzo the Mariner]] is an example of a Mawaran saint.

### Canonical synthesis to protect

- Mawaran religion is Mos Numena, not a separate pantheon.
- The Eight Divines remain the final divine authorities and the ultimate source of miracles in the current canonical interpretation.
- Mawaran saints, ancestors, and heroes are intercessors, not replacements for the Divines.
- Ancestor veneration is a major Mawaran religious feature, though presumably mostly or exclusively in the form of specific ancestor saints or demi-gods, not generic "ancestors".
- Guzo is the only fully developed named Mawaran saint.
- Lizardfolk gatza rites are definitively tied to communion with ancestral spirits and the dead, but not directly Mawaran; though gazta likely influenced Mawaran religious ideas at some point. 
- Uncontrolled gatza use is spiritually suspect: the voices may be real dead, delusions, or malevolent powers.
- Mawaran religion should feel sea-facing, coastal, and memorial, but the exact formal theology of the sea is not yet fixed, or the degree to which the sea has an explicit role in Mawaran theology/religion. 
- The connection between Mawaran religion and the Sentient Ocean is explicitly left undefined for now.
